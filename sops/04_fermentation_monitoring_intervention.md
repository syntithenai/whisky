# SOP-04: Fermentation Monitoring and Intervention

## 1. Purpose
Define routine monitoring and intervention rules to keep fermentation on target and prevent quality loss.

## 2. Scope
Applies from pitching through fermentation completion and transfer for distillation.

## 3. Roles
- Fermentation Operator: routine checks, log updates, escalation.
- Quality or Lab: analytical and microbiological confirmation.
- Production Lead: intervention approval and disposition decisions.

## 4. Procedure
1. Monitoring Frequency
- At minimum, check each active fermenter at defined intervals (for example every 4 to 8 hours early phase, then daily once stable).

2. Routine Checks
- Temperature versus profile target.
- Specific gravity or extract trend.
- pH trend.
- Sensory screen (aroma, foam behavior, visual contamination signs).

3. Trigger Limits (Examples)
- Temperature outside tolerance band.
- Gravity drop slower or faster than expected trend.
- pH unexpected drift.
- Delayed start or stalled fermentation.

4. Approved Interventions
- Temperature correction through cooling or warming.
- Controlled nutrient addition (if allowed by process design).
- Agitation or circulation where validated.
- Escalation sampling for contamination checks.

5. Escalation and Hold
- If trigger persists after first intervention, place batch on hold for disposition review.
- Document risk to final spirit quality and decide: continue with conditions, blend strategy, downgrade, or discard.

6. Completion and Handover
- Confirm endpoint criteria (final gravity window, sensory acceptability, no critical contamination findings).
- Release wash for distillation.

## 5. Small vs Large Operation Notes
- Small distillery: manual readings and trend charts can be effective if performed on schedule with clear escalation rules.
- Larger operation: automated data capture, alarm limits, historian trends, and formal shift handover controls.

## 6. Records
- Fermentation monitoring sheet.
- Intervention log.
- Lab results and contamination checks.
- Batch disposition decision.
