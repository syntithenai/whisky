# SOP-10: Non-Conformance Management

## 1. Purpose
Provide a consistent process to identify, contain, assess, and resolve product or process non-conformances.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all deviations from approved specifications, procedures, legal requirements, or quality standards.

## 3. Roles
- Any Employee: report suspected non-conformance immediately.
- Quality: own investigation and disposition workflow.
- Process Owner: implement corrective and preventive actions.
- Management: review significant events and closure effectiveness.

## 4. Procedure
1. Identification and Containment
- Stop use or shipment of affected material/product.
- Segregate and label as Hold.
- Record affected lots, quantities, and locations.

2. Risk Assessment
- Evaluate safety, legal, customer, and brand risk.
- Determine urgency and escalation level.

3. Investigation
- Collect facts, records, and timeline.
- Perform root cause analysis (for example 5 Whys or fishbone).

4. Disposition
- Options: rework, regrade, release with concession, return, or destruction.
- Obtain required approvals before disposition execution.

5. CAPA
- Define corrective actions (immediate fix) and preventive actions (systemic control).
- Assign owner and due dates.
- Verify effectiveness after implementation.

## 5. Small vs Large Operation Notes
- Small distillery: one person may manage quality records, but management review and independent verification should be retained.
- Larger operation: formal NC workflows in QMS software with escalation tiers and CAPA dashboards.

## 6. Records
- Non-conformance report.
- Risk assessment.
- Investigation and root cause documentation.
- Disposition record.
- CAPA plan and effectiveness check.
