# SOP-07: CIP and Sanitation Verification

## 1. Purpose
Ensure all product-contact equipment is cleaned and sanitized effectively before use.

## 2. Scope
Applies to tanks, transfer lines, heat exchangers, fillers, and other product-contact surfaces.

## 3. Roles
- Operator: execute CIP cycle and record parameters.
- Maintenance: maintain CIP equipment and dosing systems.
- Quality: verify sanitation effectiveness.

## 4. Procedure
1. Pre-CIP Setup
- Confirm equipment is isolated and safe for cleaning.
- Verify detergent and sanitizer concentrations, temperature targets, and circulation paths.

2. CIP Cycle
- Pre-rinse.
- Caustic or detergent wash.
- Intermediate rinse.
- Acid wash (if required by equipment/process).
- Final rinse and sanitizer step (as applicable).
- Drain and protect cleaned status.

3. Critical Parameters
- Time per stage.
- Temperature.
- Chemical concentration.
- Flow/turbulence or return conductivity endpoint.

4. Verification
- Visual inspection.
- ATP, protein swab, conductivity/pH endpoint, or microbiological checks per risk plan.
- If verification fails, repeat cleaning and re-verify before release.

5. Line Clearance
- Tag equipment as Clean and Released only after all checks pass.

## 5. Small vs Large Operation Notes
- Small distillery: manual clean-out-of-place may supplement CIP; use fixed checklists to prevent skipped steps.
- Larger operation: automated recipe-driven CIP with electronic records and alarmed parameter deviations.

## 6. Records
- CIP cycle logs.
- Chemical usage records.
- Verification test results.
- Equipment clean-release status.
