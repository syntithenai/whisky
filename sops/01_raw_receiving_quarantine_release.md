# SOP-01: Raw Receiving and Quarantine Release

## 1. Purpose
Define controls for receiving, quarantining, inspecting, and releasing raw materials (grain, yeast, enzymes, casks, closures, labels, chemicals) to protect product quality, safety, and traceability.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all incoming materials at the site.

## 3. Roles
- Warehouse or Receiving Operator: unload, inspect, identify, and stage material.
- Quality (or Designated Trained Person): verify documentation, sampling, and release decision.
- Production Lead: use only released material.

## 4. Procedure
1. Pre-arrival
- Confirm approved supplier status and open purchase order.
- Prepare receiving area with clear segregation: Released, Quarantine, Rejected.

2. Receipt and Initial Checks
- Check vehicle condition, seal integrity (if applicable), and signs of contamination or damage.
- Verify supplier COA, lot numbers, quantity, and expiry against purchase order.
- Record delivery date/time, supplier, material code, lot/batch, and receiver initials.

3. Quarantine Identification
- Apply status label: Quarantine.
- Move all materials to designated quarantine zone.
- Do not issue to production before release status is assigned.

4. Inspection and Sampling
- Inspect packaging integrity and storage condition.
- Sample according to material risk:
- High risk (yeast, processing aids, bottle-contact materials): sample every lot.
- Lower risk (some dry goods from qualified suppliers): reduced frequency per approved plan.
- Perform required checks (identity, moisture, sensory, microbiology, dimensions, or certificate review).

5. Release Decision
- Quality reviews all required checks.
- Assign status:
- Released: green label and inventory status update.
- Rejected: red label, physically segregated, supplier notified.
- Conditional release (deviation-approved): controlled use with documented restrictions.

6. Non-conforming Receipt
- Raise non-conformance record.
- Hold material pending disposition: return, rework, downgrade, or discard.

## 5. Small vs Large Operation Notes
- Small distillery: one trained person may perform receiving and quality checks if conflict is controlled by second-person review of release records.
- Larger operation: separate warehouse and QA functions, barcode scanning, ERP quarantine workflow, and formal sampling rooms.

## 6. Records
- Receiving log.
- Supplier COA and delivery documents.
- Sampling and test records.
- Material status and release records.
- Non-conformance report (if applicable).

## 7. Acceptance Criteria (Examples)
- Correct material and lot.
- Packaging intact and clean.
- COA meets specification.
- Any required test results within limits.

## 8. Review Frequency
Review annually or after major supplier/specification changes.
