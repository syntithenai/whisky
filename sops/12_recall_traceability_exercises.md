# SOP-12: Recall and Traceability Exercises

## 1. Purpose
Ensure rapid, accurate traceability and effective recall execution to protect consumers and meet regulatory obligations.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all finished goods and critical input materials linked to market-released products.

## 3. Roles
- Recall Coordinator: leads recall team and communications.
- Quality and Regulatory: risk assessment and authority notifications.
- Supply Chain and Sales: customer notification and product retrieval.
- Production and Warehouse: stock hold and trace support.

## 4. Procedure
1. Recall Readiness
- Maintain current contact lists, decision tree, product coding guide, and distribution records.
- Verify traceability one step back (suppliers) and one step forward (customers/distributors).

2. Trigger and Assessment
- Trigger events: confirmed hazard, legal non-compliance, serious labeling error, or authority direction.
- Classify recall level and urgency.

3. Execution Steps
- Issue immediate internal hold on affected lots.
- Notify regulatory authority where required.
- Notify customers with clear lot identification and action instructions.
- Track quantity shipped, recovered, quarantined, and unresolved.

4. Traceability Drill Requirements
- Conduct mock recall exercises at defined frequency (for example at least annually).
- Measure time to trace and account for affected product.
- Document gaps and corrective actions.

5. Closure
- Confirm effectiveness of retrieval and communication.
- Perform post-event review and update procedures.

## 5. Small vs Large Operation Notes
- Small distillery: may manage recalls using controlled spreadsheets and phone/email trees; ensure backups and role coverage.
- Larger operation: ERP-enabled lot genealogy, automated customer lists, and structured command-center response.

## 6. Records
- Recall decision log.
- Notification records.
- Product accounting and recovery log.
- Mock recall report and CAPA actions.
