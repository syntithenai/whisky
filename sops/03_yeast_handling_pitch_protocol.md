# SOP-03: Yeast Handling and Pitch Protocol

## 1. Purpose
Control yeast storage, propagation or rehydration, and pitching to ensure healthy fermentations and consistent flavor.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all yeast strains used in production, including dry, liquid, and propagated culture.

## 3. Roles
- Cellar or Fermentation Operator: execute yeast prep and pitch.
- Quality or Lab: verify viability and contamination checks.
- Production Lead: approve pitch rate and timing.

## 4. Procedure
1. Receipt and Storage
- Verify strain identity, supplier lot, and expiry.
- Store yeast at required temperature range.
- Segregate by status: released versus hold.

2. Preparation
- Follow strain-specific rehydration or propagation instructions.
- Sanitize all contact surfaces and transfer equipment.
- Use treated process water where required.

3. Pre-Pitch Checks
- Confirm wort temperature and oxygenation target.
- Verify yeast viability and cell count (or vendor quality certificate for approved dry yeast lots).
- Confirm no signs of contamination.

4. Pitching
- Dose at target rate by volume and gravity.
- Mix uniformly to avoid stratification.
- Start fermentation log at pitch time.

5. Post-Pitch Verification
- Confirm fermenter ID, batch ID, yeast lot, pitch rate, and operator sign-off.
- Verify expected activity window (for example first signs of active fermentation within defined time).

## 5. Small vs Large Operation Notes
- Small distillery: may use dry yeast with simpler viability checks; maintain stricter sanitation discipline due to less automation.
- Larger operation: dedicated yeast room, propagation vessel, inline dosing, and routine microbiological trend monitoring.

## 6. Records
- Yeast inventory and storage log.
- Viability or quality check record.
- Pitch record tied to fermenter and batch.
- Deviations and corrective actions.
