# SOP-11: Incident and Near-Miss Reporting

## 1. Purpose
Ensure timely reporting, investigation, and learning from incidents and near-misses involving safety, environment, quality, or security.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all employees, contractors, and visitors on site activities.

## 3. Roles
- Any Person on Site: immediately report incident or near-miss.
- Supervisor: secure area and trigger response.
- EHS or Designated Lead: investigate and track actions.
- Management: review trends and ensure controls are improved.

## 4. Procedure
1. Immediate Response
- Protect people first: stop work, isolate hazards, provide first aid, call emergency services as needed.
- Preserve scene when safe for investigation.

2. Reporting Timelines
- Serious incident: immediate verbal escalation plus written report same shift.
- Minor incident/near-miss: report before shift end.

3. Initial Classification
- Safety injury/illness.
- Environmental release.
- Quality incident.
- Property or equipment damage.
- Security event.

4. Investigation
- Establish facts, contributing factors, and root cause.
- Identify immediate and systemic corrective actions.

5. Follow-Up
- Assign action owners and due dates.
- Verify closure and communicate lessons learned.

## 5. Small vs Large Operation Notes
- Small distillery: simple forms and daily stand-up reviews can be effective if response timelines are enforced.
- Larger operation: digital incident system, formal severity matrix, and cross-functional investigation teams.

## 6. Records
- Incident or near-miss report form.
- Investigation report.
- Corrective action tracker.
- Trend review summaries.
