# SOP-02: Milling, Mashing, and Lautering

## 1. Purpose
Standardize grain preparation and wort production to achieve consistent extract, fermentability, and process safety.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all milling, mash-in, temperature rests, conversion checks, and lautering operations.

## 3. Roles
- Operator or Brewer/Distiller: execute process steps and records.
- Production Lead: verify critical control points.
- Quality or Trained Designee: confirm release of wort to fermentation.

## 4. Procedure
1. Pre-Start
- Verify recipe, target grist bill, and batch size.
- Confirm mill, mash tun, pumps, and lautering equipment are clean and ready.
- Check calibrated thermometers, flow meters, and pH meter.

2. Milling
- Set mill gap per grain type.
- Mill grain to target distribution (husk mostly intact, endosperm adequately broken).
- Record lot numbers, weight, and any observed mill issues.

3. Mash-In
- Fill mash tun to required strike water volume and temperature.
- Add milled grist gradually to avoid dough balls.
- Start timer at full mash-in completion.

4. Conversion and Mash Control
- Follow recipe rest schedule (for example protein rest, saccharification, mash-out).
- Monitor and record temperature, pH, and agitation intervals.
- Perform conversion check (for example iodine test) where required.

5. Lautering
- Recirculate first runnings until clear enough per site standard.
- Start runoff at controlled rate to avoid stuck mash.
- Sparge with temperature-controlled water to target pre-boil volume and gravity.
- Stop collection at cutoff gravity or pH limits defined by process standard.

6. Handover to Fermentation
- Cool wort to yeast pitch target.
- Confirm gravity, pH, and volume are within target range.
- Transfer to approved fermenter.

## 5. Small vs Large Operation Notes
- Small distillery: manual valve and flow control; greater reliance on operator judgment with robust checklists.
- Larger operation: automated mash profiles, inline sensors, and SCADA alarms for temperature, flow, and pH drift.

## 6. Records
- Grain lots, weights, and mill settings.
- Mash profile log (time, temperature, pH).
- Lautering start/stop times, runoff rates, and collection metrics.
- Wort transfer release check.

## 7. Key Limits (Examples)
- Mash pH within recipe-defined range.
- Temperature at each rest within tolerance.
- Pre-fermentation gravity within target window.
