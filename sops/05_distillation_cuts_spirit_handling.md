# SOP-05: Distillation Cuts and Spirit Handling

## 1. Purpose
Define safe and repeatable execution of distillation cuts and spirit handling to achieve target style and legal compliance.

## 2. Scope
Applies to stripping and spirit runs (pot or column as applicable), including heads, hearts, and tails management.

## 3. Roles
- Distiller or Operator: run still and execute cut points.
- Production Lead: verify cut strategy and tank routing.
- Quality: periodic confirmation of spirit quality and ABV.

## 4. Safety Controls
- Verify ventilation and alcohol vapor controls.
- Confirm no ignition sources in hazardous area.
- Ensure emergency shutdown and spill response materials are available.

## 5. Procedure
1. Pre-Run
- Confirm still cleanliness, valve line-up, condenser function, and receiver readiness.
- Verify batch identity and transfer volume.

2. Start-Up
- Ramp heat per approved profile.
- Begin distillate collection only once stable flow is established.

3. Cut Management
- Determine cuts using a combination of ABV trend, temperature behavior, flow stability, and sensory evaluation.
- Route fractions to designated receivers with clear labels.
- Record time, volume, ABV, and cut transitions.

4. Hearts Handling
- Collect hearts to approved spirit receiver.
- Verify in-spec ABV and sensory profile.

5. Off-Cut Handling
- Segregate heads and tails for re-run, downgrade, or disposal per policy.
- Prevent unintended blend back.

6. End of Run
- Stop heat, isolate system, and cool down safely.
- Secure spirit tanks and update inventory movement records.

## 6. Small vs Large Operation Notes
- Small distillery: cut points may be more sensory-led; use tighter record templates to improve reproducibility run to run.
- Larger operation: greater use of inline ABV and automated fraction routing, with predefined decision bands.

## 7. Records
- Distillation run log.
- Fraction volumes and ABV records.
- Spirit transfer and tank inventory logs.
- Deviation report for abnormal runs.
