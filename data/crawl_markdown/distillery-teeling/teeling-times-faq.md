# Teeling Irish Whiskey FAQ

Teeling’s FAQ page explains what constitutes Irish whiskey, how Teeling produces its range, and clarifies common questions about styles, mixing, and gluten content. It outlines the four official types of Irish whiskey—Single Malt, Single Grain, Single Pot Still, and Blended—and describes Teeling’s small‑batch approach.

---

## Key Points
- **Irish whiskey definition**: mash of cereal grains, fermented, distilled, matured ≥3 years in wood casks on Ireland. 
- **Teeling production**: uses various grains, unique yeast strains, and a range of cask styles; all products are small‑batch (≈10 000 bottles). 
- **Mixing**: can be enjoyed neat, on ice, with mixers or cocktails—no single “right” way. 
- **Gluten**: safe for gluten‑sensitive consumers because distillation removes the protein.
- **Distillery focus**: emphasis on flavour and quality over mass production.

### Glossary
- **Cask** – wooden barrel used for maturation.
- **Peat** – partially decayed vegetation used to dry barley, imparting smoke flavour.
- **Small batch** – limited production run focused on quality.
- **Single Malt** – 100 % malted barley from one distillery.
- **Single Grain** – any grain mix, ≤30 % malted barley.
- **Single Pot Still** – Irish style requiring ≥30 % malted and unmalted barley.
- **Blended** – mixture of different whiskey types.
