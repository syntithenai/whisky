# Teeling Whiskey – World’s Most Awarded Irish Whiskey

Teeling, a Dublin‑based distillery founded in 2015, has amassed **over 500 international awards** across categories such as Single Malt, Single Grain and Single Pot Still. Highlights include:
- **World’s Best Single Pot Still (2022)** – *Wonders of Wood 1* (Chinquapin Oak) 
- **World’s Best Single Malt (2019)** – *24‑Year‑Old Single Malt* aged in ex‑Bourbon and ex‑Sauternes casks
- **World’s Best Single Grain (2014)** – *Single Grain* 95 % corn, 5 % malted barley, aged in Californian Red Wine casks

All products are bottled at 46 % ABV with no chill filtration. The distillery champions urban Irish whiskey revival and sustainability.

## Source Excerpts
- Our Teeling Wonders of Wood 1 is crafted from a recipe of 50% malted barley and 50% unmalted barley, which has been triple distilled in our Dublin Distillery, fermented with a combination of white wine and distillers yeast and fully matured in Virgin Chinkapin American White Oak Casks.
- This rare and vintage Irish Whiskey is made from 100% malted barley, matured for 21 years in ex-Bourbon barrels, before it was aged further for an additional three years in ex-Sauternes Wine Casks, and bottled at 46% abv with no chill filtration.
- Single Malt is considered “The Gold Standard” of World Whiskey due to its production process, as well as its character and flavour profile.
- From barley to barrels we look to push the boundaries of all stages of the whiskey making process in order to challenge conventions and perceptions around the Irish Whiskey Category, whilst still retaining the approachability of the traditional Irish Whiskey style.
- After careful attention to detail in production we bottle our Irish whiskey at our signature 46% ABV with no chill filtration for that full flavour elevated experience.
- The Teeling Whiskey Wonders of Wood Series took the world by storm, with the Teeling Wonders of Wood 1 (Chinquapin Oak) being honoured with the award of “World’s best Single Pot Still” at the World Whiskey Awards” in 2022.
