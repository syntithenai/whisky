# FSPB1 – The First Smoky Wedding Peated Whisky

- **Release**: 16 Aug 2020
- **ABV**: 48 %
- **Distillers**: Damien Newton‑Brown & Chris Condon
- **Blender/Finisher**: Tom Ambroz
- **Maturation**: 3 × 20 L American oak ex‑bourbon casks
- **Blend**: 50 % Flinders Island peated grain + 50 % Scottish (Islay) peated grain
- **Flavour profile**: earthy, warm peat from Flinders; immediate smoky, roasted, sweet BBQ notes from Scottish peat; floral, tobacco & brown‑sugar nose; porridge, orange and light honey on the palate; long finish.
- **Accolade**: Silver Medal – 2020 World Whiskies Awards

> *“This release was beautifully floral and delicate; tobacco and brown sugar on the nose, palate of porridge, orange, and light honey.”*

## Distillery & Production Facts
- Furneaux Distillery is located off Tasmania’s sea, inspired by Scotland.
- Flinders peat consists of swampmarsh herbs, tussocks, coastal heath, tea tree and melaleucas; spends much time underwater.
- Scottish peat mainly from sphagnum moss and grasses.
- The whisky was matured in American oak ex‑bourbon casks for 3 × 20 L batches.

## Source Excerpts
- We wanted to experiment with the contrast of flavour from the smoked grain from our land, versus the smoked grain from Scotland.
- It spends much of it's time underwater, and provides a very interesting flavour.
- On the palate it presents as a "peat pillow", with the majority of the smoke flavour on the back palate, quite earthy, warm and aromatic.
- Scottish peat on the other hand, is made up of mostly spagnum moss and grasses, and provides to the palate a much more immediate smoke profile, with a dark roasted, sweet, bbq flavour up front.
- These flavours together have produced a really unique and delicious whisky.
