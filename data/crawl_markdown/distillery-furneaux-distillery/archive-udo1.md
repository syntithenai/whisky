# Unpeated Double Oak (UDO1)

- **Distillery**: Furneaux Distillery, Launceston & Flinders Island
- **Release date**: 17 Dec 2021
- **ABV**: 48 %
- **Maturation**: 20 L ex‑Tawny (French oak) + 20 L ex‑Bourbon (American oak)
- **Peat content**: ~20–30 % Flinders peated malt, but marketed as unpeated
- **Blending**: Head Distiller Tom Ambroz blended on Flinders Island; final product non‑chill filtered
- **Key people**: Damien Newton‑Brown (founder & distiller), Chris Condon (distiller), Tom Ambroz (blender/finisher)
- **Notes**: Soft sweetness from Australian tawny, dominant bourbon notes, subtle peat influence.

## Flavor profile
- Soft sweetness, Australian tawny character
- Dominant bourbon notes
- Subtle peat influence (though marketed as unpeated)
- Non‑chill filtered finish

---

## Production notes
- Early batches tested varying peated malt percentages (100 % vs 30–40 %)
- Final expression chosen for its subtle peat, not released as a peated spirit
- Blended after ~2.2 years maturation, then decanted and diluted with coastal rainwater
- Set to breathe several weeks before bottling
