## Long Description
# Furneaux Distillery

**Location & Concept**
- Situated on the Furneaux Islands, Tasmania, the distillery specialises in *maritime* whisky – a style that captures the salt‑laden air and rainwater of the island.  The brand also offers a London‑dry gin that showcases native botanicals such as Kunzea and rosemary.

**Product Range**
- **Gin** – *Untamed Gin* (700 ml, $85). Vapour‑distilled for brightness, pot‑distilled juniper for body.  Botanicals: juniper, native rosemary, kunzea, wild fennel, traditional gin aromatics.
- **Single Malts** – A portfolio of unpeated and peated expressions, each released in limited runs (e.g., 200–800 bottles).  Key releases:
  - *Smoky Wedding* (FSPB5) – 47.9 % ABV, bourbon‑cask aged, maritime salt & iodine notes.
  - *Sawyers Bay Unpeated* (UDO3) – 47.3 % ABV, double oak (tawny & bourbon) casks, caramel‑dark chocolate finish.
  - *Flinders Island Peated* (FPA2) – 49 % ABV, French Oak Apera cask, coastal peat with fig/date fruitcake.
  - *UT3*, *UR1*, *UR2*, *UDO1/2/3* – unpeated spirits aged in ex‑bourbon, fortified wine, or second‑fill ex‑tawny casks, each with distinct spice, fruit, and oak profiles.
- **Special Releases** – Historical batches such as XX 297‑301 (unpeated Apera cask) and XX 154‑155 (tawny cask spirit) showcase the distillery’s experimentation with local barley and unique maturation vessels.

**Production & Distillation**
- Small‑batch pot stills produce a high‑quality spirit.  Vapour distillation is used for gin, while whisky undergoes traditional pot distillation followed by careful blending of cask types (bourbon, fortified wine, Apera sherry, etc.).
- The island’s rainwater and maritime climate are integral to the maturation process, often finished in shipping containers or coastal dunnage warehouses.

**People & Leadership**
- Founders: *Damien Newton‑Brown* and *Chris Condon*.  Head Distiller/Blender: *Tom Ambroz*, with *Ian Reed* as Blender/Finisher.  Malt supplied by *Joe White* (Tasmanian Pilsner malt).

**Visitor & Commerce Experience**
- The distillery offers tours, tastings, and an online shop.  Age‑gate checks are enforced on the website.
- Products are sold in limited quantities; many releases have a fixed bottle count (e.g., 200–800 bottles).  Prices range from $85 for gin to $220+ for whisky.

**Awards & Media**
- Several expressions have received awards and press coverage, highlighting their unique maritime character and innovative cask use.

Overall, Furneaux Distillery blends traditional Scottish techniques with Tasmanian terroir to create distinctive whiskies and a botanically rich gin that reflects the island’s natural heritage.
