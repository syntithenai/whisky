# UDO3 – Furneaux Distillery

- **Spirit/Malt**: 100 % unpeated, Joe White Tasmanian Pilsner Malt.
- **Maturation**: Four Seppeltsfield Tawny Port quarter casks (5 yrs) + ten 20 L American Oak Bourbon casks (2.5 yrs).
- **ABV**: 47.3 %.
- **Release date**: 28 Jan 2026.
- **Tasting notes**: Musk, red apple, cinnamon; full juicy muscatel grapes, dark chocolate hint, sweet finish with caramel.
- **Production highlights**: Largest vatting to date; early maturation at Flinders Wharf, then coastal bond store; gentle flocking for ~1 month.
- **Suggested pairings**: Dark chocolate, cake, cigar.

> *“This release represents our largest vatting to date – a milestone for a small island distillery where every cask is handled with deliberate care.”*

## Source Excerpts
- Four Seppeltsfield Tawny Port quarter casks form the heart of this dram, building a rich and expansive palate layered with lush dried fruits, muscatel grapes and dates alongside the deep, vinous sweetness that only long-seasoned fortified wine casks can deliver.
- A selection of bourbon casks was introduced to the vatting, bringing soft vanilla sweetness and creamy oak that rounds the back palate and smooths the finish into a beautifully balanced whole.
- This release represents our largest vatting to date - a milestone for a small island distillery where every cask is handled with deliberate care.
- These remarkable casks, more than 50 years old, lend both intensity and structure, though their lingering tannin called for a gentle counterpoint.
- The Tawny cask spirit matured for five years, and the bourbon cask component for 2.5 before being married.
- The whisky was then gently flocked for around a month, preserving its natural texture and flavour while allowing any remaining sediment to settle.
