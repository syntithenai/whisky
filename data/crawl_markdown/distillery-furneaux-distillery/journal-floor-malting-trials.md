# Floor Malting Trials – Furneaux Distillery

Furneaux Distillery’s first on‑island malting trial used barley grown at Flinders Island. The grain was steeped in modified storage cubes, germinated on a trailer kiln floor, and then kilned with hot air to stop fermentation. No peat was added, so the malt remained clean and “green”. The resulting malt is now being used for whisky production, with plans to scale up the process for full supply‑chain independence.

Key points:
- First harvest barley (Planet 2nd gen) grown on Flinders Island.
- Traditional floor malting: steep → germinate on a thin bed → regular raking → kilning.
- No peat used; clean malt profile.
- Trial successful, providing foundation for future in‑house malthouse.

The whisky made from this grain is aging in oak casks at the coastal bond store and will be released after further maturation.
