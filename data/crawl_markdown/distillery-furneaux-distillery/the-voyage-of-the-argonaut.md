# The Voyage of the Argonaut

A narrative by Jock Serong recounting a maritime journey from Furneaux Distillery’s Flinders Island to Melbourne aboard the vessel *Argonaut*. The piece weaves historical context about Bass Strait, practical details of the voyage (weather windows, mooring, navigation), and vivid descriptions of the surrounding islands, wildlife, lighthouses, and local culture. It ends with a brief mention of author Jock Serong’s novels and a call‑to‑action for newsletter sign‑ups.

---

## Key Themes
- **Bass Strait history** – from Aboriginal observations to George Bass’s exploration.
- **Logistics of the voyage** – timing, weather windows, mooring at Blue Rocks, navigation systems on *Argonaut*.
- **Island scenery and wildlife** – Deal Island, Goose Island, Wilson’s Promontory, Rodondo Island, sea‑life such as fur seals and Cape Barren Geese.
- **Cultural references** – Jock Serong’s literary work, local folklore, and the sense of isolation at lighthouses.

---

## Practical Information
- The *Argonaut* departed in May, a one‑week window chosen for favorable weather.
- Mooring off Blue Rocks required “bush engineering” and was described as a feat.
- Navigation included brass dials, a wooden steering wheel, a traditional compass, and a touch‑screen autopilot system.
