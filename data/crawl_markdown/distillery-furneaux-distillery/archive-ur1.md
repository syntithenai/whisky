# UR1 – Furneaux Distillery

- **Spirit**: 100 % unpeated, Tasmanian Pilsner Malt.
- **Cask**: 20‑L second‑fill Flinders Island peated ex‑bourbon and fortified wine casks (3 years).
- **ABV**: 47 %
- **Release date**: 16 May 2024
- **Distiller/Blender**: Tom Ambroz (distiller), Tom Ambroz/Ian Reed (blender/finisher)
- **Bottle count**: 176
- **Tasting notes**: Honey, pear, white grapes; golden syrup, toasted oats, ginger, cinnamon; light peat smoke finish.
- **Production insight**: Second‑fill casks reduce tannin/lactone extraction, yielding a lighter, fruitier spirit.

> *“When you fill a cask for the first time…the timber will impart less flavour, and the spirit will be more present.”* – Tom Ambroz

## Source Excerpts
- Many years ago when I started my journey in the whisky world, I preferred rich, bold whiskies with loads of oak and sherry flavour.
- Spirit/Malt Style 100% Unpeated Joe White Tasmanian Pilsner Malt Maturation/Cask Type A combination of 20l second fill Flinders Island peated ex bourbon and fortified wine casks.
- 3 year maturation Release Date 16/05/2024 Distiller Tom Ambroz Blender/Finisher Tom Ambroz/Ian Reed ABV 47% Bottles 176 It’s one of the most common questions I get asked in the distillery - “can you use a cask more than once?”.
- The answer is of course yes, but it’s not been a widely common practice in Australian whisky, mostly because a large portion of our distilleries are not old enough to have used a barrel even once!
- As distilleries are emptying barrels and bottling those whiskies, we are starting to see more and more second fill cask whiskies coming out.
- When you fill a cask for the first time, there is a huge amount of tannin, lactones, aldehydes, all compounds that impart spice, flavour, intensity, astringency.
