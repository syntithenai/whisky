# The First Second Fill Cask Single Malt (FPR1)

- **Release**: Mar 25 2023
- **ABV**: 45.8%
- **Spirit**: 100% Flinders Peated Tasmanian Pilsner Malt
- **Maturation**: 3 years in two 20‑l French oak casks (ex‑Apera & ex‑Tawny)
- **Distiller/Blender**: Tom Ambroz
- **Bottles**: 70
- **Key Insight**: Second‑fill casks can preserve spirit character if the oak influence is limited; 20‑l casks are preferred for second fills.

> *“With a 20 l cask, I prefer not to leave the spirit in the cask for more than 2.5 years… The oak starts to have a diminishing influence on the balance of the whisky.”* – Tom Ambroz

## Source Excerpts
- Now that being said, the oak influence, and indeed the original fill matter (bourbon, port etc) will be far lighter than the first maturation, but that is where most distillers will agree - the spirit can shine through as the predominant flavour.
- The very fact that I decant a cask after only 2.5 years means there is still a viable level of flavour in that cask.
- Often it can be overshadowed by a rich sherry cask or a big sweet bourbon cask, but in a refill cask, the nuances of that spirit can shine.
- It’s a topic often discussed in the whisky world - can one use a cask more than once?
- Personally, I think that as always it depends on the cask.
- This release was matured in two 20l casks, both of Furneaux unpeated spirit previously.
