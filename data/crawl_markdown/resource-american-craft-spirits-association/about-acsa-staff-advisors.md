# Staff & Advisors

American Craft Spirits Association (ACSA) is a member-owned, industry-driven organization dedicated to supporting the growth and development of small-batch distillers in the United States. The association provides resources, education, advocacy, and networking opportunities for its members.

## Key Facts
- ACSA was founded in 2014 with the mission to support and promote American craft distillers
- The organization offers a range of programs and services tailored to the needs of small-batch distilleries
- Membership is open to distillers producing less than 10,000 gallons per year
- ACSA provides educational resources, advocacy efforts, and networking opportunities through its events and online platforms

## Production Signals
ACSA supports distillers in maintaining high-quality standards and best practices. The association encourages members to adhere to strict production guidelines and invest in equipment that ensures consistency and safety.

## Commercial Signals
ACSA's membership model is designed to support small-batch distilleries, providing them with a platform to showcase their products and connect with potential customers. The organization advocates for policies that foster the growth of the craft spirits industry while protecting the interests of its members.

## Risks/Unknowns
As with any emerging industry, there are inherent risks associated with starting and operating a small-batch distillery. ACSA works to mitigate these risks through education, advocacy, and community building. However, external factors such as regulatory changes, market fluctuations, and competition can impact the success of individual distilleries.
