# About ACSA – American Craft Spirits Association

The American Craft Spirits Association (ACSA) is the sole registered national nonprofit trade group representing U.S. craft spirits producers. It is member‑owned, with a Board of Directors elected by voting members—craft distillers or brands that meet specific production and ownership thresholds. ACSA’s mission is to elevate and advocate for the craft spirits community through membership support, diversity initiatives, market development, and organizational stability.

Key points:
- **Vision**: Recognize craft spirits worldwide.
- **Mission**: Advocate for craft producers.
- **Priorities**: Membership growth, diversity, market improvement, stability.
- **Values**: Commitment to diversity and respectful inclusion.
- **Membership**: Open to anyone; voting members must be craft distillers or brands with <750,001 proof gallons removed from bond and limited ownership by larger producers.
- **Resources**: Data projects, webinars, guides, magazine, and advocacy tools.

ACSA also manages a variety of programs such as conventions, competitions, packaging awards, and educational initiatives.
