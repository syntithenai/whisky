# Final2021Craft Spirits Data Project

The American Craft Spirits Association (ACSA) released a concise economic briefing summarizing the U.S. craft spirits industry for 2020‑2021. Key points include:
- **Growth**: The number of active craft distillers rose to 2,290 in August 2021 (+1.1% YoY). Retail sales volume reached over 12 million 9‑liter cases (≈$6.7 billion) with a 7.3 % volume CAGR and 9.8 % value CAGR.
- **Market share**: Craft spirits accounted for 4.7 % of total U.S. spirit volume and 7.1 % of value in 2020, up from 2.2 %/3 % in 2015.
- **Concentration**: Less than 2 % of producers supply more than 56 % of cases; 90 % are classified as small producers but only contribute ~10 % of sales.
- **Geography**: 54 % of business occurs outside the home state, with large producers driving most out‑of‑state and export activity.
- **Employment & investment**: Full‑time staff fell by ~24 % from 2018 to 2020; total capital invested rose to $759 million in 2020.

The briefing also outlines the ACSA definition of a craft spirit, data sources (TTB, state boards, surveys), and contact information for further inquiries.
