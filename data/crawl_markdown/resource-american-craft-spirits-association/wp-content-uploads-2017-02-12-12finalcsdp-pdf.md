# American Craft Spirits Association – 2022 Economic Briefing

- **Scope**: U.S. craft spirits market (licensed producers <750 k proof gallons, independent ownership, no ethics violations). 
- **Key metrics (2021)**:
  - Active distillers: **2,687** (+17.4% YoY).
  - Retail volume: **13.2 M 9‑L cases** (+10.4%).
  - Value: **$7.5 B** (+12.2%).
  - Market share: **4.9 % volume**, **7.5 % value** of total U.S. spirits.
  - Exports: **164,000 cases** (+58%).
  - Employment: **24,255 full‑time domestic employees**.
- **Producer concentration**:
  - Large (≤394k cases): 1.7 % of producers, 55.9 % of cases.
  - Small (<5,258 cases): 89.2 % of producers, 10.9 % of cases.
- **Investment**: Total $826 M in 2021; average per distiller $337 k (‑2% YoY).
- **Geography**: Top 5 states (CA, PA, TX, NY, WA) hold 32.4 % of producers.
- **Trends to 2026**: Projected market share growth ranging from 6.6 % to 9.8 % depending on CAGR assumptions.

---

## Highlights
1. **Resilient growth** post‑pandemic with double‑digit volume and value gains.
2. **Export expansion** is a key driver, especially for large producers.
3. **Investment focus**: production capacity & visitor space.
4. **Employment rebound** but still below pre‑COVID levels.
5. **Definition challenges** – no single certification; ACSA’s definition used for research.
