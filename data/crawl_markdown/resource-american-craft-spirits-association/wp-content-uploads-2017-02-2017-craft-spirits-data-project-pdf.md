# American Craft Spirits Data Project – 2017 Economic Briefing

The 2017 PowerPoint presentation from the American Craft Spirits Association (ACSA) summarizes key data on U.S. craft spirits, including definitions, market size, growth rates, producer concentration, sales channels, employment, and investment. It also outlines the collaborative project that gathers these metrics annually.

- **Definition**: Licensed producers removing ≤750 000 proof gallons from bond, self‑proclaimed craft, not controlled by a large supplier, and compliant with ACSA Code of Ethics.
- **Growth**: 1,589 active distillers in Aug 2017 (↑20.8% YoY). Retail volume 5.8 m cases (18.5% CAGR), value $3.0 bn (25% CAGR).
- **Market Share**: 2.6 % of volume, 3.8 % of value in 2016.
- **Concentration**: ~2 % of producers supply 57 % of cases; 92 % are small and supply only 12.8 %.
- **Sales Channels**: Direct‑to‑consumer (DSP) vital for small distillers (34 % of sales); large distillers rely more on out‑of‑state business (>58 %).
- **Employment & Investment**: 19,600 FTEs in 2017 (+47.8%), $593 m total investment.
- **Future Outlook**: Projected continued rapid growth; potential to surpass craft beer’s market share.

The presentation is a snapshot of the industry’s economic footprint and strategic priorities as of 2017.
