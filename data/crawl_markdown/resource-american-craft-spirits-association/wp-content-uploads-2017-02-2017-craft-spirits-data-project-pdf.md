# American Craft Spirits Data Project – 2017

The 2017 PowerPoint presentation from the American Craft Spirits Association (ACSA) provides a comprehensive snapshot of the U.S. craft spirits industry. Key highlights include:
- **Definition**: ACRA defines craft spirits as licensed producers removing ≤750,000 proof gallons from bond, self‑proclaimed craft, not controlled by large suppliers, and compliant with the ACSA Code of Ethics.
- **Industry Size & Growth**: Active craft distillers rose 20.8 % to 1,589 in August 2017; retail sales reached 5.8 million cases (18.5 % volume CAGR) and $3.0 billion (25 % value CAGR). Market share hit 2.6 % volume / 3.8 % value.
- **Concentration**: ~2 % of producers account for 57 % of sales; 92 % are small (<10,000 proof gallons) and contribute only 12.8 % of cases.
- **Sales Channels**: Direct‑to‑consumer (DSP) sales are crucial for small distillers (34 % of total), while large producers rely heavily on out‑of‑state business (>58 %).
- **Employment & Investment**: Full‑time employees grew 47.8 % to ~19,600; industry investment reached ~$600 million in 2017.
- **Future Outlook**: Projections suggest continued rapid growth, with craft spirits potentially matching or surpassing craft beer’s market share by 2020.

The presentation also outlines the collaborative research framework (ACSA, IWSR, Park Street) and provides contact details for further inquiries.
