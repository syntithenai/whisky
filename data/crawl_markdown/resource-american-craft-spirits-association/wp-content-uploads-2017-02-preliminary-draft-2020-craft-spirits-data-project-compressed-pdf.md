# Final 2020_Craft Spirits Data Project

This PDF is an internal draft of the American Craft Spirits Association’s (ACSA) annual economic briefing for November 2020. It presents key statistics on the U.S. craft spirits market, including:
- **Active craft distillers**: 2,265 in August 2020, up 10.7 % from the previous year.
- **Market volume and value**: 11.9 million 9‑liter cases (volume) and $6.1 billion (value) in 2019; growth rates of 24 % and 27.1 %, respectively.
- **Market share**: 4.6 % of total U.S. spirits volume and 6.9 % of value in 2019, up from 1.8 %/2.3 % in 2014.
- **Concentration**: Less than 2 % of producers account for ~60 % of cases; 90 % are small producers (≤10,000 proof gallons) and contribute only 11.7 % of sales.
- **Sales channels**: Direct‑to‑consumer sales dominate for small distillers (47 % of total), while large producers rely heavily on out‑of‑state business (70 %).
- **COVID‑19 impact**: Most producers saw double‑digit sales declines in H1 2020, yet 80 % remain confident they will survive the downturn.
- **Employment & investment**: Full‑time employees rose to >30,000 by 2019; total capital invested reached $698 million in 2019.

The briefing also includes detailed tables on state distribution of distillers, producer size categories (small, medium, large), and projected market scenarios for 2020‑2023.
