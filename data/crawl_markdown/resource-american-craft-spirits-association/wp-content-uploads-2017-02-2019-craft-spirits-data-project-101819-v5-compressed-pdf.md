# 2019 Craft Spirits Data Project

The American Craft Spirits Association (ACSA) released a preliminary economic briefing on the U.S. craft spirits market in October 2019. Key points:
- **Active craft distillers** grew 11.5 % to 2,046 in August 2019.
- **Retail volume** reached ~9 million cases in 2018 (25.5 % CAGR) and **value** $4.8 billion (27 % CAGR).
- Market share: **3.9 % of volume** and **5.8 % of value** in 2018, up from 1.5 %/1.8 % in 2013.
- **Exports** fell to 304,000 cases (≈3 % of total sales) but still added 3.3 % volume.
- The market is fairly concentrated: ~2 % of producers account for 58 % of cases; 92 % are small producers responsible for only 13 % of cases.
- **Direct‑to‑consumer (DSP)** sales are crucial, especially for small distillers (44 % of total sales).
- Employment rose >7,000 to 25,500 in 2018; investment grew $86 million to $679 million.
- The study was a collaboration between ACSA, IWSR and Park Street, using TTB, state boards, surveys and internal analysis.

The briefing includes tables on distiller counts by state, producer size categories (small/medium/large), sales sources (DSP, home‑state, other states, export) and growth trends. It also contains survey results showing retailers’ optimism that craft spirits will perform as well or better than craft beer.

---

**Key Takeaways for Data Enrichment**:
- The 2019 data set provides a baseline for tracking the rapid expansion of U.S. craft distillers and their market impact.
- Concentration metrics highlight opportunities for smaller producers to capture larger shares.
- Direct‑to‑consumer sales are a significant growth lever, especially for small distilleries.
