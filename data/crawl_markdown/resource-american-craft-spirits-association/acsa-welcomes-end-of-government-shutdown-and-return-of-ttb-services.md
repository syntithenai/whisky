# ACSA Welcomes End of Government Shutdown and Return of TTB Services

The American Craft Spirits Association (ACSA) announced that the federal government shutdown has ended and that the Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau (TTB) will soon resume operations. The shutdown, which began on October 1, 2025, had furloughed over 85% of TTB staff, delaying label approvals, formula submissions, and new permits during a critical sales period for craft distilleries.

ACSA’s CEO Margie A.S. Lehrman thanked members for their patience and highlighted the impact on holiday releases that were stalled in approval queues. The organization will continue to advocate for efficient processing and monitor congressional budget actions.

Key takeaways:
- Permits, COLAs, and formula approvals will restart but may experience longer turnaround times.
- Tax filings remain required; keep payment schedules current.
- Backlogged submissions will be handled in the order TTB determines.
- Certain label edits can proceed without a new COLA (e.g., deleting non‑mandatory info, repositioning text/graphics).
