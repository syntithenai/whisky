# ACSA Comments to FTC Strategic Plan

The American Craft Spirits Association (ACSA) submits a letter to the FTC on October 17, 2025 urging the agency to conduct a comprehensive study of competition in the craft spirits industry. The letter highlights systemic market barriers—outdated distribution laws, restrictive direct‑to‑consumer shipping rules, anti‑competitive practices by large producers, and patchwork state regulations—that limit growth for nearly 600 independent craft distilleries. ACSA calls for research, advocacy, and guidance to modernize policies, expand consumer access, and support small producers, echoing the FTC’s earlier wine e‑commerce study.

---

**Key Points**
- Craft spirits grew from <100 producers in 2010 to >2,200 today.
- Current barriers: mandatory distribution laws, DTC shipping restrictions, pay‑to‑play sponsorship abuse, state law patchwork.
- ACSA requests FTC research on:
  - Competitive effects of state regulations and DTC shipping limits.
  - Barriers to market access for small distilleries.
  - Economic impact of modernizing these rules.
- The letter references the 2003 FTC wine e‑commerce report as a model.
- ACSA offers further discussion on how FTC work can support craft spirits producers.
