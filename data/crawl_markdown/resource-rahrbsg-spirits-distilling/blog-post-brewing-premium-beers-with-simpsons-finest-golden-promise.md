# Brewing Premium Beers with Simpsons Finest Golden Promise™

- **Golden Promise** is a heritage Scottish barley that has become a staple in both traditional British ales and modern American craft beers.
- The article features interviews with brewers from Roosters (UK), Surly (MN, USA) and Maui (Hawaii) who share how they use the malt to enhance hop character, body, and flavor complexity.
- Key take‑aways:
  - **Roosters** uses it for Pale Ales and IPAs, noting its sweet, maltier backbone that balances New World hops.
  - **Surly** treats Golden Promise as a cornerstone of its portfolio, citing improved mouthfeel, biscuit‑y notes, and versatility across low‑ABV to high‑hop beers.
  - **Maui** employs it sparingly in premium releases, highlighting its ability to add backbone without overpowering robust Pacific Northwest hops.
- The piece also touches on logistical challenges of sourcing the malt from the UK and offers practical tips for mill adjustment when using Golden Promise.

---

## Key Facts
- Heritage barley first released in the 1960s; phased out by the 1980s but preserved by Simpsons Malt.
- Simpsons Malt secured Plant Variety Rights in 2015 to guarantee supply.
- Brewers praise its ability to project hops, provide body, and deliver biscuit‑y, malt‑rich flavor.
- Golden Promise is often blended with specialty malts or used at ~50% of the bill for premium beers.

---

## Practical Tips
- Adjust mill settings: kernels are slightly larger than standard 2‑Row.
- Use in conjunction with American hops (Citra, Cascade) to balance assertiveness.
- Works well in both light and dark styles; adds roundness without cloying.
