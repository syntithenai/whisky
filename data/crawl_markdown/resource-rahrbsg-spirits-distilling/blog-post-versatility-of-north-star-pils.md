# The Versatility of North Star Pils® in Four Beers

RahrBSG’s pilot brewery brewed four different beers at the All Pints North festival, each using at least 80 % Rahr North Star Pils (NSP) malt. The styles ranged from a German‑style pilsner and Czech pale lager to a tropical rice lager and a West Coast IPA. Brewers noted NSP’s low color, honey‑sweet bread character, and nutty/hay aroma that works well in both classic lagers and hop‑heavy styles.

- **Danger Eagle Pils** – 4.9 % ABV, 25 IBU, 2.6 SRM; 97 % NSP + 3 % dextrin malt. Crisp, dry finish with honey and lemon‑y hop notes.
- **Tropical Rice Lager** – 5.9 % ABV, 20 IBU, 1.9 SRM; 80 % NSP + 20 % flaked rice. Stone‑fruit, apricot, passionfruit hops with a light coconut mouthfeel.
- **Czech Pale Lager** – 5.1 % ABV, 28 IBU, 3.1 SRM; 100 % NSP. Classic Saaz hop bitterness and malt backbone.
- **West Coast IPA** – (details omitted in the excerpt).

The post highlights how NSP can be a versatile base for diverse beer styles, encouraging brewers to experiment with it in their own recipes.
