# Alcohol Trade Groups Call on USTR to Remove Spirits and Wine from Retaliatory Tariffs List
The Distilled Spirits Council of the United States (DSC) and other trade groups submitted comments to the U.S. Trade Representative urging that spirits, wine, and non‑alcoholic beer be removed from a draft list of European Union products targeted for retaliatory tariffs. The groups warned that such tariffs would harm U.S. jobs, consumers, and exporters already affected by unrelated trade disputes, potentially costing $6.8 billion in imports and 6,600–45,800 jobs.

**Key points**
- Draft list announced April 8 includes brandy, liqueurs, wine, non‑alcoholic beer, etc.
- Groups oppose inclusion of beverage alcohol products.
- Estimated impact: $6.8 billion imports; 6,600–45,800 U.S. jobs lost.
- Joint comment signed by DSC, American Craft Spirits Association, American Distilled Spirits Association, Kentucky Distillers’ Association, Wine Institute, WineAmerica, Wine & Spirits Wholesalers of America, American Beverage Licensees, and National Association of Beverage Importers.
