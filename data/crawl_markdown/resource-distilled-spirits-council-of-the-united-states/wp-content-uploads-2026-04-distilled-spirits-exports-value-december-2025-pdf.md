# Distilled Spirits Exports – December 2025

The U.S. Customs Service data (Census Export CD‑ROM, Dec 2025) shows that the United States shipped **$2.37 billion** worth of distilled spirits in December 2025, a **3.3 % decline** from the same month in 2024.

| Class | Value Dec 2025 | Change vs Dec 2024 |
|-------|---------------|-------------------|
| Whisky – Bourbon | $22.18 M | ‑10 %
| Whisky – Other | $54.48 M | ‑21.6 %
| Rum | $4.59 M | +3.6 %
| Brandy | $3.76 M | +56.5 %
| Gin | $4.20 M | ‑11.0 %
| Vodka | $25.27 M | ‑3.4 %
| Cordials | $57.93 M | +39.4 %
| Other Distilled Spirits | $33.21 M | +7.4 %

The **total non‑whisky** segment grew by 14.8 % to $205.6 M, while the overall export value fell by $80 M.

Export destinations are listed in detail for each class; the largest markets for bourbon and other whisky were Australia, Canada, China, and the United Kingdom.

---

**Key Takeaways**
- Whisky remains the dominant export category but is declining.
- Brandy shows a sharp rebound, largely driven by European demand.
- Bulk shipments are significant for rum and gin, especially to Asia.
- The U.S. continues to be a major exporter of distilled spirits worldwide.
