# American Spirits Exports Report (2025)

- U.S. spirits exports fell **3.8 %** to **$2.37 billion** in 2025, largely due to trade friction and retaliatory actions.
- The biggest drop was from Canada after the removal of U.S. spirits from provincial shelves; exports to Canada fell >70 %. Only Alberta and Saskatchewan have lifted their bans.
- American whiskey exports declined **19 %** (down $1.08 billion), mainly because of a 35 % fall in EU shipments following front‑loading by some producers amid potential tariffs.
- Outside Canada and the EU, exports grew **2.5 %**, with gains in Brazil, the UK, Australia and emerging markets.
- Top export destinations: EU ($1.2 billion), UK ($153 million), Australia ($138 million), Mexico ($127 million) and Canada ($89 million).
- 43 states exported spirits; Kentucky and Tennessee were the largest contributors.
- DISCUS recommends restoring tariff‑free trade with key partners (EU, UK), negotiating new agreements with Argentina, Malaysia, Indonesia, Ecuador, and opening markets in high‑tariff countries such as India, Brazil, Vietnam, South Africa.

---

**Key Takeaway:** Trade friction has exposed the U.S. spirits industry’s vulnerability; restoring tariff‑free access is essential for continued growth.

- Spirits Exports Decline 3.8% in 2025 Amid Ongoing Trade Friction Removal from Canadian Shelves, Drop in American Whiskey Exports to EU Contributing Factors WASHINGTON — U.S.
- spirits exports declined 3.8% in 2025 to $2.37 billion, reflecting the impact of ongoing trade friction, retaliatory actions and broader economic headwinds, according to the 2025 American Spirits Exports Report released today by the Distilled Spirits Council of the United States (DISCUS).
- Exports to Canada dropped sharply following the removal of American-made spirits from provincial store shelves beginning in March 2025, while exports of American whiskeys to the European Union declined after some producers front-loaded shipments in late 2024 amid the threat of retaliatory tariffs.
- Despite these setbacks, over the past 25 years, exports have grown fivefold, from $478 million in 2000 to $2.37 billion in 2025, driven by decades of tariff-free access to key markets and rising global demand for premium American spirits.
- spirits exports in 2025 underscores the industry’s vulnerability to uncertainty in the global trade environment and the vital importance of restoring the permanent return to zero-for zero tariffs on spirits products,” said DISCUS President & CEO Chris Swonger.
