# A Fine Balance – Bruichladdich Distillery

Bruichladdich is balancing its Victorian heritage with modern efficiency during its annual "silent season" maintenance. The distillery undertakes 13 projects, notably refurbishing the malt intake system and installing an ultrafiltration cooling tower to reduce water usage. While machinery like conveyors and augers are upgraded for energy‑efficiency, core elements such as the 1881 open‑top mash tun and manual still operation remain untouched to preserve the whisky’s character.

The article highlights the tension between tradition and progress, quoting Production Director Allan Logan on keeping essential Victorian equipment while embracing sustainable upgrades. It also explains why Islay distilleries shut down in summer due to low water tables.

## Key Points
- 13 maintenance projects during shutdown.
- Major focus: renovation of malt intake conveyors/elevators (1960s‑era).
- New ultrafiltration system for cooling tower to recycle rainwater.
- Core Victorian equipment (mash tun, mill, washbacks, pot stills) kept unchanged.
- Manual distillation process retained; automation limited.
- Summer shutdown due to low water tables on Islay.
