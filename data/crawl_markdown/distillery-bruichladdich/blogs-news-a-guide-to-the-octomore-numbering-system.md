# A Guide to the Octomore Numbering System

Bruichladdich explains how each Octomore edition is numbered and what distinguishes them:
- **.1** – 100 % Scottish barley, aged mainly in ex‑American oak (bourbon/Tennessee). Shows raw spirit with modest American influence.
- **.2** – Same barley, but matured in European oak (ex‑Amarone, Sauternes, etc.). Choice of casks is at the Head Distiller’s discretion.
- **.3** – 100 % Islay barley from James Brown’s farm; single‑estate, single‑vintage bottlings. Maturation varies between ex‑American and ex‑European oak.
- **.4** – Introduced in Series 7; incorporates Virgin Oak for tannic, spicy character, sometimes combined with American oak or full Virgin Oak maturation. Occasionally swapped with a Ten‑Year‑Old.

The article also notes the distillery’s playful approach to bottling and occasional mischief.
