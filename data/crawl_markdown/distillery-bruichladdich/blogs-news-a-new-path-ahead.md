# A New Path Ahead

Bruichladdich Distillery announced the completion of a **2.6 km shared‑use pathway** linking Port Charlotte and Bruichladdich, finished in 2021. The route runs along the shore from the distillery pier to Port Charlotte village, featuring accessible ramps, a gateway at the World War memorial, and a spur to Port Ban bay for picnics and swimming.

The project was led by **Iain Hepburn** (designer/engineer) with funding and support from Islay Community Access Group, Argyll & Bute Council, Bruichladdich Distillery, The Botanist Foundation, and several match‑funding bodies. Local contractors handled groundworks, stonework, fencing, bridge construction, steelwork, and the final Colas topping.

Bruichladdich’s Production Director Allan Logan highlighted the path’s benefits for locals and visitors, noting increased car‑free commuting for employees on the Port Charlotte side.
