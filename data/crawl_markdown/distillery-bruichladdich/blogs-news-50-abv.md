# Jim McEwan on 50% ABV

Former Bruichladdich Master Distiller **Jim McEwan** explains why the distillery increased the alcohol‑by‑volume (ABV) of some barley‑exploration single malts to **50 %**. The decision followed a six‑year study comparing flavours at 46 % and 50 % ABV, showing that higher strength brings out richer barley notes, less oak dominance, and a more viscous mouthfeel. McEwan stresses the importance of raw ingredients, oak policy, and bottling young to preserve essential oils.

---

## Key points
- Six‑year study on Islay barley at 46 % vs 50 % ABV.
- 50 % ABV enhances barley flavours, reduces oak dominance.
- Richer viscosity and longer finish.
- Oak policy: best world‑sourced staves, minimal evaporation loss.
- Bottling young preserves essential oils; non‑chill filtered, non‑coloured.

---

## Takeaway
Bottling at 50 % ABV captures the essence of Islay barley and Hebridean craftsmanship, delivering a distinct, richer whisky experience.
