# Bruichladdich Single Malt Scotch Whisky

Bruichladdich is a progressive Hebridean distillery located in Islay, Scotland. Founded in 1881, they are known for their unpeated whiskies that showcase the raw ingredients and terroir of the region.

## Key Facts
- **No Peat**: Bruichladdich whiskies are made without peat smoke, allowing the natural flavors of the barley to shine through.
- **Terroir Focus**: The distillery traces ingredients from origin to bottling, embracing every growing season and barley varietal.
- **Slow Fermentation & Distillation**: Whiskies are fermented slowly in wooden washbacks and distilled using tall, narrow necked stills for a unique character.
- **Unchill-filtered & Color-free**: Bottled unchill-filtered and colorless to preserve the full expression of the whisky.

## Production Signals
- **Slow Fermentation**: 48 hours in wooden washbacks before distillation.
- **Trickle Distillation**: Whisky is trickle distilled through tall, narrow necked stills for a concentrated flavor profile.
- **No Peat Smoke**: No peat used during malting or production.
- **Unchill-filtered & Color-free**: Bottled as-is to maintain the natural color and texture.

## Commercial Signals
- **Unique Expressions**: Each release tells the story of its ingredients, location, and distillation process (e.g., Eighteen Years, Thirty Aged Years).
- **Limited Editions**: Exclusive releases like X4+18 Edition 01 showcase innovative maturation techniques.
- **Sustainability Focus**: Commitment to responsible sourcing and environmental stewardship.

## Risks / Unknowns
- **Limited Availability**: Many expressions are limited release or exclusive, making them hard to find.
- **Price Points**: High-end whiskies like Thirty Aged Years command premium prices.
- **Maturation Variability**: Unchill-filtered and color-free bottling can lead to variability in appearance but not flavor.
