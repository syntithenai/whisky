# A Toast to the Curious

This blog post explores how curiosity and experimentation have shaped Bruichladdich Distillery’s approach to whisky making, especially the creation of Octomore. It traces the historical shift from peat‑based production to modern innovation, highlights key figures (Mark Reynier, Simon Coughlin, Jim McEwan), and explains the technical choices behind Octomore’s extreme phenolic levels, use of virgin oak, short maturation, and high strength.

- **Historical context** – Peat was once essential; industrialisation reduced its role.
- **Curiosity as driver** – Reopening in 2001, regenerative farming, soil trials.
- **Octomore genesis** – Born from a pub conversation about pushing peat beyond limits.
- **Technical details** – Five‑day peat exposure, phenolic measurement, blending of peated and unpeated malt, fresh bourbon/wine casks, high‑strength bottling.
- **Philosophy** – Quality spirit, balance over quantity; experimentation as a continual question‑asking process.
