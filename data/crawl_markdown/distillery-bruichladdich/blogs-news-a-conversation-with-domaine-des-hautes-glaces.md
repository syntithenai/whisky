# Bruichladdich x Domaine des Hautes Glaces

A conversation between Bruichladdich’s Production Director Allan Logan and Frédéric Revol, founder of France’s first organic whisky distillery, explores how grain variety, terroir, and regenerative farming can reduce carbon footprints while creating distinct flavour profiles. Both distilleries collaborate with local farmers to grow barley, rye, spelt, oats, einkorn, and wheat, experimenting with malting techniques that highlight the unique characteristics of each grain.

Key points:
- Bruichladdich was founded by wine merchants who brought a terroir‑centric mindset to whisky.
- Revol’s distillery in the French Alps uses organic, regenerative practices and local rye varieties.
- Both distilleries emphasize long‑term soil health over short‑term yield.
- The resulting whiskies showcase diverse flavour notes: Bruichladdich’s rye is earthy with maritime hints; Hautes Glaces’ Vulson is fruity and floral.
- The partnership demonstrates how sustainable agriculture can benefit producers, farmers, and consumers alike.
