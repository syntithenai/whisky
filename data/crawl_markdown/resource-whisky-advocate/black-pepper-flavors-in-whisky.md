# How to Identify Black Pepper Flavors in Whisky

The article explains that black‑pepper notes are common in whisky and traces their origin to the compound **rotundone**. It discusses how rotundone is extracted from oak during maturation, its presence in various whiskies (Talisker, Bulleit, rye, Irish single pot), and factors that influence its concentration such as mashbill, barrel type, aging temperature, and humidity.

It also lists a few example expressions with peppery characteristics:
- Talisker 8‑year‑old Tidal Churn – peat smoke, peppercorn, oyster shell
- Maker’s Mark Star Hill Farm wheat (2025) – brown sugar, chocolate chip cookies, burnt wood, citrus, fruit tart, prickly pepper
- Bulleit Bourbon – maple syrup, pecan, nutmeg, black currant jelly, root beer, pepper

The piece ends with a reminder that rotundone is the science behind the peppery punch in whisky.
