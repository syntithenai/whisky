# American Single Malt Whiskey Commission

## Key Facts
- TTB officially added **American Single Malt Whiskey** to the Code of Federal Regulations on December 18th, 2024 with an effective date of January 19th, 2025.
- New Standard of Identity defines the category and protects producers' products.
- Production standards:
  - Made from 100% malted barley
  - Distilled entirely at one distillery
  - Mashed, distilled, and matured in the USA
  - Matured in oak casks not exceeding 700 liters
  - Distilled to no more than 160 (U.S.) proof (80% ABV)
  - Bottled at 80 (U.S.) proof or more (40% ABV)

## Production Signals
- Strict adherence to the new Standard of Identity ensures consistency and quality across American Single Malt Whiskey producers.
- Distilleries must maintain strict control over production processes, from barley selection to maturation in specified oak casks.

## Commercial Signals
- The definition provides clarity for consumers, allowing them to confidently identify genuine American Single Malt Whiskey products.
- Increased marketability and brand protection for distillers who adhere to the new regulations.
- Potential for global recognition and expansion of the American Single Malt category.

## Risks / Unknowns
- Ensuring compliance with the new Standard of Identity across all distilleries may present challenges, particularly for smaller producers.
- Ongoing education and awareness efforts will be necessary to inform consumers about the new category and its significance.
- Potential regulatory changes or revisions in the future could impact the definition and production standards.
