# Drum Key Spirits 'The Debut' Whiskey Quickie Review

## Overview
- **Producer**: Jason from The Mash & Drum
- **Spirit**: Drum Key Spirits 'The Debut'
- **ABV**: 123 proof (61.5% alcohol by volume)
- **Barrel Type**: Green River barrels
- **Age**: 7 to 9 years old

## Flavor Profile
- Toasted oak notes
- Brown sugar undertones reminiscent of an old fashioned cocktail
- Apricot and citrus hints
- Strawberry shortcake flavors

## Tasting Notes
- The bourbon delivers a balanced blend of toasted oak, sweet brown sugar, and fruity apricot/citrus notes with subtle strawberry shortcake accents.
- Despite the high proof (123), the flavor profile is well-integrated without overwhelming intensity.
- Suitable for sipping neat or in cocktails due to its robust yet approachable character.

## Production Signals
- **ABV**: High at 123 proof, indicating a potent spirit
- **Barrel Type**: Green River barrels suggest unique aging characteristics
- **Age Statement**: 7 to 9 years old, providing depth without excessive maturation

## Commercial Signals
- First release from Drum Key Spirits, positioning itself as a bold entry in the bourbon market
- Potential for niche appeal among whiskey enthusiasts seeking high-proof expressions

## Risks / Unknowns
- High proof may limit broad consumer adoption initially
- Age range (7-9 years) might not satisfy collectors preferring longer-aged spirits
- Market competition from established brands and premium offerings
