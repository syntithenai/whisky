# Bourbon 101 - BOURBON PURSUIT

**Key Facts:**
- Bourbon is a type of American whiskey.
- Distillation, aging, and production processes significantly influence bourbon's flavor profile.
- Recent legislative changes (HB415) aim to reduce the three-tier system, allowing direct sales from distilleries.
- Research into wood science (char levels, stave seasoning) impacts bourbon flavor development.

**Production Signals:**
- Char levels in barrels affect flavor compounds and aging processes.
- Stave seasoning and selection play crucial roles in barrel construction.
- New technologies allow for precise control over aging conditions.

**Commercial Signals:**
- Increased interest in single-barrel and limited-edition releases.
- Growing demand for transparency in bourbon production methods.
- Expansion of distilleries beyond Kentucky to other states.

**Risks/Unknowns:**
- Regulatory changes may impact distribution models.
- Environmental factors affecting wood sourcing and aging timelines.
- Consumer acceptance of non-traditional barrel types (e.g., wine casks).
