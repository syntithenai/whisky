# HB 415 Summary

House Bill 415 (HB 415) modernises Kentucky’s direct‑to‑consumer (DTC) shipping rules for alcohol producers, replacing the older HB 400 framework that required in‑person sales. The bill allows distilleries, wineries and breweries to ship directly to consumers both within Kentucky and to reciprocal states, subject to a $100 annual license, record‑keeping, ID checks at sale and delivery, and use of licensed common carriers (UPS/FedEx). Quantity limits are 10 L spirits, 10 cases wine or beer per consumer per month. Reciprocal states as of March 2020 include Alaska, Arizona, Connecticut, Hawaii, Kentucky, Nebraska, Nevada, New Hampshire, North Dakota, Rhode Island and Washington D.C.

Key points:
- Only producers may ship; retailers and wholesalers lose DTC privileges.
- Licenses are required for both in‑state and out‑of‑state producers.
- Taxes are collected at the destination state; Kentucky imposes no tax on outbound shipments but requires inbound shipments to pay local taxes, excise, sales and an 11 % wholesale‑tax equivalent.
- The three‑tier system remains intact for all other sales.

The bill is expected to take effect mid‑July 2020, pending regulatory finalisation by the Kentucky Department of Alcohol Beverage Control (ABC) and Department of Revenue.
