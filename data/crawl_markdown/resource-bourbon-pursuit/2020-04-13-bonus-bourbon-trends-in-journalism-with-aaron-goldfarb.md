# Bonus Podcast: Bourbon Trends in Journalism with Aaron Goldfarb

- **Host**: Kenny (Bourbon Pursuit) hosts a special COVID‑19 edition featuring whiskey journalist Aaron Goldfarb.
- **Focus**: Goldfarb’s background, how he entered journalism and the spirits world, his experiences during the pandemic, and his thoughts on current trends such as secondary‑market crackdowns, craft‑whiskey diversity, and the rise of non‑Kentucky distilleries.
- **Key Points**:
  - Goldfarb transitioned from film school to freelance writing after immersing himself in New York’s burgeoning cocktail scene.
  - He first wrote for *Esquire* (2008–09) before expanding into niche outlets like *Whisky Advocate*, *Bourbon Plus*, and *Fine Pair*.
  - The pandemic forced him to adapt his routine, balancing family life with writing while reflecting on the changing market dynamics.
  - He highlights the shift from “trophy” bottles in NYC to more accessible craft offerings such as Russell’s Reserve and Wilderness Trail.
  - Goldfarb discusses how younger distilleries outside Kentucky are producing high‑quality, relatively young whiskies that challenge old preconceptions about age.

- **Takeaway**: The episode offers insight into the evolving bourbon landscape, the role of journalism in shaping consumer perception, and the personal journey of a prominent writer navigating both industry trends and global events.
