# Founders, Presidents, and CEOs - BOURBON PURSUIT

## Key Facts
- The podcast features interviews with founders, presidents, and CEOs of various bourbon distilleries.
- Topics include journeys into whiskey, challenges faced by craft distillers, experiments with mashbills and aging processes, and the impact of low entry proof on flavor and taxes.
- Discussions cover brand building, sourcing whiskey from other distilleries, scaling businesses, and unique approaches to production like using malt or high-proof versions.

## Production Signals
- Emphasis on experimentation with mash bills (e.g., Tennessee High Malt by Chattanooga Whiskey).
- Use of different aging processes such as seasoning barrels after 4 years (Barrell Craft Spirits) and stave finishing in Woodinville (Woodinville Whiskey Co.).
- Preference for certain stills and unique barrel treatments like low entry proof contributing to flavor profiles.

## Commercial Signals
- Brands gaining recognition through partnerships with larger spirits companies (e.g., Moet Hennessy acquisition by Woodinville Whiskey Co.).
- Successful scaling of sourced whiskey brands despite market challenges.
- Focus on local community engagement and visitor experiences at distilleries.

## Risks/Unknowns
- Dependence on barrel availability due to reliance on sourcing from other distilleries.
- Regulatory changes affecting taxes based on entry proof levels.
- Variability in consumer acceptance of experimental mash bills or aging techniques.
