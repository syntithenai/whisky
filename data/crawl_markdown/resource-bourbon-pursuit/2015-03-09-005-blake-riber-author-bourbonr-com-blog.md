# 005 – How Bourbonr Got Credit for Poor Man's Pappy

Bourbon Pursuit’s March 9, 2015 episode features Blake Riber, author of **bourbonr.com**, discussing:
- His early interest in bourbon and the birth of his blog.
- The evolution from a simple review‑tracking site to a full‑blown blog and upcoming social app.
- How *Poor Man’s Pappy* (60 % Weller 12 + 40 % Weller Antique) was discovered, its similarity to older Van Winkle releases, and why it became popular in Florida.
- Practical tips for bourbon hunters in Jacksonville and the role of state liquor stores.

The episode also includes a brief commercial segment about Bourbon Pursuit’s own products and services.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. **Blake Riber** started bourbonr.com in 2013, growing it into one of the largest bourbon blogs.
2. The new app will add social features to the existing review‑tracking platform.
3. *Poor Man’s Pappy* is a blend that mimics older Van Winkle styles and has become a go‑to for budget‑conscious collectors.
4. Jacksonville offers decent access to mainstream bourbons, but rare releases still require luck or lottery.
