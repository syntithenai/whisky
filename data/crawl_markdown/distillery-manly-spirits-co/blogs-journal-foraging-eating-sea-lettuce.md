# Foraging + Eating Sea Lettuce

Manly Spirits Co. explains how to safely forage, harvest and prepare sea lettuce (Ulva lactuca) in New South Wales. The article covers:
- Legal status: only sea lettuce can be foraged live from rocks; other seaweeds are restricted.
- Where and when to find it: inter‑tidal zones, low tide, use a sharp knife and avoid damaging the holdfast.
- Environmental considerations: high nutrient waters near outfalls or river mouths encourage growth but may carry contaminants.
- Cleaning and storage: rinse thoroughly, dry, optionally dehydrate.
- Culinary uses: fresh salads, blanched greens, soups, dried sprinkle with sesame salt, homemade “faux nori” sheets.

The piece ends with a reminder to forage responsibly and enjoy the local ecosystem.

---

## Key Take‑aways
1. **Legal** – Only sea lettuce is permitted for live harvesting in NSW.
2. **Harvesting** – Low tide, use a knife, preserve holdfasts.
3. **Safety** – Check water quality near outfalls or urban areas.
4. **Preparation** – Rinse, dry, optionally dehydrate.
5. **Recipes** – Sesame‑salt sprinkle, faux nori, salads, soups.
6. **Mindful foraging** – Respect limits and observe the environment.
