# Lemon Myrtle – Gin Botanical
Lemon Myrtle (Backhousia citriodora) is a native Australian shrub prized for its fresh, creamy lemon‑lime fragrance. The leaves are harvested, cooled and dried to preserve essential oils before being ground for use in gin production.

## Key facts
- **Botanical name**: Backhousia citriodora
- **Habitat**: Wet coastal areas of northern NSW & southern QLD
- **Appearance**: Up to 3 m tall, soft green leaves, cream‑feathery flowers in autumn
- **Flavor profile**: Creamy lemon and lime aroma, versatile and refreshing
- **Storage**: Cool dried leaves, ground and kept cool/dry
