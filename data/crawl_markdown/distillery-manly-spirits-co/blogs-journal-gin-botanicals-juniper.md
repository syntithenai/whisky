# Juniper – The Core Botanical of Gin

Manly Spirits Co. explains why juniper is the defining flavour in gin, its botanical characteristics, cultivation, historical uses and key aroma compounds.

- **Botanical role**: Juniper must dominate the flavour profile for a spirit to be legally classified as gin.
- **Plant traits**: Evergreen shrub, 50‑67 species worldwide, drought tolerant, slow growth (≈28 cm/yr). Berries mature over ~18 months and are harvested dried.
- **Historical uses**: From Roman spice substitute to medieval medicine, ritual purification, and even as a fuel for illicit distillation.
- **Aroma chemistry**: Dominated by alpha‑pinene (~40‑45 %), sabinene (5‑15 %), limonene (4‑5 %) and other terpenes such as farnesene and borneol.
- **Modern relevance**: Distilleries source juniper from different terroirs to create subtle flavour variations; the article cites Origin gin’s six terroir‑based variants.
