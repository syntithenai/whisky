# What Is Gin & How Is Gin Made?

Manly Spirits Co., a Sydney‑based craft distillery, explains the fundamentals of gin production—from sourcing Australian wheat spirit to selecting botanicals and scaling from small stills to their 600 L Holstein still “Bella.” The article highlights the importance of juniper berries, traditional London Dry ingredients (coriander, angelica), and modern native botanicals. It outlines the one‑shot distillation process, cut terminology (heads, hearts, tails), ABV ranges, and regulatory criteria for London Dry gin. The piece also promotes their Gin School experience and distillery tours.

---

## Key Takeaways
- **Base spirit**: Australian wheat grain spirit; barley used for whisky.
- **Botanicals**: Juniper mandatory; coriander, angelica common; native marine botanicals added.
- **Still**: 600 L Holstein “Bella” (named after distillery founders’ daughter).
- **Distillation method**: One‑shot; cuts of heads/hearts/tails.
- **ABV**: Typically 37.5–50%, most around 41%.
- **London Dry criteria**: Single shot, no additives, minimal sweetener.
- **Learning opportunities**: Gin School hands‑on distillation and tours.
