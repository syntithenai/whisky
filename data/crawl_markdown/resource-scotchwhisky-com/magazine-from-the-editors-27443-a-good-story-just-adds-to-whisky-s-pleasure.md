# A good story just adds to whisky’s pleasure

Richard Woodard discusses how narratives—whether a distillery’s history or personal memories—enhance the tasting experience at events like The Whisky Exchange Whisky Show. He contrasts blind tasting with real‑world choices, noting that preconceptions can colour perception but stories rooted in authenticity add depth without compromising quality.

## Key points
- Event context: The Whisky Exchange Whisky Show at Old Billingsgate.
- Blind vs. informed tasting: preconceptions influence enjoyment.
- Authentic storytelling (e.g., Cardhu’s Cumming family, Bruichladdich Bere Barley) enriches the experience.
- Quality ultimately judged by aroma, flavour, texture, balance and complexity.
