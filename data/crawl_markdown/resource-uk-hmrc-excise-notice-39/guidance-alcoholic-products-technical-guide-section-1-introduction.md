# Alcoholic Products Technical Guide – Section 1 Introduction

This section of the UK HMRC guidance explains the legal framework for producing, storing, and accounting for duty on alcoholic products. It replaces several older Excise Notices (226, 39, 163, 162) and is effective from 1 February 2025.

Key points:
- **Scope**: Covers all commercial producers, importers and exporters of alcoholic beverages.
- **Relevant legislation**: Finance (No.2) Act 2023; Alcoholic Products (Excise Duty) Regulations 2023; Customs and Excise Management Act 1979; Revenue Traders (Accounts and Records) Regulations 1992; Excise Goods (Drawback) Regulations 1995; Excise Goods (Holding, Movement and Duty Point) Regulations 2010.
- **Product‑specific rules**: Additional requirements apply to spirits with protected geographical indications such as Scotch Whisky, Irish Whiskey, etc.
- **Online services**: From 1 February 2025 producers can apply for an Alcoholic Products Producer Approval (APPA) online; from 1 March 2025 duty returns and payments are also available online.
- **Responsibilities**: Producers must secure premises approval, maintain physical security, keep accurate records, submit timely returns, and register under the Alcohol Wholesaler Registration Scheme if selling to other businesses.
- **Enforcement**: Non‑compliance can lead to assessments, civil penalties (see Excise Notice 209), seizure of goods or equipment, and appeals procedures outlined in Section 28.
