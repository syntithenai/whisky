# Attendance Allowance Overview

Attendance Allowance is a UK benefit that helps people with severe disabilities or health conditions who need personal support. It provides weekly payments of **£76.70** (basic) or **£114.60** (enhanced), depending on the level of care required. The allowance does not cover mobility needs and can be claimed even if no carer is present.

Key points:
- Eligibility: physical/mental disability or health condition, aged State Pension age or older.
- No requirement for a carer; carers may claim Carer’s Allowance separately.
- Moving between England/Wales and Scotland requires switching to the appropriate Scottish benefit (Pension Age Disability Payment).
- Attendance Allowance can be combined with other benefits such as Pension Credit, Housing Benefit, or Council Tax Reduction.
