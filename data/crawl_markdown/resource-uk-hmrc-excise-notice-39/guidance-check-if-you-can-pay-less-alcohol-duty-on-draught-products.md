# Check if you can pay less Alcohol Duty on draught alcoholic products
This GOV.UK guidance explains when producers, excise warehousekeepers and importers can claim a reduced alcohol duty rate for draught beverages with <8.5 % ABV. It details eligible product types, container requirements (≥20 L, pump or gas‑pressurised tap), repackaging rules, evidence needed for takeaway sales, and the current duty rates per litre of pure alcohol.

## Key Points
- **Eligibility**: Producers, excise warehousekeepers, importers of beer, cider, wine, spirits, RTDs, and other fermented products <8.5 % ABV.
- **Containers**: Must hold ≥20 L and connect to a pump or gas‑pressurised tap.
- **Repackaging**: Allowed only if duty already paid at full rate; otherwise penalties apply.
- **Duty Rates (2026)**:
  - All <3.5 % ABV: £8.58/litre
  - 3.5–<8.5 % ABV: £19.45/litre
  - Sparkling cider 3.5–5.5 %: £8.95/litre
  - Sparkling cider >5.5 %: £19.45/litre
- **Takeaway Sales**: Must declare full‑rate duty paid and provide evidence.
