# Excise Notice 39 - Spirits Production in the UK

## Key Facts
- Regulates spirits production, including distillation and maturation processes
- Sets standards for quality control and labeling requirements
- Governs excise duties on spirits manufacturing
- Ensures compliance with health and safety regulations

## Production Signals
- Distilleries must adhere to strict batch recording and reporting procedures
- Regular inspections of stills and fermentation equipment
- Compliance with minimum maturation periods (3 years for grain, 4 years for malted whisky)
- Use of approved spirits containers and storage facilities

## Commercial Signals
- Excise duty rates vary based on alcohol content and packaging type
- Licensing requirements for distilleries and retailers
- Reporting obligations for production volumes and sales
- Anti-fraud measures to prevent illicit distillation activities

## Risks / Unknowns
- Potential changes in government policy affecting excise duties or licensing
- Environmental regulations impacting distillery operations
- Supply chain disruptions affecting raw material availability
- Competition from imported spirits brands
