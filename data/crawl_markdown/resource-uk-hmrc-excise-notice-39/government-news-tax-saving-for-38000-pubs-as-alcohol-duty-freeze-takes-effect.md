# Tax Saving for 38,000 Pubs

The UK government has announced a six‑month freeze on alcohol duty from **1 Feb 2024 to 1 Aug 2024**. The measure is part of a broader support package that includes £4.3 billion in business rates cuts and duty protection for pints sold in pubs. The freeze will benefit roughly **38,175 pubs**, helping them cope with rising costs while the government continues to curb inflation.

Key points:
- Duty freeze follows major 2023 reforms that simplified alcohol duties.
- Savings include up to £0.03 on a pint of beer, £0.02 on cider, £0.04 on whisky and £0.18 on wine.
- The package also extends business rates relief for retail, hospitality and leisure properties.

Stakeholders such as Diageo Great Britain and the Society of Independent Brewers welcomed the decision, citing support for the home‑grown sector and certainty for brewers.
