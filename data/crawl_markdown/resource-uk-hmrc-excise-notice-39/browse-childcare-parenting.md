# Excise Notice 39 - Spirits Production in the UK

## Key Facts
- Regulates spirits production, including distillation and maturation processes for whisky and other spirits.
- Sets standards for quality control, labeling requirements, and taxation of spirits sold within the UK.
- Ensures compliance with EU regulations governing alcohol content and proof levels.

## Production Signals
- Distilleries must adhere to strict batch recording and documentation procedures during distillation.
- Proof spirit production requires precise measurement and adherence to maximum allowable strengths (e.g., 96% ABV for whisky).
- Maturation periods vary by style, with single malts typically aged longer than blended whiskies.

## Commercial Signals
- Brand positioning heavily influenced by compliance with Excise Notice requirements (e.g., '100% Scottish Malt Whisky' labeling).
- Pricing strategies often reflect excise duty costs and market demand for premium vs. value spirits.
- Export markets scrutinize adherence to UK regulatory standards when importing spirits.

## Risks / Unknowns
- Potential changes in taxation or regulation could impact profit margins for distillers.
- Supply chain disruptions affecting raw materials (e.g., barley, grains) may affect production timelines.
- Increasing consumer demand for transparency regarding sourcing and sustainability practices.
