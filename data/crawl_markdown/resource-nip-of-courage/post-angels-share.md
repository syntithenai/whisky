# Distilling the Angels’ Share

The article explains why whisky (and other spirits) lose volume during barrel ageing, a phenomenon known as the **Angels’ Share**. It describes how temperature and humidity cause barrels to “breathe”, pulling spirit into wood fibres and evaporating it as vapour. In Australia, high heat and humidity mean losses of 6‑10 % per year, compared with 2‑7 % elsewhere; over a typical ageing period this can amount to 20 % or more of the original spirit.

The piece encourages readers to appreciate the spirit that is lost to the “angels” when enjoying Australian craft spirits.
