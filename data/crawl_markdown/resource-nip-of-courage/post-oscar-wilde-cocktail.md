# How To Make An Oscar Wilde Cocktail

A quick guide to a cocktail inspired by Oscar Wilde, featuring absinthe and Champagne as the core ingredients. The recipe was created by bartender Chris Edwards at Brighton’s Handbury Club.

**Ingredients**
- 5 ml Head Heart and Tales Jezebel Absinthe (to rinse)
- 30 ml Australian gin (recommended Karu Affinity Gin)
- 1 sugar cube soaked in 4 dashes Peychaud’s bitters
- Champagne or high‑quality Australian sparkling wine to top up

**Method**
1. Rinse a champagne flute with absinthe and discard.
2. Place the bitters‑soaked sugar cube at the bottom of the glass.
3. Add gin, then fill with Champagne.
4. Serve immediately.

The result is an aromatic, bubbly drink that balances the aniseed notes of absinthe with the floral, herbaceous character of Peychaud’s bitters and a fruity, nutty gin base.
