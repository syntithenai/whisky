# Oscar Wilde Cocktail

A quick recipe for a celebratory drink that blends **absinthe**, **gin**, **Peychaud’s bitters** and **champagne**. The cocktail was created by bartender Chris Edwards at the Handbury Club in Brighton, UK.

1. Rinse a flute with 5 ml of Head, Heart & Tales Jezebel Absinthe and discard.
2. Soak one sugar cube in Peychaud’s bitters; place it in the flute.
3. Add 30 ml of Australian gin (recommended Karu Affinity Gin) to cover the sugar.
4. Top up with quality Australian sparkling wine.

The result is a bubbly, anise‑aroma cocktail that honours Oscar Wilde’s love for absinthe and champagne.

---

## Key Ingredients
- **Absinthe** – 5 ml (Head, Heart & Tales Jezebel)
- **Gin** – 30 ml (Karu Affinity Gin recommended)
- **Peychaud’s bitters** – 4 dashes
- **Sugar cube** – 1
- **Champagne / sparkling wine** – to top up

---

## Notes
- The absinthe rinse gives aniseed aroma.
- Use a fruity, nutty gin; citrus‑forward gins are not ideal.
- The sugar and bitters create a subtle sweetness and floral note.
- Champagne bubbles react with the sugar for extra effervescence.
