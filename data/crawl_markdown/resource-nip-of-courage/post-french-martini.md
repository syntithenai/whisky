# How To Make A (Not Very) French Martini

This article explains how to craft an Australian‑style French Martini using local spirits: **Underground Spirits vodka** and **Rhubi Mistelle**, a rhubarb mistelle from a Victorian distillery. The recipe calls for 60 ml vodka, 45 ml pineapple juice, 20 ml Rhubi Mistelle, and 15 ml lemon juice, shaken with ice, double‑strained into a coupe glass, and garnished with a lemon twist. The author notes that the cocktail is less sweet than the classic version (which uses Chambord) and highlights the botanical complexity of Rhubi Mistelle.

The post also provides background on Chambord’s origins, the history of mistelle, and a brief review of Rhubi Mistelle.

> *“Using excellent Aussie craft spirits, we’ve turned a staid, overly sweet cocktail of the flaming ’80s into something sophisticated, nuanced and surprising.”*

---

## Key Points
- **Ingredients**: 60 ml Underground Spirits vodka, 45 ml pineapple juice, 20 ml Rhubi Mistelle, 15 ml lemon juice.
- **Method**: Shake with ice for 30 s, double‑strain into a chilled coupe, garnish with lemon peel.
- **Flavor profile**: Less sweet, more botanical and slightly bitter; lemon balances bitterness.
- **Background**: Chambord is French but the cocktail was invented in New York. Rhubi Mistelle revives an old French distillation technique using rhubarb.
