# FSANZ Subscriptions

## Key Facts
- **Food Standards Australia New Zealand (FSANZ)** is an independent statutory authority responsible for administering the Food Act 1984 and associated regulations.
- The agency develops, maintains and administers food standards to ensure safe and wholesome food in Australia and New Zealand.
- FSANZ provides subscriptions for access to publications, guidance documents, and regulatory updates related to food safety.

## Production Signals
- **Food recalls** are monitored through the Food Recall Alerts service, which tracks incidents involving unsafe or non-compliant products.
- The **Food Industry Recall Protocol** outlines steps for recalling food products from retailers and consumers.
- **State and territory food recall action officers** coordinate local responses to food safety issues.

## Commercial Signals
- FSANZ engages with the **food industry** through consultation processes, such as reviewing labelling requirements and nutrient profiling systems.
- The agency assesses **risk assessments** for imported foods, particularly focusing on allergens and novel foods.
- **Nutrient Profiling Scoring Criterion** is used to evaluate the nutritional quality of food products.

## Risks / Unknowns
- Emerging risks include **foodborne pathogens** like Listeria monocytogenes in ready-to-eat foods.
- The safety of **novel foods** and genetically modified organisms (GMOs) remains under continuous review.
- Ensuring compliance with **allergen labelling regulations** is an ongoing challenge for the food industry.
