# Japan National Tax Agency – Whisky Labelling Standards (PDF 0023006-036)

This document outlines the Japanese tax regulations concerning whisky labeling and the requirements for obtaining a **“4/2”** classification (the “four‑two” system). It explains how the classification is applied after the distillery’s closure, the necessity of filing an application, and the financial implications such as taxes and penalties. The text also provides statistical data on the number of applications, cases of non‑compliance, and associated tax amounts for recent years.

Key points:
- **4/2 classification**: After a distillery ceases operations, its whisky can be sold under this classification if the buyer meets specific criteria.
- **Application requirement**: A formal application is mandatory to obtain the 4/2 status; failure to apply leads to penalties.
- **Financial impact**: The document lists tax amounts (e.g., 14 billion yen in 2023) and penalties for non‑compliance.
- **Statistical data**: Numbers of applications, cases of non‑application, and related tax figures are provided for the years 2022–2024.
