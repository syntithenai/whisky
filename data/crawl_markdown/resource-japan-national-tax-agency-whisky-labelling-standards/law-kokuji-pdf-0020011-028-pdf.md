# Japan National Tax Agency – Whisky Labelling Standards (PDF 0020011-028)

This PDF contains a section of Japanese tax law that governs the use of electronic information‑processing technology for the notification and management of whisky labelling. It outlines specific provisions relating to:

* The application of electronic communication systems in the administration of whisky‑related taxes.
* Rules for identifying individuals who are subject to special licence usage, based on their personal identification numbers.
* Procedures for electronic calculation devices used by tax officials.
* Requirements for electronic information‑processing units and how they interact with tax officials’ computers.

The text is largely legalese in Japanese; the English translation below captures the key points.

## Key Legal Provisions (English Translation)
1. **Use of Electronic Communication Technology** – Tax officials may use electronic communication technology to advance whisky‑related tax notifications and related procedures.
2. **Identification of Special Licence Holders** – The law defines how to identify individuals who hold special licences, using their personal identification numbers.
3. **Electronic Calculation Devices** – Tax officials must employ electronic calculation devices that can communicate with the national tax system via telephone lines for accurate calculations.
4. **Electronic Information‑Processing Units** – These units are required for handling electronic information processing related to whisky labelling and must be compatible with the official computer systems.

The provisions reference specific articles (e.g., Article 2, Item 6; Article 3; Article 8; Article 10) of the Japanese tax law.

---

## Practical Implications for Whisky Producers
* **Compliance** – Producers must ensure that their labelling data can be transmitted electronically to the National Tax Agency (NTA).
* **Licensing** – Individuals responsible for handling licence information must have valid personal identification numbers and follow the prescribed electronic procedures.
* **Technology Requirements** – Companies should use approved electronic calculation devices and maintain compatible communication lines with NTA systems.
