# The Whisky Club's Guide to Whisky Glassware

This article explains how different glass shapes affect the tasting experience of whisky. It reviews common types—Glencairn, tumbler, tulip, snifter (brandy balloon), and neat glass—highlighting their pros and cons for aroma concentration, pouring, and ice use. The guide also offers practical tips such as tilting glasses at 45°, using a straw to alter flavour perception, and experimenting with the same dram in multiple vessels.

The article concludes with FAQs that reinforce the importance of glass shape, recommend the Glencairn for nosing, and note that the club’s tasting panel uses its own crystal‑cut Glencairn glasses.
