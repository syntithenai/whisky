# The Whisky Club's Guide to Whisky Glassware

This article explains how different glass shapes affect the tasting experience of whisky. It reviews common types—Glencairn, tumbler, tulip, snifter (brandy balloon), and neat glass—highlighting their pros and cons for nosing, aroma concentration, and ice use. The guide also offers practical tips such as tilting the glass at 45°, using a straw to alter flavour perception, and experimenting with multiple glasses.

Key takeaways:
- **Glencairn** is the gold‑standard for tasting due to its tapered lip and wide base.
- **Tumblers** are best for cocktails or whisky on the rocks but lose aroma concentration.
- **Tulip** mirrors Glencairn’s nosing benefits while keeping hands away from the rim.
- **Snifter** is stylish but can overwhelm aromas with volatile vapours.
- **Neat glass** steers harsh vapours away, though it isn’t as efficient for nosing.

The article also answers FAQs about glass choice and offers a brief call‑to‑action to join the club.
