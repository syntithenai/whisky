# How Whisky Is Made
A step‑by‑step guide that walks through the eight core stages of whisky production – from malting to bottling – and explains how each decision shapes flavour, colour and character. The article also links to deeper resources on ingredients and casks.

## Key Takeaways
- **Malting** activates enzymes in barley via steeping, germination and kilning (often with peat). 
- **Milling** turns dried grain into grist for mashing. 
- **Mashing** extracts sugars to produce wort; temperature control is critical. 
- **Fermentation** converts wort sugars to wash (~5‑10 % ABV) using yeast, time and vessel material that influence taste.
- **Distillation** concentrates the wash in copper pot or column stills (20–95 % ABV). Still shape affects spirit profile.
- **Maturation** requires ageing in oak casks; wood type, previous contents, size and duration create complexity.
- **Blending** mixes casks to achieve consistency or new styles.
- **Bottling** dilutes to final strength, filters, and packages for distribution.

The article also answers FAQs on pot vs. column stills, wort, minimum ageing, angels’ share, and more.
