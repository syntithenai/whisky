# Pairing Japanese Whisky and Food

The article is a concise guide to pairing Japanese whisky with food, emphasizing balance and personal preference. It explains that neither the whisky nor the dish should dominate; instead they should either complement or contrast each other. High‑fat foods pair well with cask‑strength whiskies because fat softens alcohol bite. Garlic‑heavy or overly spicy dishes are discouraged as they can overpower the spirit.

The piece quotes Fuji Gotemba’s Master Blender Jota Tanaka: *“Pairing is a matter of preference.”* It also highlights Fuji Gotemba’s upcoming 50th‑anniversary release, the **Fuji Single Malt**, and invites readers to join The Whisky Club for exclusive access. Finally, it showcases member reviews that praise various whiskies, underscoring the club’s community focus.

## Key Takeaways
- Balance is essential: whisky and food should enhance or contrast without one dominating.
