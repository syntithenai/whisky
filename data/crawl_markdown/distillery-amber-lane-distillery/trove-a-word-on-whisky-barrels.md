# A Word on Whisky Barrels

Amber Lane Distillery explains why single‑malt whisky benefits from seasoned casks rather than new oak. They favour used bourbon barrels for their low oak activity and vanilla‑bourbon synergy, and sherry casks (Pedro Ximénez & Oloroso) for exotic dried‑fruit and chocolate notes. The distillery experiments with blending these two styles in its core releases—Destiny, Liquid Amber, and Amberosia—to achieve a balanced, evolving flavour profile.

- **Bourbon barrels**: 200 L, low oak activity, 10–15 kg of bourbon dry‑soaked; impart vanilla‑bourbon flavours.
- **Sherry casks**: 250–300 L, 30‑year old from Fernando de Castilla (Jerez); deliver Christmas‑pudding, dried fruit, dark chocolate notes.
- **Core releases**:
  - *Destiny*: balanced blend of both styles.
  - *Liquid Amber*: almost entirely sherry cask with a touch of bourbon.
  - *Amberosia*: mostly bourbon cask with a hint of sherry.

The author, Rod Berry, is the distiller and co‑founder.
