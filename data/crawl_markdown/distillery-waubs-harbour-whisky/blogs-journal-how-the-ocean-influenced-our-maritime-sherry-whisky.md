# How the Ocean Influenced Our Maritime Sherry Whisky

Head Distiller Rob explains how Tasmania’s coastal climate shapes Waubs Harbour’s limited‑release Maritime Sherry whisky. Temperature, humidity and pressure changes in the bond store affect barrel maturation, sometimes producing a “weepy” barrel or rusting on the top of the rings due to salt mist. The ocean buffers temperature swings, creates pressure build‑up that pushes spirit into oak, and ultimately gives the spirit higher ABV and a distinct character compared with distilleries inland.

---

**Key points**
- Coastal climate: warm northerly winds buffered by sea
- Pressure changes influence barrel behaviour (weepy barrels)
- Salt mist causes rusting on top of barrel rings
- Higher ABVs and unique oak‑interaction due to pressure build‑up
- Maritime Sherry limited release
