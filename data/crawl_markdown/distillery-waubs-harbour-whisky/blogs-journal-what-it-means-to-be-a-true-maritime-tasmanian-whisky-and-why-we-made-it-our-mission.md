# A Truly Maritime Australian Whisky

Waubs Harbour Distillery explains why a Tasmanian single‑malt whisky should reflect its maritime environment. The article describes how the salty, crisp coastal air subtly influences cask character without making the spirit salty, likening it to salt enhancing a roasted potato’s flavor. It highlights the distillery’s commitment to local terroir, water source, barley, yeast and craftsmanship while celebrating Tasmania’s growing reputation in the global whisky scene.

## Source Excerpts
- If you’re a whisky aficionado like ourselves, you’ll know that Tasmanian single malt whisky is exploding onto the international whisky scene.
- So, Tasmanian single malt has proven itself worthy but what does it mean to be a truly maritime Tasmanian whisky?
- One that’s been set to rest right on the edge of the ocean (the enviable view just a bonus), so close that you can smell, taste and touch the salt that rusts the rings of the cask.
- Adding salt doesn’t make the potato salty, it enhances and extracts the beautiful, natural flavour.
- We believe the maritime environment has a similar effect on the cask.
- A cask is a living, breathing thing and as it breathes in and out, the coastal, salty air penetrates the cask and the spirit inside, drawing out its unique and rich character.
