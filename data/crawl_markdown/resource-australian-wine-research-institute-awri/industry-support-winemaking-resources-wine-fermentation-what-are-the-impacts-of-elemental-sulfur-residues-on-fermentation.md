# Impact of Elemental Sulfur Residues on Wine Fermentation

The Australian Wine Research Institute (AWRI) explains that residual elemental or crystalline sulfur applied to grapevines can persist in the must if harvested within a thirty‑day withholding period (WHP). These residues may convert into off‑flavours such as hydrogen sulfide (H₂S) during fermentation, or oxidise to sulphur dioxide (SO₂), potentially inhibiting yeast activity and causing sluggish or stalled fermentations. Key factors influencing H₂S production are temperature, oxidation potential, and ethanol concentration. Management strategies include settling white musts, monitoring fermentation conditions, ensuring adequate yeast‑assimilable nitrogen (YAN), using low‑H₂S yeast strains, and employing sacrificial inocula to bind sulfur compounds.
