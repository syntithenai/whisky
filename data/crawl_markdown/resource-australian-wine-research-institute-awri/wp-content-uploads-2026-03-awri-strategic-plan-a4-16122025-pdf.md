# AWRI Strategic Plan 2025‑28

The Australian Wine Research Institute (AWRI) has released a three‑year strategic plan that outlines how it will support the Australian wine sector through science, innovation and partnership.

- **Vision**: A thriving Australian wine industry powered by world‑class research.
- **Core focus areas**:
  - *Listening to growers & winemakers* – market research, customer insights and engagement.
  - *Science & innovation* – maintain scientific excellence, expand collaborations, build a Catalyst Fund.
  - *Turning science into solutions* – translate research into products, services and extension activities.
  - *Organisational strength* – financial sustainability, diversified revenue streams, efficient systems.
  - *People & culture* – gender pay equity, staff development, inclusive workplace.
- **Key performance indicators**: Customer satisfaction ≥70 %, net surplus 4 % of revenue, at least one innovation translated into a new product/service per year, etc.

The plan aligns with the One Grape & Wine Sector Plan and Wine Australia’s strategic priorities, positioning AWRI as a trusted partner across all Australian wine regions.

---

## Key facts
- Founded 1955; not‑profit research institute.
- Offers lab discoveries, help desk, education, digital resources, Sustainable Winegrowing Australia program.
- Rapid response during 2019‑20 bushfires: analysed >4 600 samples, answered >1 600 queries, delivered 25 seminars.
- Strategic plan covers 2025‑28 with annual implementation plans and quarterly monitoring.

---

## Highlights
- **Strategic pillars**: Listening, Science & Innovation, Turning Science into Solutions, Strengthening Organisation, People & Culture.
- **KPIs**: Customer Satisfaction Score ≥70 %, net surplus 4 % of revenue, at least one innovation per year, etc.
- **Funding strategy**: Balanced mix (70 % discovery research, 30 % development), Catalyst Fund for independent projects.
- **Digital engagement**: Expand digital channels, interactive resources, national footprint.
- **People focus**: Gender pay gap closure, staff retention >90 %, professional development pathways.
