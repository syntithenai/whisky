# Preparation of a Yeast Starter Culture from an Agar Slope

The Australian Wine Research Institute (AWRI) provides a step‑by‑step protocol for creating a yeast starter culture from live yeast grown on agar slopes. The guide covers:
- **Yeast strain handling** – keep agar slopes sealed at 2–4 °C, discard if older than three months.
- **Starter media preparation** – 2 L of sterile, diluted juice (≈15 Brix/8 Baume) with ≤5 mg L⁻¹ free SO₂ and ≤50 mg L⁻¹ total SO₂; add 2 g L⁻¹ diammonium phosphate.
- **Inoculation** – transfer yeast from the slope using ~5 mL sterile juice, aerate at 2.5–5 % v/v/min, maintain 20–25 °C until ≈2×10⁸ cells mL⁻¹ is reached.
- **Scale‑up** – for up to 1 000 L use incremental additions of starter culture; for >1 000 L employ a dedicated propagator with 10‑fold volume increases.
- **Barrel inoculation** – inoculate 200 L barrels at 2×10⁶ cells mL⁻¹, acclimatise the culture to juice temperature (≤5 °C difference).
