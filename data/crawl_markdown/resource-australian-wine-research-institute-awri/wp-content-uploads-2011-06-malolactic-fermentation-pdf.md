# Malolactic Fermentation Fact Sheet (AWRI, Sep 2020)

- **Purpose**: Practical guidance on inoculating, conducting and monitoring malolactic fermentation (MLF) in red, some white and sparkling wines.
- **Key parameters**: temperature (18–22 °C), pH 3.3–3.5, total SO₂ <30 mg/L, alcohol <13 % v/v.
- **Inoculation**: use freeze‑dried or in‑house strains; early inoculation during primary fermentation is possible but requires careful SO₂ timing and temperature control.
- **Monitoring**: track malic → lactic acid conversion via TLC, enzymatic kits or HPLC; aim for <0.05 g/L malic acid (or ≤0.1 g/L) to consider MLF complete.
- **Effects on acidity**: each g L⁻¹ of malic acid reduces titratable acidity by 0.56 g L⁻¹ when fully converted.
- **References & contacts**: Coelho et al. 2015; Howe et al. 2018; AWRI helpdesk (08 8313 6600, helpdesk@awri.com.au).
