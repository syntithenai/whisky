# Active Dry Wine Yeast (ADWY) Rehydration

The Australian Wine Research Institute provides a concise, generic protocol for rehydrating and activating active‑dry wine yeast (Saccharomyces cerevisiae). The guide emphasizes following the manufacturer’s instructions first, then outlines equipment, materials, inoculation rates, and step‑by‑step procedures:

1. **Rehydration** – pre‑heat water to 38–40 °C, sprinkle yeast over 5–10× its weight in water, stir gently, and let stand 10–15 min.
2. **Amelioration** – cool the culture gradually (5–10 °C steps) by adding clean water or low‑SO₂ juice before inoculation.
3. **Inoculation** – add yeast to must/juice ≥15 °C; active fermentation should start within 24 h.

The page also lists equipment, recommended inoculum rates (0.25 g L⁻¹ for typical musts), notes on high‑solids or SO₂‑rich musts, and cautions about storage and clumping. References to external literature are provided.

---

## Key Takeaways
- Follow the yeast manufacturer’s instructions first.
- Use clean, chlorine‑free water at 38–40 °C for rehydration.
- Gradual cooling (amelioration) improves performance in difficult musts.
- Inoculate at ≥15 °C; monitor viability microscopically if fermentation stalls.
- Store yeast refrigerated and avoid opening sachets between vintages.
