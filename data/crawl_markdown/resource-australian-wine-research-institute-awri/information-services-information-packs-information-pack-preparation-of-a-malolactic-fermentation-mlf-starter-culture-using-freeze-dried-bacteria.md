# Information Pack – Preparation of a Malolactic Fermentation (MLF) Starter Culture Using Freeze‑Dried Bacteria

This resource from the Australian Wine Research Institute (AWRI) provides step‑by‑step guidance on creating an MLF starter culture with freeze‑dried bacteria. It includes:
- A brief introduction to MLF and its importance in winemaking.
- Practical instructions for rehydrating, culturing, and storing the freeze‑dried inoculum.
- Safety and quality control recommendations.
- References to key literature (e.g., Costello 1993).

The pack is part of AWRI’s Information Services and can be requested through their library system.

---

## Key Points
- **Purpose**: Enable winemakers to reliably initiate malolactic fermentation using a stable, freeze‑dried bacterial culture.
- **Method**: Rehydrate the bacteria in sterile water or nutrient solution, incubate under controlled conditions, and transfer to wine.
- **Quality Control**: Monitor pH, temperature, and bacterial viability; follow AWRI safety guidelines.
- **Reference**: Costello, P.J. (1993). *Induction procedures for malolactic fermentation*.
