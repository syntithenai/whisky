# Yeast Assimilable Nitrogen (YAN)

Yeast assimilable nitrogen is a key grape nutrient that influences fermentation rate, completion, and wine flavour. YAN comprises ammonia (NH₃) and free‑amino nitrogen (αAN). Typical Australian YAN ranges from 50–450 mg/L, with 100–200 mg/L common. Minimum YAN for low‑risk fermentations is ~150 mg/L in whites and ~100 mg/L in reds; higher levels (~250–350 mg/L) can promote cleaner, fruitier aromas but may increase ester taint if inorganic nitrogen (DAP) is added excessively.

**Analysis methods**: enzymatic or ion‑selective electrode for NH₃; NOPA, HPLC, or NIR for αAN. **Sampling**: clarified juice for non‑skin contact grapes, berry pulp/skin for skin‑contact varieties; avoid seeds and post‑clarification samples.

**Practical guidance**: early YAN testing allows informed nitrogen additions (inorganic DAP or organic supplements). Excessive inorganic nitrogen can raise ester taint risk. Dividing DAP doses moderates fermentation heat and residual YAN.

References include works by Bell & Henschke (2005), Coulter et al. (2008), Cowey (2022, 2008), Dukes & Butzke (1998), Ugliano et al. (2007, 2009), and Vilanova et al. (2007).
