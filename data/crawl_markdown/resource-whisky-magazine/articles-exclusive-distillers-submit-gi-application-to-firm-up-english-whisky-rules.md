# Exclusive: Distillers Submit GI Application to Firm‑Up English Whisky Rules

The newly formed **English Whisky Guild (EWG)** has lodged a geographical indication (GI) application to legally define what can be called *English whisky*. The proposal aims to:
- Ensure consistent standards while allowing innovation.
- Require UK‑origin raw materials and permit maturation in any wood, with oak not mandatory.
- Mandate at least two batch distillations in copper pot stills, tolerating a column plate on top of the pot.

The application is under review by DEFRA, with a response expected within six months. The EWG, consisting of 16 members (11 on board), seeks to distinguish English whisky from Scottish and Irish styles while adopting best practices.

Key participants include Copper Rivet Distillery, Oxford Artisan Distillery, and the English Whisky Company. The guild plans its first AGM in April to discuss public engagement and potential CEO recruitment.
