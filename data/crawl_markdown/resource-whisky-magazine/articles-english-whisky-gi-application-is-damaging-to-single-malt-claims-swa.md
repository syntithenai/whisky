# English Whisky GI Application Controversy

The **English Whisky Guild (EWG)** has opened its geographical indication (GI) application for consultation by the UK Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs (Defra). If approved, England would join Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland in having protected GI status for whisky.

- The application was drafted with 26 member distilleries and originally submitted in 2022. It outlines labelling rules: use UK cereal grain, English water, distilled in England below 94.8 % ABV, matured in wooden casks (e.g., oak), bottled at ≥40 % ABV.
- A *single malt* sub‑category requires distillation at a single English distillery using malted barley and batch copper pot stills.
- The **Scotch Whisky Association (SWA)** has criticised the proposal, arguing that its definition of “single malt” would dilute the reputation of Scotch whisky and be "damaging" to the category.
- EWG counters that the rules mirror Welsh GI standards and U.S. single‑malt whiskey definitions, allowing collaboration with local breweries and fostering innovation.

The consultation period runs until 19 May 2025. The debate centres on protecting England’s growing whisky sector while preserving the integrity of the broader UK single‑malt tradition.
