# How do you develop a whisky recipe?

Whisky Magazine explains that every distillery, whether historic or new, follows a *recipe*—a set of technical decisions that shape flavour and consistency. The article outlines the key variables: grain choice, yeast strain, fermentation time, still cuts, cask selection, maturation climate, filtration, and dilution. It stresses that recipe development often starts with the desired final product and works backward to determine ingredients and processes. A practical example is given for a light‑peated single malt with berry and stone‑fruit notes, illustrating how ABV, cask type, distillation technique, yeast choice, and peat level can be tuned.

The piece also highlights that many factors—water quality, still design, fermenter geometry—are considered but not detailed in the example. The article is written by Matt Strickland (23 Jun 2023).
