# Brora toasts its first whisky coming of age since reopening

Brora Distillery marks three years since its 2021 reopening by bottling a **44‑year‑old** single‑cask whisky, the *Brora 44 Years Old Untold Depths*. The spirit dates from 1977, was taken from a single cask and bottled at 49.1 % ABV. Only **150 bottles** are released, priced at £10 000 each, available only in person at the distillery.

The bottling celebrates Brora’s historic *age of peat* (1969‑1981) and the reintroduction of peated malt production. It follows the 2021 release *Hidden Beneath*, a 39‑year‑old whisky matured in American oak. Master blender Craig Wilson describes the nose as waxy incense, green grape skins, light peat smoke, dried herb, scented oil, buttery toffee and a finish with white pepper.

Visitors on the “Eras of Brora” tour will be able to sample this spirit from July 2024.

## Flavor profile (as described by Craig Wilson)
- Nose: waxy incense, green grape skins, light peat smoke, dried herb, scented oil
- Palate: buttery toffee, sweet, lightly fruity
- Finish: long, fragrant, with a pinch of white pepper
