# A dram with… Bill Lumsden

- Dr Bill Lumsden, former head of distilling at Glenmorangie and Ardbeg, shares his 39‑year career in whisky.
- He began during a PhD in fermentation science at Heriot‑Watt University, where he met industry figures and tasted his first single malt – *Glenmorangie 10 Years Old*.
- Lumsden highlights two major industry shifts: the rise of age‑statement‑free whiskies and the use of diverse cask finishes.
- He praises underrated distilleries such as Diageo’s Linkwood (Scotland) and Four Roses Bourbon (USA). Currently he enjoys Glenmorangie, Ardbeg and a non‑aged Mortlach from Diageo.
- Outside whisky, he prefers beer, wine, running, and the film *Once Upon a Time in America*.

The article is an interview‑style Q&A with no product reviews or pricing data.
