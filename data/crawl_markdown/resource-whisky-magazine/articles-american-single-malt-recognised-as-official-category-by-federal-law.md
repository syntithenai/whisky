# American Single Malt Recognised as Official Category by Federal Law

The Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau (TTB) has officially defined the *American single malt* whiskey category, a milestone after more than a decade of lobbying by U.S. distillers.

**Key points**
- **Fermentation**: 100 % malted barley grown in the United States.
- **Distillation**: Proof no higher than 160 (80 % ABV) and performed at a single U.S. distillery.
- **Maturation**: New or used, charred or uncharred oak barrels with a maximum capacity of 700 L; must be stored in the United States.
- **Additives**: No neutral spirits, colourings, flavourings or blending materials allowed, except for disclosed caramel colouring.
- **Straight American single malt**: Minimum two‑year age statement.

The rule will appear in the Federal Register on 18 Dec 2024 and take effect on 19 Jan 2025. The decision is seen as a win for U.S. producers, protecting authenticity and encouraging innovation.
