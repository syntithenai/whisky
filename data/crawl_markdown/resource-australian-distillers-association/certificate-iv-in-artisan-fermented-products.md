# Certificate IV in Artisan Fermented Products

## Key Facts
- **Accreditation**: Nationally accredited qualification designed in collaboration with Spirits Victoria Association and Australian Distillers Association.
- **Purpose**: Equips distillers with theory and practical skills for distilled spirits production, focusing on artisanal methods.
- **Target Audience**: Those producing fermented food or beverage products specializing in distilled spirits.

## Production Signals
- **Artisanal Methods**: Emphasis on traditional techniques alongside modern practices.
- **Quality Control**: Rigorous sensory analysis and HACCP-based safety plans are integral components.
- **Regulatory Compliance**: Strict adherence to Australian distilling industry standards and legal requirements.

## Commercial Signals
- **Skill Development**: Enhances producers' ability to select, operate equipment, and manage resources efficiently.
- **Problem Solving**: Develops skills in routine and non-routine problem-solving within the production environment.
- **Industry Standards**: Aligns with industry benchmarks for quality and safety in distilled spirits.

## Risks/Unknowns
- **Regulatory Changes**: Potential shifts in legislation could impact compliance strategies.
- **Market Adaptation**: Rapid changes in consumer preferences may require continuous adaptation of production methods.
- **Skill Gap**: Ensuring a steady supply of trained personnel to maintain quality and safety standards.
