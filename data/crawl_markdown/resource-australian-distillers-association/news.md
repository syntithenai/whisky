# Australian Distillers Association News

## Key Facts
- The ADA represents and strengthens Australia's distilling industry, consisting of over 400 distilleries and their supply chain partners.
- Membership offers opportunities to participate in shaping policy, receive valuable resources, and promote the unique contributions of Australian craft distilleries.
- The association plays a critical role in leveling regulatory playing fields and providing information and resources to support members' success.

## Production Signals
- The industry is growing significantly, with four clear priorities: Win at Home, Scale & export, Community & Advocacy, and Collaboration.
- Victorian Spirits have grown substantially over the past three years, supported by government initiatives like the Distillery Door Program.
- Regulators are moving towards a more nationally consistent Container Deposit Scheme (CDS) expansion, including a national portal, micro-supplier concept, simplified reporting, and an education-first compliance model.

## Commercial Signals
- The Australian Spirits Industry has secured a $50,000 increase to the Alcohol Manufacturers Remission (AMR), restoring its original value.
- Distillery visits by MPs have proven effective in building support for the industry, with several distilleries securing follow-up support and meetings simply by opening their doors.

## Risks/Unknowns
- The future of alcohol taxation in Australia remains uncertain, with potential impacts on excise rates and government investment in the industry.
- Navigating regulatory changes, such as the CDS expansion and harmonisation, will require ongoing advocacy and collaboration within the industry.
