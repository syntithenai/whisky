# 2023 Pre‑Budget Submission
A joint submission by Spirits & Cocktails Australia and the Australian Distillers Association to the 2023‑24 Federal Budget. The document argues that the current spirits excise regime—high rates, twice‑annual CPI indexation and a $350k remission cap—undermines domestic manufacturing, investment, exports and employment. It presents PwC modelling showing that aligning the spirits rate with the brandy rate and freezing indexation would increase government revenue while supporting industry growth.

Key points:
- 400+ distilleries in Australia; $11.6 bn added value; 52,900 jobs (5,000 manufacturing).
- High excise discourages scaling beyond craft size and deters foreign investment.
- Modest tax reform could unlock regional job creation, export potential and innovation.
- Case studies of Brown‑Forman and Granddad Jack’s illustrate the impact of excise on growth decisions.

The submission calls for:
1. Reduce spirits excise to brandy rate.
2. Freeze CPI indexation for three years.
