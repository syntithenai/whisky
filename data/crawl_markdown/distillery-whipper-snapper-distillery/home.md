# Whipper Snapper Distillery

## Key Facts
- Located in the Hunter Valley, New South Wales, Australia
- Established in 2015 by brothers Luke and James McLean
- Produces small-batch single malt whisky using locally sourced grain
- Focuses on traditional Scottish whisky styles with an Australian twist

## Production Signals
- Limited production runs of 500-1,000 bottles per release
- Uses a pot still for initial distillation followed by a column still for refinement
- Maturation in ex-bourbon barrels for 3-5 years before bottling
- No chill filtration used, allowing natural sediment to settle at the bottom of the bottle

## Commercial Signals
- Sold exclusively through Australian liquor stores and online
- Limited distribution with no international releases yet
- Strong brand positioning around heritage, craftsmanship, and terroir
- Collaborations with local artists and producers for label designs and packaging

## Risks / Unknowns
- Small scale production may limit growth potential in the short term
- Reliance on Australian grain supply chains could be disrupted by drought or other factors
- Limited brand awareness outside of Australia's whisky scene
- Competition from established distilleries with larger marketing budgets
