# Irish Whiskey - Key Facts & Overview

**Key Facts:**
- Irish whiskey is a globally recognized spirit with a rich heritage dating back centuries.
- Distilled using copper pot stills, resulting in a lighter and smoother profile compared to other whiskies.
- Traditionally triple distilled for purity and smoothness.
- Key production regions include Cork, Dublin, Kilkenny, Limerick, Tipperary, Waterford, and Wicklow.

**Production Signals:**
- Triple distillation is a hallmark of Irish whiskey, contributing to its distinctive character.
- Use of copper pot stills enhances the purity and smoothness of the spirit.
- Production volumes have remained relatively stable despite global challenges.

**Commercial Signals:**
- Strong export performance with increasing cider exports as domestic sales hold steady.
- Resilience in the face of global economic pressures, maintaining a 2 billion euro sector value.
- Growing consumer interest in premium and craft whiskey brands.

**Risks / Unknowns:**
