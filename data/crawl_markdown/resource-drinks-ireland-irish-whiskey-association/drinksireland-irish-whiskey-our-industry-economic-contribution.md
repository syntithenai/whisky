# Economic Contribution of Irish Whiskey Industry

## Overview
- Irish whiskey has become one of the leading all-island industries, contributing substantially to the shared economy with tangible impacts for communities across the island.
- Sales have grown from under five million cases in 2010 to over twelve million cases by January 2020, achieving heights not seen since the early 1900s.

## Key Facts
- **Distillery Growth**: The number of operational Irish whiskey distilleries increased from four in 2010 to 38 by the end of 2020.
- **Sales Volume**: Sales grew from less than five million cases (60 million bottles) in 2010 to over twelve million cases (144 million bottles) by January 2020.
- **Export Value**: The aggregate value of Irish whiskey exports reached €890 million in 2019, with sales in 140 markets globally.
- **Tourism Impact**: Irish whiskey tourism grew significantly, with visitor numbers to distilleries and brand homes increasing by 54% from 653,000 in 2015 to over one million in 2019.

## Production Signals
- Distillery developments have contributed to local economic regeneration and social fabric of communities throughout Ireland.
- Investments in Midleton and Tullamore, among other towns and villages, demonstrate the industry's commitment to regional growth.

## Commercial Signals
- Irish whiskey is sold in 140 markets globally, with the United States leading sales growth at 51% across the decade.
- The value of exports to the all-island economy exceeded one billion euros for the first time in 2022.

## Risks/Unknowns
- **Economic Volatility**: Global economic conditions could impact tourism and export markets.
- **Regulatory Changes**: Potential changes in trade agreements or tariffs could affect international sales.
- **Sustainability Concerns**: Increasing focus on environmental sustainability may require industry-wide adjustments.
