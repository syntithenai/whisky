# Employment at Irish Distilleries (2010-2020)

## Key Facts
- Only four operational distilleries on the island of Ireland in 2010
- Number increased to 38 by November 2020 due to investment and entrepreneurship
- Over €400 million invested by Irish Distillers in operations this decade
- Largest investments in Midleton (€400 million) and Tullamore (€100 million)
- Provincial towns like Ballina, Clonakilty, Dingle, Dundalk, and Drumshanbo saw distillery returns
- Historic estates (Slane Castle, Powerscourt Estate, Hazelwood Estate, Rademon Estate) developed new distilleries
- Dublin's Liberties re-emerged as a hub for distilling with four distilleries in 2020

## Production Signals
- Distilled over 100 million litres of pure alcohol in recent years
- Purchased large quantities of timber pallets, used American oak barrels, malt and grain, water, and casks for maturation
- Revival of independent bottling and bonding tradition

## Commercial Signals
- New distilleries located in Midleton, Tullamore, Ballina, Clonakilty, Dingle, Dundalk, Drumshanbo, Slane Castle, Powerscourt Estate, Hazelwood Estate, Rademon Estate, and Dublin's Liberties
- Partnership with local businesses to showcase food, drinks, and craft makers
- Development of company offices, bonders, maturation facilities, blending, bottling, and non-distillery brand homes

## Risks/Unknowns
- Dependence on multi-national spirits companies for investment
- Potential challenges in attracting skilled labor to rural areas
- Maintaining the vibrancy and vitality of newly developed distillery towns
