# Irish Whiskey - Key Facts and Overview

## Distillery Information
- **Distilleries**: There are currently 13 operational distilleries across Ireland producing premium single malt whiskies.
- **Key Players**: Jameson, Bushmills, Cooley, Midleton (Cooley now owned by Brown-Forman), Powers, Glenfiddich (Irish subsidiary), and others.

## Production Signals
- **Annual Output**: Irish whiskey production has seen steady growth in recent years, with a focus on quality over quantity.
- **Maturation**: Most whiskies are aged for 3-12 years before bottling, adhering to EU regulations requiring a minimum of 3 years maturation.
- **Export Growth**: Ireland's share of global whiskey exports has increased significantly, driven by premium positioning and international demand.

## Commercial Signals
- **Brand Positioning**: Irish whiskey is marketed as smooth, approachable, and high-quality, appealing to both domestic and international consumers.
- **Trends**: There is a growing trend towards craft distilling and small-batch production, with many distilleries emphasizing heritage and tradition.
- **Retail Presence**: Strong presence in duty-free markets globally, particularly in Asia-Pacific regions.

## Risks / Unknowns
- **Regulatory Environment**: Potential changes in EU regulations could impact Irish whiskey exports if not managed proactively.
- **Competition**: Increasing competition from other premium spirits and the rise of craft distilleries worldwide.
- **Supply Chain Disruptions**: Vulnerability to global supply chain issues affecting raw materials and logistics.
