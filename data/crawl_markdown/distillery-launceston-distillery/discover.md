# Discover – Launceston Distillery

Launceston Distillery is a Tasmanian single‑malt whisky producer founded in 2013 by Chris Condon, Chris Byrne, Rob Carroll, Peta Dolan and Ilya Brucksch. The distillery operates from historic Hangar 17 at Launceston Airport, using two custom copper pot stills built by Peter Bailly. Water comes from the South Esk River and barley is grown in northern Tasmania. The site celebrates Tasmania’s heritage, craft and place while reviving the legacy of the 1824 Caledonian Distillery.

Key points:
- **Founders**: five individuals with a Scottish single‑malt influence.
- **Location**: Hangar 17, Launceston Airport (1932‑1968 passenger terminal).
- **Production start**: 2015.
- **Water source**: South Esk River (plipatumila).
- **Barley**: grown locally in northern Tasmania.
- **Community**: acknowledges Tasmanian Aboriginal custodians and is a member of the Tasmanian Whisky & Spirits Association.
- **Sales**: cell‑door, wholesale partner David Johnstone & Associates, export enquiries via sales manager.
