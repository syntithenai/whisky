# Types of distillation columns – Headlands Distilling Co.

Headlands Distilling explains the two main categories of distillation columns—**batch** and **continuous**—and their suitability for different spirits (vodka, whisky, gin). It details how batch stills allow separation of heads, hearts and tails, while continuous systems offer high throughput and energy efficiency but lack that flexibility. The article then dives into column internals: bubble caps, sieve trays, valve trays, random packing (Pall Rings), structured packing, and distribution plates, explaining their advantages, drawbacks and typical use cases.

Finally, Headlands describes the specific setup used at its Wollongong distillery for Seacliff Vodka: a small continuous column with sieve‑tray stripping section, Pall‑Ring random packing above the feed, heat‑exchanger preheating, and copper mesh top. The system is PLC‑controlled, energy‑efficient, and produces nutrient‑rich bottom water for local farmers.
