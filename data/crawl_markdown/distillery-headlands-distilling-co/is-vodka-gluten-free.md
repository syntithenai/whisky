# Is Vodka Gluten Free?
Headlands Distilling Co. explains that although their vodka is made from wheat and barley, the distillation process removes gluten proteins, making the final product highly unlikely to contain detectable gluten. Australian regulations set a threshold of 1 ppm for gluten‑free labeling, while global standards allow up to 20 ppm; laboratory tests have found gluten levels far below these limits in distilled spirits.

Key points:
- Gluten is a protein that does not volatilize during distillation.
- Headlands’ grain‑to‑glass process includes milling, fermentation and distillation.
- No gluten testing has been performed on every batch, but the chemistry of production suggests negligible risk.
- Coeliac Australia and NIH state distilled alcohols are safe for people with celiac disease.
- Consumers can use home gluten detection kits or visit the distillery to observe quality controls.
