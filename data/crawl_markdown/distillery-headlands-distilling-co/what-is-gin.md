# What is gin?

Headlands Distilling Co. explains that gin is essentially a neutral spirit (over 99% vodka) infused with juniper berries and other botanicals such as coriander seed, angelica root, orris root, citrus peels, cassia, cardamom, and nutmeg. The base alcohol can come from any starch‑or‑sugar source; Headlands uses locally grown La Trobe barley. Two main extraction methods are described: maceration (steeping botanicals in spirit before distillation) and vapor infusion (passing steam through packed botanicals). The article also notes a special “Boobialla gin” that uses Australian native juniper, which technically deviates from the classic definition.

## Key points
- Gin is a neutral spirit with >99 % alcohol by volume.
- Juniper berries are mandatory; other botanicals add flavour.
- Extraction methods: maceration vs. vapor infusion.
- Headlands uses barley‑derived neutral spirit.
- Boobialla gin uses Australian native juniper.
