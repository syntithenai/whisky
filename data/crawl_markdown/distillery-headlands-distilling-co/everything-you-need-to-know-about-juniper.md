# Everything You Need to Know About Juniper
Juniper ( *Juniperus communis* ) is the core botanical of gin, providing its signature pine‑citrus flavour. The article explains:
- **Botanical background** – a coniferous shrub/tree with spherical seed cones that ripen over 2–3 years.
- **Ripening cycle** – flowers → hard green berries (year 1) → dark purple/black ripe berries (end of year 2 or year 3).
- **Flavor profile** – varies by soil, altitude and climate; key compounds include alpha‑pinene, myrcene, sabinene, limonene and beta‑pinene.
- **Historical & cultural context** – juniper used medicinally, in rituals, and as a flavouring for gin (origin of the word *gin*).
- **Australian context** – native *Boobialla* (*Myoporum insulare*) is sometimes called “juniper” but differs botanically and flavor‑wise.
