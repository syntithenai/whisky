# What is Tonic Water?

Headlands Distilling explains that tonic water is simply carbonated water infused with quinine from cinchona bark. Historically used for malaria prevention, it now mainly serves as a gin mixer. The article details how to extract quinine, the importance of balancing flavour so it doesn’t overpower gin, and why modern medical guidelines no longer rely on quinine for malaria treatment.

Key points:
- Quinine is sourced by boiling bark from the *Cinchona* tree.
- Typical tonic contains 0.05 % quinine; a 250 ml bottle holds ~125 mg of quinine.
- To reach therapeutic doses (~2 g/day) one would need to consume many bottles, illustrating why tonic isn’t an antimalarial.
- Sweet tonics use citrus sweeteners and can vary in bitterness depending on infusion timing.
