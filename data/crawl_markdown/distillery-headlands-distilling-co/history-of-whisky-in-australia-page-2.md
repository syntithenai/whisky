# History of Whisky in Australia
Headlands Distilling Co. traces the evolution of Australian whisky from the first legal distillery in 1822 to modern craft producers. Key milestones include:
- **Early pioneers**: Thomas Haigh Midwood (Sorell Distillery, Hobart), Sydney Distillery (later Glenmore), Brisbane Distillery.
- **Regulatory shifts**: 1862 Victorian Distillation Act, 1901 Distillation Act limiting still size, 1990 reforms enabling craft distilling.
- **Major players**: Milne & Co., Federal Distilleries, Corio Distillery (Diageo), Sullivans Cove, Lark Distillery, and Headlands Distilling.
- **Modern achievements**: Sullivans Cove won 2014 World Whisky Award; Headlands plans a world‑first Illawarra Plum cask whisky.

The article also lists numerous historical references and anecdotes about early distilleries, legal changes, and the decline of large‑scale production in the late 20th century.
