# Why are alcoholic spirits so expensive in Australia?

The article explains that the high cost of spirits in Australia is largely due to **excise duty**—a tax of AUD 88.91 per litre of ethanol (AUD 94.41 as of December 2022). This translates into roughly AUD 24.89 for a 700 ml, 40 % ABV bottle. Compared with other OECD countries, Australia has the highest alcohol tax rates.

Key points:
- **Tax calculation**: 0.7 L × 40 % = 0.28 L ethanol; 0.28 L × AUD 88.91 ≈ AUD 24.89 per bottle.
- **International comparison**: Iceland and Norway have similar or higher rates, but Australia remains the highest among OECD nations.
- **Tax pass‑through**: Studies show spirits like cognac and Scotch whisky absorb more of tax hikes than gin, which tends to under‑shift.
- **Market dynamics**: Competitive Australian gin market keeps prices below psychological thresholds (e.g., AUD 100).
- **Policy context**: Excise duty is a major revenue source and part of public health strategies.

The article cites OECD data, ATO rates, and academic studies to support these claims.
