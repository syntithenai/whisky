# History of Whisky in Australia

This page traces the development of Australian whisky from the first legal distillery in 1822 to modern craft producers. Key milestones include:
- **Early pioneers**: Thomas Haigh Midwood (Sorell Distillery, Hobart), Sydney Distillery (later Glenmore), Brisbane Distillery.
- **Regulatory shifts**: Victorian Distillation Act of 1862 and the 1901 Distillation Act imposed minimum still sizes that hindered small distilleries.
- **Major players**: Federal Distillery (Melbourne, 1888), Corio Distillery (Geelong, 1928) – the largest in Australia at its peak.
- **Industry decline**: Lower import duties and higher excise taxes in the 1960s–1980s led to closures such as Corio (1989).
- **Resurgence**: Bill Lark’s legal challenge to the 1901 Act enabled craft distilleries; Lark Distillery (1992), Sullivans Cove (1994) and Headlands Distilling Co. (2015) followed.
- **Innovations**: Use of non‑traditional casks – Illawarra plum, Red Gum, virgin oak – and world‑award wins for Australian single malts and rye whiskies.

The article also lists numerous historical references and anecdotes about early distilleries across Tasmania, New South Wales, Victoria and Western Australia.
