# Sustainability at Headlands Distilling Co.

Headlands Distilling Co. commits to renewable energy, waste‑heat recovery, bottle reuse, and zero single‑use plastic seals. Their continuous still design saves up to **80 %+** power, while a $5 bottle return program recycles 700 ml bottles for credit. The distillery uses aluminium or wax top seals instead of heat‑shrink film, serves samples in reusable glass beakers, and supplies spent grain husks and yeast to local farmers. Illawarra plum skins are donated to restaurants, and seeds from hand‑foraged fruit are planted to grow a native orchard.

---

## Key Points
- Renewable energy powers heating, chiller, bottling, lights, computers.
- Continuous still recovers heat, eliminating waste water in the condenser.
- $5 bottle return credit; bottles sterilised and reused.
- No single‑use plastic seals: aluminium or wax top only.
- Glass laboratory beakers for tasting cups; biodegradable paper cups for full drinks.
- Spent grain husks & yeast given to farmers; plum skins used by restaurants.
- Seeds from hand‑foraged plum fruit planted to grow an orchard.
