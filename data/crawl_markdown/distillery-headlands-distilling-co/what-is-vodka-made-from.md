# What is vodka made from?

Headlands Distilling explains that while many people think vodka comes only from potatoes, most commercial vodkas are produced from grains such as wheat and rye. The article lists several Russian brands (e.g., Russian Standard, Stolichnaya) that use wheat or a wheat‑rye blend, and notes that some Polish vodkas like Luksusowa and Chopin are potato‑based.

Vodka can be made from any source of starch or sugar plus yeast. Starch sources include wheat, rye, corn, rice, potatoes, barley, soy, sorghum; sugar sources include cane, molasses, beet, honey, maple syrup, and fruit juices. Enzymes (alpha‑amylase, glucoamylase) convert starch to fermentable sugars before yeast fermentation produces alcohol.

The author stresses that the “best” base material depends on many variables—fermentation temperature, yeast strain, ingredient quality, distillation column height—and ultimately comes down to personal preference. The post encourages readers to experiment with different bases to find their favorite vodka profile.
