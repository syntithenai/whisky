# History of Whisky in Australia
Headlands Distilling Co. chronicles the evolution of Australian whisky from the first legal distillery in 1822 to modern craft producers. Key milestones include:
- **Early pioneers**: Thomas Haigh Midwood (Sorell Distillery, Hobart), Sydney Distillery (later Glenmore), Brisbane Distillery.
- **Regulatory shifts**: Victorian Distillation Act 1862, 1901 Distillation Act, and the 1990 repeal that enabled craft distilleries.
- **Major players**: Milne & Co., Federal Distillery, Corio Distillery (now part of Diageo), Sullivans Cove, Lark Distillery.
- **Modern breakthroughs**: First Australian whisky aged in native Illawarra plum casks (Headlands) and Red Gum cask single malts (Old Kempton).
- **Current landscape**: ~300 distilleries, yet domestic whisky accounts for only ~2% of spirits consumption.
