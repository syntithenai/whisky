# 9 Best Foods to Pair with Australian Gin

Headlands Distilling Co. recommends a range of foods that complement the botanical profile of Australian gins, from creamy cheeses and fresh cucumber salads to cured salmon, native berries, and spicy meats. The article highlights local ingredients such as Illawarra plum jam, macadamia nuts, and coriander‑rich Vietnamese dishes, offering practical pairing ideas for gin cocktails or neat servings.

---

## Key Pairing Ideas
- **Cheese** – rich, fatty cheeses like vintage cheddar, camembert, or goat’s cheese smooth the spirit.
- **Cucumber** – pairs well with cucumber‑heavy gins such as Hendrick’s; use in salads or sandwiches.
- **Quince/Illawarra plum paste** – sweet spreads that enhance a cheese platter.
- **Salmon** – gin‑cured or smoked salmon, especially when combined with citrus.
- **Berries** – native muntries or common berries add fruitiness to gin and tonic.
- **Cured meats** – salami, chorizo provide saltiness and fat that coat the palate.
- **Macadamia nuts** – a native nut that complements Australian gins.
- **Coriander‑rich dishes** – Vietnamese‑style rice paper rolls or other coriander‑heavy foods bridge citrus notes.
