# Why can’t Scotch Whisky be made in Australia?

Headlands Distilling Co. explains that the name *Scotch whisky* is legally protected and may only be used for spirit produced **in Scotland** under strict technical rules. The article lists those rules, compares them with Australian regulations, and highlights notable Australian whiskies that have won international awards.

Key points:
- Geographic restriction: only Scotland can produce Scotch.
- Technical limits: max 94.8 % ABV at distillation; oak casks ≤700 L; must mature in Scotland for ≥3 years.
- Cask rules: new or ex‑wine/beer/spirits barrels, no fruit‑added casks.
- Ingredients: malted barley + yeast (other cereals allowed only in grain whiskies).
- No added enzymes beyond those from malting; only water and caramel colouring may be added.
- Bottling must be ≥40 % ABV.
- Distinct Scottish regions (Campbeltown, Islay, Lowland, Highland, Speyside) have their own protected designations.
- Australian whiskies such as *Sullivans Cove* and *Archie Rose Rye* have earned world‑level awards despite not being Scotch.

The article also points readers to the UK government technical file for full details.
