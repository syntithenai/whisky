# Illawarra Plum - Headlands Distilling Co.

## About
- Illawarra Plum (Podocarpus elatus) is a native Australian fruit also known as Daalgaal, Gidneywallum, Goongum and Plum Pine.
- The seed is on the outside of the fleshy fruit which can be eaten raw, cooked, made into jam or distilled into spirits like Headlands Distilling Co.'s Spirit of the Illawarra Plum.

## Characteristics
- Trees can grow up to 40 metres tall but take a long time to reach that size.
- Female trees produce fruit; male trees are needed for pollination. At least 3 trees should be planted together.
- The fruit is about the size of a large grape (2.5cm in diameter).

## Production Notes
- Best flavour when perfectly ripe or slightly overripe, not before.
- Freezing plums negatively impacts flavor.
- Dwarf variety podocarpus spinulosus exists with similar edible fruit.
- Different strains exist based on growing regions (Wollongong/Sydney vs. northern NSW/southern QLD).

## Spirit of the Illawarra Plum
- Handcrafted alcoholic spirit from scratch, steeped in oak barrel for 9+ months with perfectly ripe plums.
- No added sugar; sweetness comes only from the plums themselves.
