# History of Whisky in Australia

The article traces the evolution of Australian whisky from the first legal distillery in 1822 to modern craft producers. Key milestones include:
- **Early pioneers**: Thomas Haigh Midwood (Sorell Distillery, Hobart), Sydney Distillery (later Glenmore), Brisbane Distillery, and Milne & Co.
- **Legislative shifts**: The Victorian Distillation Act 1862 and the 1901 Distillation Act imposed large still‑size requirements that stifled small distilleries.
- **Major players**: Federal Distillery (Melbourne), Corio Distillery (Geelong, now part of Diageo), and United Distilleries.
- **Decline & revival**: Import duty reductions in the 1960s weakened domestic production; the industry collapsed by 1989. Bill Lark’s legal challenge to the 1901 Act enabled craft distilling from 1992 onward, leading to successes such as Sullivans Cove and Headlands Distilling.
- **Current landscape**: ~300 distilleries exist, yet Australian spirits account for only ~2 % of domestic consumption. Recent accolades (World’s Best Single Malt, World’s Best Rye) highlight the growing quality.
