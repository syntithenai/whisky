# Negroni Cocktail Recipe

A concise guide to making the classic Italian Negroni using any gin of choice, sweet red vermouth and Campari. The recipe includes a brief history of the drink’s origin in Florence (1919) and a fun fact about Campari’s former use of cochineal for colouring.

## Ingredients
- 30 mL gin
- 30 mL sweet red vermouth (vermouth rosso)
- 30 mL Campari

## Directions
1. Fill a glass with ice.
2. Add gin, vermouth and Campari.
3. Stir for 20‑30 seconds until the mixture is very cold.
4. Strain into an Old Fashioned‑style glass over a large ice cube.
5. Garnish with an orange peel or slice.

## Notes
- Any preferred gin works; Australian craft gins are highlighted.
- Campari’s colour now comes from commercial dye, not cochineal insects (used until 2006).
