## Key Points
- **Malting**: Germination, activation and drying of barley to produce malt.
- **Mashing**: Hot water converts starches into fermentable sugars; temperature profiles shape flavour.
- **Fermentation**: Yeast turns sugar into alcohol; strain, time and temperature affect character.
- **Distillation**: Pot or column stills separate foreshot, heart and after‑spirit; copper is essential for flavour.
- **Cask Ageing**: Wood type (sherry, bourbon, wine), pre‑seasoning, climate and duration influence colour and taste.
- **Bottling**: Filling level, cork/screw cap choice and hygiene standards ensure product integrity.
- **Production styles**: Differences between Scotch, Irish, Bourbon and Japanese whisky are explained with legal constraints and typical grain/still choices.
