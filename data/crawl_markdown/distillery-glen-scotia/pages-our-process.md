# Glen Scotia Whisky‑Making Process
Glen Scotia’s single‑malt production follows a traditional, hand‑crafted route:
1. **Malt arrival** – 60 t of barley weekly from Montrose.
2. **Milling** – 1956 Robert Bobby mill grinds malt to the right consistency.
3. **Mashing** – 13 Victorian‑era cast‑iron mash tuns, 5 water additions from Crosshill Loch, 8.5 h per mash.
4. **Fermentation** – 9 washbacks (6 inside, 3 outside); average 128.8 h at ~7–8 % ABV.
5. **Distillation** – Classic double copper pot stills; new‑make spirit 71 % ABV, hand‑filled into casks at 63.5 %.
6. **Maturation** – Palletised, racked or dunnage warehousing; first‑fill American oak/bourbon and various finishing casks; minimum 3 years.
7. **Tasting & bottling** – Finished whisky is aged, bottled at a sister distillery near Loch Lomond.
The result is a robust, oily malt with vanilla, toffee and subtle salt notes, celebrated in multiple awards.
