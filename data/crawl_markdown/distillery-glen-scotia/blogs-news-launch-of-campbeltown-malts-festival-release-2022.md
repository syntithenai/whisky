# Launch of Campbeltown Malts Festival Release 2022
Glen Scotia unveiled its 8‑year‑old **Festival Release 2022** to celebrate the return of the Campbeltown Malts Festival (24‑26 May). The dram is finished in Pedro Ximénez hogsheads after first‑fill bourbon barrels, delivering a balance of gentle smoke, treacle and spice with maritime notes. It is non‑chill filtered, cask strength at 56.5 % ABV and available for purchase on the Glen Scotia site.

The article also highlights:
- The festival’s significance to Campbeltown’s whisky heritage.
- Glen Scotia’s recent accolades (Best in Class 2021, Distillery of the Year 2022).
- A new exhibition by artist Alice Angus titled *Spirit Safe*.

## Source Excerpts
- In the mouth, the liquid is smooth and silky with hints of runny treacle, spicy white pepper, sweet fruits and salted toffee – a wonderful balance of spice and the sherry cask influence.
- The exquisite 8-year-old expression has been matured in heavily peated, first-fill bourbon barrels before a twelve-month finish in Pedro Ximénès hogsheads, a fortified dessert wine from southern Spain, giving it a perfect balance of gentle smoke, treacle and spice interwoven with Glen Scotia’s signature maritime notes.
- Non-chill filtered, the heavily-peated and rich, amber-coloured liquid has been bottled at cask strength, 56.5% ABV.
- Iain McAlister, Distillery Manager and Master Distiller at Glen Scotia, said: “ Our Festival Release 2022 is very special as it heralds the return of the much-loved Malts Festival and the opportunity for us to showcase our work in person once more to whisky fans from around the world.
- It is a liquid that has been crafted by our talented team and is a fantastic dram to enjoy while soaking up Campbeltown and all it has to offer.
