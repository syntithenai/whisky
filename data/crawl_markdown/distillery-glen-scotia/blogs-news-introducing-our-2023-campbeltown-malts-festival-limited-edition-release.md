# Limited‑Edition Campbeltown Malts Festival 2023

Glen Scotia launches an 11‑year‑old, lightly peated single malt finished in white‑port casks for the 2023 Campbeltown Malts Festival. The 70 cl bottle (54.7 % ABV) is priced at £65 and comes with a QR code that unlocks a virtual “Whisky Experiment” video featuring Becky Paskin and master distiller Iain McAlister.

**Tasting notes**
- Nose: spiced stone fruit, stewed white peach & cloves, lemongrass, mineral edge
- Palate: molasses, caramel, prune, fig, waxed wood, sandalwood spice
- Finish: long fragrant finish with subtle Campbeltown saltiness.

The release celebrates the festival’s heritage and showcases Glen Scotia’s innovative style.

## Source Excerpts
- In keeping with our tradition of bold innovative styles and flavours for this annual global release, this year’s 11-Year-Old lightly peated single malt has been finished for 12 months in white port casks.
- It is brimming with bright spiced stone fruit flavours, fragrant prune and fig notes, and a sandalwood spice, all intertwined with a distinctly-Campbeltown soft smoke and sea salt.
- The packaging of the latest release is embossed with a bespoke QR code giving our fans further afield a chance to transport themselves into the heart of the distillery for a unique new digital experience, the “Glen Scotia Whisky Experiment”.
- The region, which is characterised by its climate, nature, and location to the sea, is a deeply sensorial spot, defined by the elements, which also play an important role in its whisky-making.
- The bottling itself stays true to the classic Campbeltown whisky character, while offering something intriguing and bold in its unique flavour profile.
