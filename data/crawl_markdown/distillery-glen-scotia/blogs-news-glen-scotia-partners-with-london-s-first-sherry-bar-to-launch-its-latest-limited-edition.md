# Glen Scotia Seasonal Release 2022 – Andalusian‑Inspired Whisky

- **Product**: 12‑year‑old, unpeated, cask‑strength (53.3 % ABV), natural coloured.
- **Casks**: First‑fill bourbon barrels + 12‑month finish in Amontillado sherry casks from Montilla, Spain.
- **Flavour profile**: Warm, nutty with vanilla, mildly dry finish; subtle maritime nuances typical of Campbeltown malts.
- **Partnership**: Launched with London’s first dedicated sherry bar, Bar Pepito (owner Richard Bigg, chef Nacho del Campo).
- **Food pairings**: Whisky‑marinated gordal olives, Jamón Ibérico de Bellota, Manchego/Parmesan/blue cheeses, dark chocolate.
- **Distillery context**: Glen Scotia – Campbeltown’s oldest distillery (est. 1832), part of the Kintyre peninsula whisky region.
- **Awards**: 2021 Best In Show Whisky (SFWSC) and Scottish Whisky Distillery of the Year; 2022 Victoriana Best In Class (SFWSC).

## Source Excerpts
- Inspired by a winter’s day in Glen Scotia’s home, Campbeltown, the latest unpeated, cask strength, natural coloured release, available now, has been matured in first-fill bourbon barrels before a final twelve-month finish in Amontillado Sherry casks, which hail from the Montilla region of southern Spain.
- Working alongside Bar Pepito’s owner, Richard Bigg, and its Executive Chef, Nacho del Campo, Glen Scotia has explored the character of sherry and its exquisite flavour profile to craft a list of food accompaniments to enjoy alongside its latest expression.
- “Our partnership with Bar Pepito and the team has been a fantastic voyage of discovery, exploring the Amontillado flavour which plays such an important role in our latest release.
- Amontillado sherry starts as a Fino sherry before an ageing period of around ten years, which dissipates the ‘flor’, and through wood exposure and oxidation, produces a nutty, dry, and beautifully amber liquid.
- His passion for gastronomy paired with a love of sherry has accelerated the establishment to become one of the most sought-after authentic Spanish dining experiences in London.
- Jamón Ibérico de Bellota – one of Spain’s signature culinary items; a meat cured for around two to four years, giving it a rich nutty flavour and a texture that melts in the mouth.
