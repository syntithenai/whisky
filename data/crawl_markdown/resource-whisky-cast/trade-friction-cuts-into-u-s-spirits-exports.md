# Trade Friction Cuts Into U.S. Spirits Exports

U.S. spirits exports fell 3.8 % in 2025, largely due to trade tensions with Canada and the EU. Canadian provinces have largely boycotted American spirits following tariff disputes over fentanyl trafficking, cutting Canadian sales by 70 %. The EU’s suspended 30 % retaliatory tariff also reduced European demand by 35 %. Despite these setbacks, exports outside Canada and Europe grew 13.2 %, driven by Brazil, the UK, Australia and emerging markets. Total U.S. spirits export revenue reached **$2.37 billion** in 2025—five times higher than in 2000.

> *“The decline of U.S. spirits exports underscores the industry’s vulnerability to global trade uncertainty.”* – Chris Swonger, DISCUS President & CEO
