# Diageo to Close Clynelish Visitors Centre
Diageo announced the closure of its visitors centre at Clynelish Distillery after a recent review, despite having invested £185 million in renovations just three years earlier. The decision does not affect whisky production, which will continue unchanged.

- **Location**: Scottish Highlands, near Brora Distillery.
- **Context**: Part of Diageo’s cost‑cutting strategy under CEO Sir Dave Lewis and CFO Nic Jhangiani.
- **Other Impacts**: Similar reviews are underway for other visitor centres; no details released yet.

Diageo has also idled Teaninich, paused Roseisle Maltings, halted several U.S. distillery operations until mid‑2026, and will close its Crown Royal bottling plant in Ontario next month.

> *“Following a recent review, we have proposed changes to our visitor centre operations, which regrettably includes a proposal to close the visitor centre at Clynelish.”* – Diageo spokesperson
