# Geographical Indications and Certification Trade Marks

IP Australia explains that a **geographical indication (GI)** can be protected in Australia as a *certification trade mark* (CTM). A GI identifies goods produced in a specific region where certain qualities, reputation or characteristics are attributable to that area. Unlike standard trademarks, a CTM GI protects the product’s origin and associated quality rather than just its brand.

Key points:
- **Protection**: Registered as a CTM for up to 10 years (renewable). GIs can also be protected under the Wine Act.
- **Eligibility**: The region must have a distinct reputation or special characteristics linked to the product.
- **Application**: Requires detailed rules covering geography, production standards, certification requirements, approval procedures and dispute resolution.
- **Ownership & Management**: Can be owned by an incorporated association of producers, a central organisation or local authority. Owners must market, enforce, and monitor use of the GI.
- **Cancellation Grounds**: Includes lack of distinctiveness, confusion with earlier marks, genericness, non‑compliance with rules, etc.
