# How global cereal markets impact barley prices and drive malt costs

Simpsons Malt explains how the price of barley – a key raw material for malting – is influenced by broader cereal markets (wheat, corn) through substitution, production competition, trade dynamics and weather. The article outlines four main drivers:
1. **Substitution effect** – when wheat or corn prices rise, barley becomes a cheaper feed alternative.
2. **Production competition** – high cereal prices encourage farmers to plant wheat/corn instead of barley, tightening barley supply.
3. **Trade dynamics** – export restrictions or geopolitical events (e.g., Ukraine‑Russia war) shift global barley supply and price.
4. **Climate & weather** – droughts, floods or frosts reduce yields and spike prices.

Barley’s price fluctuations directly affect malt costs because barley is the primary raw material for malting worldwide. The article notes that future demand growth, climate volatility and trade uncertainty will keep barley (and thus malt) prices variable.

- T r a d e a n d e x p o r t d y n a m i c s Major barley-exporting countries like Canada and Australia also produce other cereals.
- Global trade policies, tariffs and geopolitical events that affect the export of the other cereal crops can influence barley prices.
- For instance, if a country restricts wheat exports, it might increase its barley exports to fill the gap, altering global barley supply and prices.
- From a geopolitical point of view, prior to the war between Russia and Ukraine, the latter accounted for around 10% of the world's wheat exports.
- In the early months of the war, concerns rose about whether, firstly, Ukrainian farmers would be able to harvest their crops at all and, secondly, whether the harvested crops would be able to be exported.
