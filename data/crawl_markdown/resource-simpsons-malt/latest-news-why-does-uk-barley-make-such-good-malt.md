# Why does UK barley make such good malt?

Simpsons Malt explains that the unique climate, soil and agronomic support in the UK produce barley with low protein and nitrogen, high starch, and a fuller grain profile. These traits give malts that are well‑modified, easy to mash, and lend themselves to both full‑bodied beers and consistent coloured malts. The company’s own merchanting division ensures traceability from seed to finished malt.

## Key points
- **Climate** – Jet Stream and Gulf Stream create mild winters, wet springs, warm summers and a dry late summer that favour steady barley growth.
- **Long growing season** – Early sowing (mid‑March) to harvest (July/August) gives more sunlight, higher starch content and yields.
- **Terroir** – Lighter soils along the coasts produce low nitrogen grains.
- **Protein/Nitrogen** – UK barley typically has TP <10 % and TN <1.6 %, compared with continental values >11.6 % and 1.85 %.
- **Mashing** – Four‑day germination yields a malt that can be mashed at 65.5 °C for ~80 % attenuation without a proteolytic stand.
- **Varieties** – Maris Otter, Golden Promise bring distinct flavours and fuller mouthfeel.
- **Roasting** – State‑of‑the‑art drums produce homogeneous colour roasts with cleaner flavours.
- **Traceability** – MSP merchanting division supplies seed, supports farmers, stores barley under controlled conditions, and tracks malt back to the farm.

---

## Practical takeaways for brewers
1. Use UK barley for styles requiring a fuller mouthfeel or high extract.
2. Consider a single‑vessel isothermal infusion mash at 65.5 °C.
3. Leverage Simpsons Malt’s traceability if provenance matters to your brand.
4. For coloured malts, rely on their roasting drums for consistency.
