# 2025 Barley Harvest: Drought, Quality & 2026 Market Outlook

Simpsons Malt’s grain‑trading team (Rob Austin and Sarah Hynes) analyse how a severe spring drought, early harvests and global market pressures shaped the UK barley crop in 2025. Key points:
- **Early winter barley intake** – 10 % of winter barley harvested by July 9, ahead of the five‑year average.
- **Spring barley challenges** – high nitrogen levels, low ear population, up to 30 % screenings and variable grain size.
- **Global competition** – strong US maize supply and a robust Scandinavian harvest have lowered malting premiums.
- **Market outlook** – price volatility is expected; early autumn sowing may offset some losses, but reduced demand could shrink the 2026 malting area.

The article ends on a cautiously optimistic note, noting that market dynamics can shift quickly and new trade deals (e.g., India) might influence future supply.

---

## Key facts
- **Early winter barley harvest** – 10 % by July 9 vs. 6 % five‑year average.
- **Spring barley screenings** – up to 30 % in some regions.
- **Nitrogen levels** – higher than usual, especially in the south.
- **Global market pressure** – US maize surplus and Scandinavian harvest reduce UK premium.
- **Potential 2026 impact** – growers may cut malting acreage due to low prices.

## Glossary & terminology
- *Malting barley* – grain used for whisky production.
- *Screenings* – damaged or broken grains removed during processing.
- *Nitrogen levels* – indicator of grain quality; high nitrogen can affect malt performance.
- *Premium* – price differential paid by distillers for higher‑quality malts.
- *Cereal market* – global supply and demand dynamics affecting barley prices.
