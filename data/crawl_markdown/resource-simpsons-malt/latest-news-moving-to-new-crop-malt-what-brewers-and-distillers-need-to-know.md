**Moving to new crop malt: What brewers and distillers need to know**

Simpsons Malt explains how yearly changes in barley growth conditions affect malting behaviour and downstream brewing/distilling performance. Key points include:
- Variations in rainfall, temperature and soil influence protein (TN), starch composition, grain size and husk integrity.
- Higher total nitrogen reduces starch‑to‑protein balance, lowering fermentable sugars, extract potential and friability.
- Grain quality is assessed via TN, grain size, screenings, 4 ml/8 ml tests, and starch gelatinisation temperature (critical for beta‑amylase activity).
- The 2025 crop was drier with higher TN, slightly lower extract and friability compared to the wet 2024 crop.
- Early testing and clear communication are essential for a smooth transition.
