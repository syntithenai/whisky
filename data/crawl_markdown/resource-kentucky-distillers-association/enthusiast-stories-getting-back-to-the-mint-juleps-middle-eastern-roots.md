**Getting Back to the Mint Julep’s Middle Eastern Roots**

- The article explains how a rosewater‑infused simple syrup (called *atar*)—a staple in Middle‑Eastern desserts like knafeh—was incorporated into a Kentucky mint julep.
- Authors Janice W. Fernheimer and Jim Ridolfo share the backstory: Jim’s Fulbright experience sparked an interest in knafeh, leading to homemade *atar* that later inspired a new cocktail variation.
- The post provides step‑by‑step recipes for both *atar* (rose or orange blossom water) and the Middle Eastern Mint Julep, including ingredient lists, preparation notes, and serving suggestions.
- It also includes brief biographies of the authors, highlighting their academic roles and contributions to bourbon scholarship.
