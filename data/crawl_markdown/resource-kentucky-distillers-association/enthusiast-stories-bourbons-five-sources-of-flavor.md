# Bourbon's Five Sources of Flavor

Fred Minnick explains the five core elements that shape bourbon: **grain**, **water**, **fermentation**, **distillation**, and **maturation**. He details how grain composition (corn, wheat, rye, barley) influences sweetness and spice; how limestone‑filtered water removes impurities; how proprietary yeast strains affect flavor profiles; how distillation limits proof to 160; and how charred new oak barrels impart vanilla, caramel, smoke, and other notes while the aging process creates the final taste.

The article also highlights notable wheated bourbons (Larceny, Maker’s Mark, Old Fitzgerald, Pappy Van Winkle, Weller) and discusses the importance of barrel selection and evaporation rates in single‑barrel releases.
