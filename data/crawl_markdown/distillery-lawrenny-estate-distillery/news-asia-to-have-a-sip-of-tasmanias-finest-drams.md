# Tasmanian Whisky Distillers Head to Hong Kong

Nine Tasmanian distilleries—including Lawrenny Estate Distilling—will attend the **Hong Kong International Wine and Spirits Fair** (Oct 30‑Nov 15) to showcase Tasmania’s whisky industry. The event aims to open Chinese and broader Asian markets, leveraging Tasmania’s clean climate, high‑quality barley and passionate producers.

Key voices:
- **Derek Charge**, CEO of Hellyers Road Distillery, stresses the untapped Chinese market.
- **Kristy Lark‑Booth**, President of the Tasmanian Whisky & Spirits Association, highlights Tasmania’s premium attributes.
- **Guy Barnett** and **David Morris** (ACBC Tasmania) frame the initiative as a strategic export push.

The delegation includes Greenbanks, Old Kempton, Sullivans Cove, Spring Bay, Turner Stillhouse, McHenry Tasmania, Sanyou, and Hellyers Road.

## Summary Text

The event seeks to break into Chinese and Asian markets, leveraging Tasmania’s clean climate, high‑quality barley, and passionate producers. ## Source Excerpts
- Hellyers Road Distillery CEO and managing director Derek Charge said that his company had been exporting to Europe for more than 15 years but had yet to crack the Chinese market.
- “If you look at the 20-year growth of Japan, which was originally the new source of interesting global whisky, Tasmania has a similar opportunity.
- “And without the multinationals, but a homegrown base of extraordinary whiskies, people will flock to our products in export markets.
- “The people making our products here are so passionate about what they create and that really comes through in the product.” Ms Lark-Booth said the Hong Kong International Wine and Spirits Fair was a massive opportunity for Tasmanian distillers.
- “The initiative is a strategic push to grow exports, build brand awareness and position Tasmania as a premium destination for craft spirits,” Mr Barnett said.
