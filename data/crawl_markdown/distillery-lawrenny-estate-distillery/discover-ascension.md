# Lawrenny Ascension Release

- **Style**: Single‑malt whisky from Tasmanian barley, River Derwent water and a blend of Saison, Ale & Distiller’s yeasts.
- **Maturation path**: 20 L ex‑Australian Tawny, Portuguese Port & Kentucky bourbon → fresh 100 L Bourbon & Port → finished in 600 L Pedro Ximénez sherry casks.
- **Entry strengths**: 50–65 % ABV, varied per stage to influence maturation rate and flavour extraction.
- **Final strength**: 46.6 % (no chill‑filtering).
- **Tasting notes**: Deep ruby colour; nose of honey‑roasted cashews, treacle & raisins; palate of grilled nectarine, toasted coconut, mango lassi, blackberry jam, tobacco and oak.

> *“The result is a single malt that not only showcases the very best of each cask type and size but introduces you to Lawrenny Estate's vision for our ongoing journey.”*

## Source Excerpts
- All the tawny/port whisky was then transferred into the fresh 100L Tawny casks and the bourbon aged whisky entered their own 100L Bourbon casks - this is where the secondary maturation occurs, injecting new flavour while polishing off the profile of the whisky.
- The final touch is achieved by the now combined bourbon and port whisky resting in rich, antique 600L Pedro Ximenez sherry casks.
- Nose Each cask is delivered to the nose through an even hand of honey roasted cashews from the bourbon casks mingling with the dripping treacle and warm raisins of the port and sherry casks.
- 20L ex- Australian Tawny, Ex - Portugese Port and Kentucky bourbon casks were chosen to begin the maturation.
- The different strengths that were used all have effects on most aspects of the maturation, from the maturation rate to the types of flavours extracted from the barrels.
- The Port/tawny casks were then married together with the Bourbon casks before being laid down in the Pedro Xiemenez barrels to Finish for three months bringing a balanced richness to the whisky.
