**Key take‑aways:**
1. **Fermentation flavour compounds** are produced by yeast metabolism (fusel alcohols → esters, acids, sulphur compounds). 2. **Yeast growth conditions** (oxygen, temperature, nitrogen) strongly influence ester and fusel profiles. 3. **Malt sugars** – especially maltotriose uptake – determine final alcohol yield. 4. **Historical yeast strains** (M‑strain, DCL, Kerry) have shaped Scotch whisky flavour for decades.
