# Japanese Whisky 'Absolutely' Needs a GI

- **Key Facts**
  - Japanese whisky has grown in popularity, leading to some bottles being labeled as Japanese without containing any distillation in Japan.
  - Number one Drinks Company co-founder Marcin Miller advocates for a Geographical Indication (GI) status to protect and clarify the origin of Japanese whisky.

- **Production Signals**
  - Several producers have discontinued aged-stated whiskies due to stock shortages, indicating pressure on supply chains.
  - The practice of blending whiskies from different distilleries is not new but needs clearer labeling to avoid consumer confusion.

- **Commercial Signals**
  - Japanese whisky commands premium prices, leading some brands to be marketed as Japanese without specifying the actual production location.
  - Distinctive branding and GI status could help differentiate authentic Japanese whiskies from those containing blended or imported components.

- **Risks / Unknowns**
  - Stefan Van Eycken expresses skepticism about the likelihood of a GI being granted soon due to industry interests protecting internal processes.
  - The impact on market positioning and consumer perception remains uncertain without clear regulatory action.
