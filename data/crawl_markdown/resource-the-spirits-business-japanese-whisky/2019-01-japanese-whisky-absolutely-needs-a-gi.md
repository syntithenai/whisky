# Japanese whisky ‘absolutely’ needs a GI

The article argues that Japanese whisky should receive Geographical Indication (GI) status to protect its authenticity and prevent mislabeling of non‑Japanese whiskies sold as “Japanese”. Marcin Miller, co‑founder of the Number One Drinks Company and The Kyoto Distillery, stresses the need for clear labeling. Stefan Van Eycken, author of *Whisky Rising*, agrees but doubts a GI will be granted soon. The piece also notes supply shortages that have forced producers like Suntory to discontinue aged releases such as Hibiki 17 Year‑Old and Hakushu 12 Year‑Old.
