# Japanese whisky ‘absolutely’ needs a GI

The article argues that Japanese whisky should receive Geographical Indication (GI) status to protect its authenticity and prevent misleading labeling. Co‑founder Marcin Miller of Number One Drinks Company and The Kyoto Distillery, along with author Stefan Van Eycken, support the idea but note challenges in implementation. The piece also highlights supply issues leading to discontinuation of aged Japanese whiskies such as Suntory’s Hibiki 17 Year Old and Hakushu 12 Year Old.

---

## Key points
- GI status would clarify that a whisky is truly produced in Japan.
- Current practice allows non‑Japanese whiskies to be bottled as “Japanese”, causing consumer confusion.
- Some producers keep internal distilleries secret, complicating GI enforcement.
- Stock shortages have forced discontinuation of popular aged Japanese whiskies.
