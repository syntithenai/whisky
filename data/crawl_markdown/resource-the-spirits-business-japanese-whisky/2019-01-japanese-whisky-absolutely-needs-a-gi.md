# Japanese whisky ‘absolutely’ needs a GI

The article argues that Japanese whisky should receive Geographical Indication (GI) status to protect its authenticity and prevent mislabeling of blends or non‑Japanese whiskies sold as “Japanese.” Co‑founder Marcin Miller of the Number One Drinks Company and Kyoto Distillery, along with author Stefan Van Eycken, support this view. The piece notes that rising demand has caused stock shortages, leading to discontinuations such as Suntory’s Hibiki 17 Year Old and Hakushu 12 Year Old.

## Key points
- GI status would clarify what qualifies as Japanese whisky.
- Current practice allows non‑Japanese whiskies to be bottled under the label.
- Stock shortages have forced some producers to drop aged offerings.
