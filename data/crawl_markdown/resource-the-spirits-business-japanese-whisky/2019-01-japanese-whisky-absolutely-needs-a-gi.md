# Japanese whisky ‘absolutely’ needs a GI

The article argues that Japanese whisky should receive Geographical Indication (GI) status to protect its authenticity and prevent misleading labeling. Marcin Miller, co‑founder of the Number One Drinks Company and The Kyoto Distillery, stresses that many bottles marketed as “Japanese” contain whiskies not distilled in Japan, which misleads consumers. He calls for a GI to ensure only whisky truly produced in Japan can carry that label.

Stefan Van Eycken, author of *Whisky Rising*, agrees but doubts the feasibility of obtaining a GI soon due to industry resistance and internal secrecy. The piece also notes supply shortages: Suntory has discontinued its Hibiki 17‑Year‑Old and Hakushu 12‑Year‑Old whiskies because of stock constraints.

The article concludes by pointing readers to the January 2019 *Spirits Business* magazine for a deeper dive into Japanese whisky.
