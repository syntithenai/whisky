## Distillery Overview
Belgrove Distillery, founded by Peter Bignell, is the first and (for a time) sole Australian rye distillery. The site operates a **hand‑built still** that is uniquely powered almost entirely by biodiesel derived from waste fryer oil (95 % of power), with the remaining 5 % supplied by hydro‑electricity. Grain is grown within 0.5 km, malted on‑site, and all equipment is largely second‑hand, giving the distillery a low‑carbon footprint.

### Production & Style
- **Grain**: 100 % rye (occasionally oat for batch 8). 
- **Mashing & Fermentation**: Manual, hand‑crafted processes with sensory sampling at each stage. 
- **Distillation**: Double distillation in a hand‑built still; no automated controls.
- **Peat**: Sourced from a family bog in Tasmania, providing a subtle coastal peat character.
- **Maturation**: Small‑batch casks (50–200 L) of ex‑malt barrels (Lark, Sullivan’s Cove, Old Hobart). Some expressions are finished in smoked or coffee‑infused barrels.
- **Cask Strength & Non‑Chill Filtering**: Many releases are unfiltered and bottled at 59 % ABV.

## Tasting & Reviews
- **Julia Nourney** (International Spirits Judge) praised *White Rye* for its grainy nose, peppery palate and creamy mouthfeel; noted a slight smoky note.
- **Barrel‑aged Rye (8 months)** received high scores (94/100) from Winespider Evaluation for its complex dill/pickle opening, chamomile tea, honeycomb and mustard seed notes.
- **Jim Murray** called Belgrove “Liquid Gold” in 2015.
- **Master of Malt UK** reviews highlighted a rich, spicy profile with cinnamon, clove and subtle paraffin‑smoke finish.

## Sustainability & Awards
- Claimed to be the world’s greenest distillery (95 % biodiesel, 5 % hydro‑electric, local grain, second‑hand equipment).
- Recognised by *Whisky Magazine* as “Icons of Whisky Australia” and “Craft Producer of the Year 2019”.
- Featured in ABC media spotlight (2011) and selected by Noma restaurant for a Sydney pop‑up bar.

## Key Takeaways
Belgrove Distillery blends traditional hand‑crafted whisky production with a strong sustainability ethos. Its rye expressions range from classic single‑cask styles to experimental smoked and coffee‑infused variants, all bottled at high ABV and unfiltered. The distillery’s unique biodiesel still and local grain sourcing set it apart in the Australian craft whisky scene.
