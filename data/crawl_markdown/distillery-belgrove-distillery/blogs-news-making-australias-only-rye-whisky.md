# Making Australia’s (edit, no longer the only) FIRST rye whisky

Belgrove Distillery in Tasmania is producing a 100 % rye whisky that delivers spicy notes similar to traditional American rye. The distillery also makes a “white rye” that does not require long oak ageing. Bignell uses ex‑malt barrels to give his rye a familiar malt character for Australian drinkers, while bartenders across Sydney, Melbourne and Hobart are already using it in classic cocktails.

## Key points
- First (now one of several) Australian rye distillery.
- Rye is spicier than malt; flavours develop quickly after distillation.
- White rye can be bottled after a few months, bypassing the 2‑year ageing rule.
- Ex‑malt barrels from Lark, Sullivan’s Cove and Old Hobart Distillery influence flavour.
- Bartenders use rye in Manhattans, Old Fashioneds, Whisky Sours and simple ginger‑beer mixes.
