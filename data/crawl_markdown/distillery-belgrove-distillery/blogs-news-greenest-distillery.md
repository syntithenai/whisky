# “Greenest” Distillery

Belgrove Distillery claims to be the world’s greenest distillery, powered almost entirely by renewable energy and employing a range of sustainable practices:
- **95 %** of its power comes from biodiesel made from waste fryer oil collected locally.
- The remaining **5 %** is hydro‑electric.
- Grain (rye) is grown within 0.5 km, requiring minimal fertilizer, irrigation, insecticides or fungicides.
- The distillery uses its own grain for malting, eliminating kilning energy.
- Waste mash feeds livestock; cooling water is recycled or used for irrigation.
- Most equipment is second‑hand and the building was an abandoned horse stable needing little modification.

These measures are presented as reducing fossil‑fuel CO₂ per litre of alcohol produced.

## Key facts
- **Location:** 3121 Midland Highway, Kempton, Tasmania, AU
- **Licence number:** 65290 (Liquor Licensing Act 1990)
- **Energy mix:** 95 % biodiesel from waste fryer oil, 5 % hydro‑electric
- **Grain:** Rye grown locally; no chemical inputs
- **Water use:** All distillery water captured on farm; cooling water recycled or used for irrigation
- **Equipment:** Mostly second‑hand; still is the only biodiesel‑powered still in the world (per 2011 claim)
- **Waste management:** Spent mash fed to livestock, waste heat capture under development
