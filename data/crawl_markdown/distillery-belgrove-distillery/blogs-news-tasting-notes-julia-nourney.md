# Tasting Notes by Julia Nourney – Belgrove Distillery

Julia Nourney, an international spirits judge from Germany, reviews two of Belgrove’s rye whiskies:

* **Belgrove White Rye (40 % abv)** – a grain‑heavy, dried‑fruit and spice profile with peppery, slightly smoky notes, creamy mouthfeel and a molasses aftertaste. The reviewer praises the overall taste and suggests that a touch of wood could enhance it further.

* **Barrel‑aged Rye (8 months, 50 L barrel, 57 % abv)** – features ripe apple, toffee, crème brûlée, bitter orange peel on the nose; bitter grapefruit, nougat, creamy toffee, hazelnut and dried plum on the palate. Watering reveals more creaminess, nuttiness and spice. The recommendation is to transfer it into a neutral cask for the final ageing period.

The post includes brief production details, tasting observations, and a note that shipping is currently unavailable due to fuel constraints.
