# Buffalo Trace Distillery Overview

Buffalo Trace Distillery, located in Frankfort, Kentucky, is the oldest continuously operating distillery in America. Established in 1789 by Colonel Edmund Haynes Taylor Jr., it has a rich history of over 200 years.

## Key Facts
- **Location**: Frankfort, Kentucky
- **Founded**: 1789
- **Age**: Over 200 years
- **Tours**: Complimentary tours and tastings offered daily (reservations recommended)
- **Products**: Bourbon & whiskey from Buffalo Trace Distillery

## Production Signals
Buffalo Trace is known for its traditional bourbon-making process, which includes:
- Continuous distillation in copper pot stills
- Maturation in new charred oak barrels
- A focus on quality and heritage

## Commercial Signals
The distillery offers a range of products that appeal to both casual drinkers and connoisseurs:
- **Bourbon Collections**: Various lines including Old Taylor, Blanton's Single Barrel, and Eagle Rare
- **Limited Editions**: Special releases like the George T. Stagg Reserve
- **Retail Availability**: Widely distributed in liquor stores across North America

## Risks/Unknowns
While Buffalo Trace is a well-established brand with strong commercial signals, potential risks include:
- Market competition from other premium bourbon brands
- Changes in consumer preferences towards lower-alcohol content options
- Supply chain disruptions affecting product availability
