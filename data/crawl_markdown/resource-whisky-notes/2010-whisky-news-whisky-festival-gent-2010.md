# Whisky Festival Gent 2010 – Review by Ruben

Ruben reports a mixed experience at the 2010 Gent whisky festival. He criticises the scheduling clash with Whisky Live in Spa and the absence of several key distributors (e.g., Malts of Scotland, Nectar). The organisers also dropped complimentary coupons for entry‑level malts, making the event more expensive.

He lists a selection of whiskies tasted, noting price points (€30–€130) and brief tasting impressions:
- **Old Ballantruan** – mild peat, floral, €30
- **Tullibardine 1993** – Sauternes finish, honey‑rich, €45
- **BenRiach 18 yo** – Moscatel finish, sweet, €60
- **Yamazaki Sherry cask** – raisin & cocoa, €70
- **Connemara Sherry finish** – dried fruit + smoke, €55
- **Benromach Organic Special Edition** – cereal‑like, mono‑dimensional, €45
- **Banff 32 yo** – flinty, fruity, toffee, €100 (recommended)
- **Highland Park 16 yo Calvados finish** – honeyed apples, €130 (highly recommended)
- **Glen Grant 23 yo** – balanced fruit & spice, €130
- **Bunnahabhain Toiteach** – peat & smoke, €40
- **Greenore 8 yo** – vanilla‑banana, €30

He also mentions purchasing the Bunnahabhain 1968 Auld Acquaintance and a Macallan 12 yr HKDNP for future reviews.

The post ends with comments from other readers praising Spa over Gent and noting similar frustrations about cost and quality.
