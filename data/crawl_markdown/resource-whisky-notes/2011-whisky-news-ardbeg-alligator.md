# Ardbeg Alligator
Ardbeg Alligator is a limited‑edition single malt released in June 2011. The name refers to the deepest level of cask char, producing a scale‑like pattern reminiscent of an alligator’s skin. Unlike most Scotch distilleries, Ardbeg experimented with toasted (not charred) new oak casks for this release, aiming for bourbon‑style flavours such as vanilla, cocoa, mint and tobacco rather than peat smoke.

The bottling was initially available to committee members in early June 2011, then released publicly later that month. Availability varied by country; Sweden sold it through Systembolaget at ~70 £ (799 SEK). The release is linked to earlier experimental casks (1998 cask 1189 & 1190) presented at Feis Ile 2009.

> *“Ardbeg Alligator… focuses less on peat smoke and more on bourbon flavours.”*
