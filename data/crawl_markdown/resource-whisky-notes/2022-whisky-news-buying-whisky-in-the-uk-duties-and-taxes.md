# Buying whisky in the UK: duties and taxes

The article explains how Brexit has affected buying whisky from the UK for EU customers. It details the three main extra costs – VAT, alcohol duties, and customs administration – using a Glen Dronach example. The author shares personal experience with several suppliers (The Whisky Exchange, Tyndrum Whisky, Bruichladdich, Bunnahabhain) and notes that pre‑paid options can reduce delays. It concludes that buying a single bottle is usually not worth it unless it’s a premium or exclusive release, but larger orders spread fixed costs more favourably.

---

## Key points
- VAT shifts from UK to customer’s country; duties are based on pure alcohol volume.
- Customs administration fees (e.g., DHL) add €14‑€30 per order.
- Pre‑paid shipping options exist and can be cheaper.
- Fixed costs become less significant for higher‑value or multiple bottles.
- Local shops should still be supported to reduce transport impact.
