# Whiskey Elements (Time & Oak)

WhiskyNotes reviews a Kickstarter‑derived product that inserts laser‑cut, partly charred oak pieces into a bottle to accelerate barrel‑age effects. The author tests two whiskies—anCnoc 12 yr and Grant’s Family Reserve—over 24–72 h, noting colour deepening, aroma shifts (pencil shavings, vanilla, banana candy), and smoother mouthfeel after 24 h but increasingly woody character after 72 h. The author questions the practicality for single malts and the cost‑benefit for blends, concluding the method is unpredictable and may not justify the expense.

---

**Key takeaways:**
- Oak elements claim to mimic barrel ageing in days.
- Colour deepens quickly; aroma shifts from fruity/floral to woody/vanilla.
- 24 h gives a smoother, rounder profile; 72 h can overwhelm with oak.
- Practical issues: large element size, single‑use, potential bottle waste.
- Author skeptical of hangover claims and cost‑effectiveness for premium whiskies.
