# Whisky festival Gent – first impressions

The 2009 Gent whisky festival was larger than the previous year but crowded, making detailed tastings difficult. The author sampled a range of whiskies from India, Scotland and elsewhere, noting their character, price and overall value.

**Key whiskies tasted**
- **Amrut Single Cask (46 %)** – fruit‑rich with peach, orange, pineapple and vanilla; long finish.
- **BenRiach 20 y (43 %)** – fresh, fruity, vanilla, smoky hints of cow stable; €65.
- **BenRiach 13 y Maderensis Fumosus (46 %)** – heavily peated with a sweet wine finish, citrus and salty licorice notes.
- **Girvan 18 y 1989 Raw Cask (56.7 %)** – modern grain whisky, complex, reminiscent of Maker’s Mark.
- **Bruichladdich X4 (50 %)** – technically not whisky; distilled four times to 90 % then diluted to 50 %, tasting like a flavored vodka or mezcal with cocoa and banana liqueur notes.
- **Arran bottlings** – praised for daily drams but lacking distinct character.

The author also highlighted the generous pricing policy of BenRiach and The Nectar, which offered festival dram tastings for just €1.
