# Whisky festival Gent – first impressions

The 2009 Gent whisky festival was larger than the previous year, making it hard to reach stands and talk in detail with staff. The author tasted several whiskies but could not give full tasting notes due to the crowded environment.

**Whiskies sampled**
- **Amrut Single Cask (46 %)** – fruit‑rich (peach, orange, pineapple) with vanilla; long finish.
- **BenRiach 20 y (43 %)** – fresh, fruity, vanilla, hints of smoke and cow stable; priced at €65.
- **BenRiach 13 y Maderensis Fumosus (46 %)** – heavily peated with a sweet wine finish; citrus, coal, salty licorice.
- **Girvan 18 y 1989 Raw Cask (56.7 %)** – grain whisky at cask strength, complex and reminiscent of Maker’s Mark.
- **Bruichladdich X4 (50 %)** – distilled four times to 90 % then reduced to 50 %; described as a flavored vodka/gin/mezcal rather than true whisky.
- **Arran bottlings** – praised for quality and fair pricing but lacking character compared to other whiskies.

The author also noted the festival’s pricing strategy, with some brands charging up to €20 for a 2 cl dram, while BenRiach and The Nectar offered any bottling for just €1.

---

**Key take‑away:** The festival was busy; many good whiskies were available but only brief impressions could be given. The author appreciated the value of BenRiach and the generous tasting policy at some brands.
