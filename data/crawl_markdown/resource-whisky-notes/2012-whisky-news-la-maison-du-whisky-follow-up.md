# La Maison du Whisky: follow‑up

The article is a customer’s post‑purchase review of the French distributor **La Maison du Whisky (LMdW)**, focusing on an issue with ordering the *Karuizawa 1981* bottle. The author recounts receiving incorrect stock information and a misleading reminder email, then details LMdW’s response: they negotiated additional bottles from other networks and promised measures to prevent future problems. The piece ends with a note of cautious optimism about buying Karuizawa again.

---

## Key points
- **Problem**: Wrong stock indication, incorrect reminder e‑mail, random order allocation.
- **LMdW’s explanation**: Karuizawa is reserved for the domestic market; selling large quantities abroad causes logistics issues and French customer backlash. Allocation system (country, license type, internet) was not respected in real time.
- **Resolution**: LMdW negotiated extra bottles from other networks to fulfil orders and pledged new measures to avoid repeat incidents.
- **Author’s stance**: Skeptical about the policy but appreciative of LMdW’s investigation; hopeful for better stock‑tracking and quicker response mechanisms.
