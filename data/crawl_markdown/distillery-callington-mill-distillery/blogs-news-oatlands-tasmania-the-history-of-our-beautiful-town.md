# Oatlands, Tasmania: The history of our beautiful town

Callington Mill Distillery’s blog post recounts the rich colonial heritage of Oatlands, a small Tasmanian town that grew around convict-built sandstone architecture and a windmill‑powered flour mill. The article highlights key historical milestones—from its 1821 founding to the 1837 railway and post office—while noting modern revitalisation efforts such as heritage preservation by the Southern Midlands Council and tourism initiatives.

The piece also touches on the Callington Mill’s own history: built in the 1830s, later converted into a steam‑driven mill, destroyed by fire in 1910, and finally restored in 2021 to produce legal single‑malt whisky. Visitors are encouraged to explore the distillery’s viewing platform, cellar door dining (opening 2022), and nearby attractions like Lake Dulverton and The Kentish Tasmania pub.
