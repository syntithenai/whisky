# Oatlands, Tasmania: The History of Our Beautiful Town

Callington Mill Distillery’s blog post chronicles the rich heritage of Oatlands, a small Tasmanian town that blends colonial history with modern tourism. Key points include:
- Founded in 1821 and once a convict settlement.
- Notable for sandstone architecture, a functioning Lincolnshire‑style windmill, and historic buildings such as the oldest rural supreme court house.
- The Callington Mill windmill (built by John Vincent) later became a distillery site; the current distillery was restored in 2021.
- Oatlands is experiencing a tourism revival with attractions like Lake Dulverton, The Kentish Tasmania pub, and The Imbibers.

The article highlights how the town’s past and present coexist, making it an appealing destination for visitors interested in history, architecture, and Tasmanian whisky.

---

## Key Facts
- **Founded**: 1821 (convict settlement)
- **Historic Structures**: 40+ Georgian sandstone buildings on High Street
- **Windmill**: John Vincent’s Callington Mill (mid‑1830s), later a distillery
- **Distillery Restoration**: Completed in 2021, now produces legal single‑malt whisky
- **Tourism Highlights**: Lake Dulverton, The Kentish Tasmania, The Imbibers, Blue Lake Lodge
- **Population**: <600 full‑time residents
- **Transport History**: Railway closed by late 20th century; Midland Highway realignment bypassed town in the 1960s‑80s
- **Current Status**: Growing visitor numbers and potential as a tourist hub

---

## Distillery Facts
- Callington Mill Distillery operates on the historic mill site at 6 Mill Lane, Oatlands.
- The distillery produces single‑malt whisky using local grains.
- On‑site dining (Cellar Door) opened in 2022.
- The distillery is Tasmania’s largest and offers viewing platforms for visitors.
