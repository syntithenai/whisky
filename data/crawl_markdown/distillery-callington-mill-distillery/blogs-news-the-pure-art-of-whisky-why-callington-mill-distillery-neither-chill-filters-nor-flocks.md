# The Pure Art of Whisky: Why Callington Mill Distillery Neither Chill Filters Nor Flocks
Callington Mill Distillery explains its decision to avoid chill filtration and flocculation, arguing that these processes remove fatty acids, esters and proteins that contribute to flavour, mouthfeel and texture. By keeping the whisky unfiltered, the distillery preserves a richer, fuller-bodied spirit that may appear hazy when chilled or diluted but remains authentic.

## Key Points
- Chill filtration removes compounds that cause cloudiness at low temperatures.
- Those same compounds add depth and body to the whisky.
- Flocculation similarly clears fats and esters, reducing flavour complexity.
- Callington Mill values purity, transparency and natural character over visual clarity.

The article is a brief statement of philosophy rather than a technical guide.
