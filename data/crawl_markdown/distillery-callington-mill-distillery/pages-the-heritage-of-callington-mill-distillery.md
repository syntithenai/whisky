# The Heritage of Callington Mill Distillery

Callington Mill Distillery traces its roots back to the windmill built in **1837** by entrepreneur **John Vincent**. While the mill produced flour for the local community, Vincent secretly operated a distillery—illegal at the time—to supply his pubs and hotels. The site evolved into a full‑scale whisky operation, now featuring a state‑of‑the‑art distillery that blends 19th‑century heritage with modern Tasmanian technology.

Key points:
- Original windmill built in 1837; commercial mill opened in 1840.
- Illegal distillery supplied Vincent’s hospitality venues.
- Current distillery uses cutting‑edge technology while honoring historical roots.
- The Mill Precinct includes a piggery, stable, barn, storage and granary.
- Products highlighted: *Quagmire Peated Tasmanian Single Malt Whisky* (AUD 159), *El Sol Tasmanian Single Malt Whisky* (AUD 149), *Poltergeist Barrel‑Aged Gin* (AUD 89.95).

The distillery invites visitors to explore the restored precinct and offers a range of single‑malt whiskies, gin, and merchandise.
