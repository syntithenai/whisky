## Source Excerpts
- This year, we donated a gallon (4.5-litre) bottle of 58-year-old single malt, presented in a truly remarkable hand-crafted oak box created by our neighbour and local sculptor, Stuart Murdoch.
- Matured in a first fill Oloroso Sherry Butt and bottled at natural cask strength, an impressive 59.2% ABV.
- Matured in the distillery’s traditional dunnage warehouses in Speyside, this release was laid down in first-fill sherry casks.
- Palate: Dark chocolate laced with sherry-soaked dried fruits gives way to a refreshingly spicy tingle on the palate leading to a bold thread of rich, roasted coffee.
- On the palate, the rich velvety dark chocolate notes lead to flavours of treacle toffee, mixed peel and a slightly smoky, sweet taste, surprisingly mellow for a single malt of this age.
- It showcases their skill and dedication to producing the highest quality Highland single malt, including the care and experience that goes into the time-honoured handcrafted tradition of whisky making, which is so important to us at Glenfarclas.
