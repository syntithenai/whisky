# Wild Turkey Bourbon Production
Wild Turkey’s bourbon is crafted on a hilltop in Lawrenceburg, Kentucky, using non‑OGM grains, limestone‑filtered water from the Kentucky River, and a proprietary yeast strain that has been used for over 60 years. The mash is fermented with sour mash, distilled twice, and aged in new American white oak barrels carbonized to level 4. Bottles are not released until at least five years of aging, often longer, and the whiskey is bottled at a lower proof to preserve its full flavor.

The production process emphasizes tradition, quality control, and patience: grains undergo four quality tests; water naturally purifies through limestone; yeast is kept secret by Master Distillers Jimmy and Eddie Russell; barrels are aged in steel‑lined warehouses since 1890. The result is a bourbon with an intense amber color, deep vanilla‑oak aromas, caramel sweetness, subtle smoke, and a long, full finish.

Wild Turkey also offers Rye, 101, Rare Breed, Kentucky Spirit, and American Honey variants, each following similar high‑standard production principles.
