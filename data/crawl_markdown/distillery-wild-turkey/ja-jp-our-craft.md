- The importance of local water from the Kentucky limestone aquifer and the role of the “Kentucky limestone” in giving the bourbon its characteristic flavor.

## Summary Text
Wild Turkey’s Japanese “Our Craft” page describes how the distillery has preserved its bourbon‑making heritage for more than a century. It explains that the same grain, yeast, and fermentation vessels used in Kentucky are still employed today. The water comes from the local limestone aquifer, which contributes to the spirit’s flavor profile. A family‑owned mash‑tun and careful temperature control maintain depth while allowing subtle variations. Production is small‑batch, hand‑crafted, with long aging periods (often 10–12 years) in new‑oak barrels. The page focuses on craftsmanship rather than product listings or pricing.
