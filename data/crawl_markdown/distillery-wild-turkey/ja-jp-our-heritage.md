# Wild Turkey Heritage
Wild Turkey Bourbon traces its roots back to the mid‑19th century, evolving from a small Kentucky distillery into a global bourbon brand. The page outlines key milestones: founding in 1855, expansion under Jimmy and Eddie Russell, the introduction of the flagship “Old No. 1” line, and modern innovations such as the 2016 “Amelia” series. It highlights the company’s commitment to quality, heritage, and its role in shaping American bourbon culture.

## Key Historical Highlights
- **1855** – First distillery established by the O'Steen & Co. family.
- **1869–1893** – Expansion into Kentucky and Louisville; launch of “Old No. 1” bourbon.
- **1940s‑1950s** – Post‑war growth, introduction of new product lines.
- **1976** – Global recognition as the first American bourbon to reach worldwide markets.
