# Wild Turkey History
Wild Turkey traces its roots back to the mid‑19th century with Austin Nichols’ company and the Ripy brothers’ distillery in Lawrenceburg, Kentucky. The brand’s name derives from a hunting story involving wild turkeys. Jimmy Russell became Master Distiller in 1954, followed by his son Eddie in 2015, making them the only father‑son duo of Master Distillers worldwide. Key milestones include the launch of the first bourbon liqueur (1976), Rare Breed (1991), Kentucky Spirit single‑barrel (1995), and the appointment of Matthew McConaughey as creative director in 2016.

## Production & Legacy
Wild Turkey emphasizes traditional bourbon production—mashing, fermentation, distillation, and aging in new charred oak barrels. The company highlights its heritage through storytelling on its website and maintains a strong presence in Kentucky’s bourbon community.
