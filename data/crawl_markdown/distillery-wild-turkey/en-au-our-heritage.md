# Wild Turkey Heritage
Wild Turkey traces its roots back to 1855 when Austin Nichols began selling spirits, eventually owning the brand. The Ripy brothers opened a distillery in 1869 on Wild Turkey Hill, and by 1893 their bourbon represented Kentucky at Chicago’s World’s Fair. In 1940 the name “Wild Turkey” was coined after an executive shared his bourbon with friends during a hunting trip.

The Russell family has shaped the brand since 1954: Master Distiller **Jimmy Russell** joined in that year, followed by his son **Eddie Russell** who became Master Distiller in 2015. The duo are the only active father‑and‑son pair of bourbon master distillers worldwide.

Key milestones:
- 1976 – first bourbon liqueur (later American Honey)
- 1991 – Rare Breed, a barrel‑proof bourbon
- 1995 – Kentucky Spirit, single‑barrel 101‑proof
- 2018 – Longbranch, small‑batch with Texas Mesquite and American Oak charcoals
- 2020 – Rare Breed Rye, premium barrel‑proof rye

Creative Director **Matthew McConaughey** joined in 2016.

The distillery emphasizes traditional techniques and barrel aging to maintain its “American icon” status.
