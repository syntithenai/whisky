# Exploring the Differences Between Rye Whiskey and Bourbon
Wild Turkey explains that both spirits are American whiskeys but differ mainly in mash bill, flavor profile, and aging rules. Bourbon must contain at least 51 % corn, be distilled ≤160 proof, aged in charred new oak for ≥2 years, and bottled ≥80 proof. Rye whiskey requires ≥51 % rye, similar distillation/aging limits, but can be bottled at higher proofs (Wild Turkey’s 101 Rye is 101 proof). The article contrasts the sweet, vanilla‑caramel character of bourbon with the spicy, peppery notes of rye, and highlights how grain choice shapes flavor.

---

## Key Takeaways
- **Mash bill**: Bourbon ≥51 % corn; Rye ≥51 % rye (often 95 % in Pennsylvania style). 
- **Distillation limit**: ≤160 proof for both. 
- **Aging**: Charred new oak, max 125 proof entry, minimum 2 years straight. Wild Turkey ages many bourbons ≥5 years; 101 Rye aged ≥4 years. 
- **Flavor**: Bourbon = sweet, vanilla, caramel, oak; Rye = spicy (pepper, cinnamon), earthy, with vanilla/fruit undertones.
- **Bottling proof**: Bourbon ≥80 proof; Rye ≥80 proof, Wild Turkey’s 101 Rye at 101 proof.

---

## Glossary
- **Mash bill** – grain recipe used before fermentation. 
- **Straight whiskey** – no additives, aged ≥2 years. 
- **Charred new oak barrels** – primary source of vanilla and caramel notes.
- **Proof** – twice the alcohol by volume (ABV).
