# Exploring the Differences Between Rye Whiskey and Bourbon
Wild Turkey explains that both spirits are American whiskeys made from a mash bill of grains, aged in charred new oak barrels, but differ mainly in grain composition and flavor profile.

- **Bourbon**: at least 51 % corn → sweet, buttery, vanilla‑caramel notes. Must be distilled ≤160 proof, aged ≥2 years (Wild Turkey ages 5+ years), bottled ≥80 proof.
- **Rye whiskey**: at least 51 % rye → spicy, peppery, allspice, earthy tones with vanilla/fruit undertones. Same distillation and aging limits; Wild Turkey’s 101 Rye is aged twice the minimum and bottled at 101 proof.

The article also covers mash bills, historical origins (Kentucky for bourbon, Pennsylvania/Maryland for rye), and how grain choice shapes flavor.

---
*Key facts:* Bourbon = corn‑heavy, sweeter; Rye = rye‑heavy, spicier. Both use new charred oak barrels and must be American‑made.
