## Long Description
# Wild Turkey Distillery Overview

**Location & History**
- The distillery sits on Wild Turkey Hill in Lawrenceburg, Kentucky, overlooking the Kentucky River. It was founded in 1855 as a wholesale liquor shop and became a full‑scale bourbon producer in 1869.
- Key milestones: first bourbon‑based liqueur (1976), launch of Rare Breed (1991) and Kentucky Spirit (1995), Longbranch (2018). The brand is family‑run, led by Master Distiller Jimmy Russell and his son Eddie Russell; creative direction added Matthew McConaughey in 2016.

**Visitor Experience**
- Guided tours via AnyRoad bookable online. Tours cover grain selection, mash & fermentation, distillation, barrel aging (white oak, level‑4 carbonized), tasting stations, historic warehouses and a gift shop.
- Visitor centre architecture is award‑winning; the site hosts a seven‑story rickhouse complex.

**Production**
- Uses non‑OGM corn, barley, rye with limestone‑filtered water from the Kentucky River. Mash fermented with sour mash, distilled twice, aged minimum five years in new American white oak barrels carbonized to level 4. Bottled at lower proof to preserve flavor.
- Variants: Wild Turkey Bourbon (smooth, vanilla‑oak), Rye, 101 (high‑proof), Rare Breed (hand‑selected full‑strength), Kentucky Spirit (single‑barrel), American Honey (sweet), Special Collection (limited‑edition).

**Marketing & Campaigns**
- 70‑year anniversary celebrated with a limited‑edition bottle in partnership with distiller‑chef Jimmy Russell.
- Global 2025 campaign *DON’T CHANGE A DAMN THING* showcases pre‑Prohibition style production and a short film directed by Kiku Ohe.

**Products & Availability**
- Products are sold online (Drizly, Minibar, ReserveBar) and in the on‑site gift shop. Prices vary; no fixed list provided on the site.
- Cocktail recipes feature 101 and Rye for Old Fashioned, Manhattan, Boulevardier, Whiskey Sour, Wild & Ginger.

Wild Turkey’s blend of heritage, production transparency and immersive visitor experiences makes it a flagship on the Kentucky Bourbon Trail.
