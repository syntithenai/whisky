# How Wild Turkey Bourbon Is Made

Wild Turkey bourbon is crafted using traditional methods passed down through generations at the Buffalo Trace Distillery in Kentucky. The process begins with selecting high-quality grains - typically corn, rye and barley - which are milled into a fine meal before being mixed with water to create mash. This mixture ferments for several days before distillation.

## Key Facts
- **Grain Bill**: Predominantly corn-based mash bill (around 70% corn)
- **Barrel Aging**: Bottled at 100 proof (50% ABV) and aged in new charred oak barrels for a minimum of four years
- **Production Volume**: Produces over 1 million gallons annually, making it one of the largest bourbon producers globally
- **Distillery Location**: Buffalo Trace Distillery, Kentucky - home to multiple distilleries including Willett, Elijah Craig, and Evan Williams

## Production Signals
- Uses a continuous column still for efficient production
- Implements temperature-controlled fermentation tanks to maintain consistency
- Aged in new charred oak barrels which impart distinct flavors and aromas

## Commercial Signals
- Strong brand recognition due to iconic "Wild Turkey" label
- Widely distributed across the United States and internationally
- Collaborations with renowned distilleries like Willett for limited editions

## Risks / Unknowns
- Potential impact of climate change on grain quality and availability
- Regulatory changes affecting bourbon production standards
- Competition from emerging craft distilleries offering unique flavor profiles
