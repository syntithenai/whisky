# Wild Turkey Heritage
Wild Turkey traces its roots back to the mid‑19th century, evolving from a local grocer’s wine and spirit sales into a storied bourbon brand. Key milestones include:
- **1855** – Austin Nichols begins selling spirits.
- **1869** – The Ripy brothers open a distillery on Wild Turkey Hill.
- **1893** – Their bourbon represents Kentucky at the World’s Fair.
- **1940** – The name “Wild Turkey” is coined after a hunting‑trip anecdote.
- **1954–1995** – Jimmy Russell joins as Master Distiller, followed by his son Eddie in 1981. They introduce innovations such as the first bourbon liqueur (1976), Rare Breed (1991) and Kentucky Spirit (1995).
- **2000‑present** – Continued recognition (Hall of Fame inductions, 50th‑year celebrations) and new releases like Longbranch (2018) and Rare Breed Rye (2020). The brand also partners with Matthew McConaughey as Creative Director.

The site highlights the family legacy, production philosophy, and commitment to quality while inviting visitors to explore products and community events.

## Source Excerpts
- 1991 Wild Turkey introduces our premium Rare Breed bourbon, one of the first barrel proof bourbons to hit the market.
- 1995 Wild Turkey follows it up with the launch of Kentucky Spirit, a single-barrel 101-proof bourbon, one of the only single barrel American whiskey offerings of the time.
- 2018 Wild Turkey introduces Longbranch, a small-batch bourbon refined with Texas Mesquite and American Oak charcoals, and the first co-creation from Master Distiller Eddie Russell and Creative Director Matthew McConaughey.
- 2020 Wild Turkey introduces Rare Breed Rye , our premium quality, masterfully crafted barrel proof rye.
