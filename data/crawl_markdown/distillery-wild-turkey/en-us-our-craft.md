# Wild Turkey Craft Overview

Wild Turkey’s bourbon is produced at its historic distillery on Wild Turkey Hill in Lawrenceburg, Kentucky. The process relies on non‑GMO grains, a proprietary yeast strain, limestone‑filtered river water, and the use of No. 4 “alligator” charred American white oak barrels. Bottling occurs only after a minimum five‑year aging period, often longer, to preserve depth of flavor and color.

Key production highlights:
- **Water**: Kentucky River, naturally filtered through limestone.
- **Yeast**: Proprietary strain used for over 60 years.
- **Grains**: Non‑GMO corn, rye, malted barley.
- **Char level**: No. 4 “alligator” char on all barrels.
- **Aging**: Minimum five years; many products aged 6–8+ years in wooden rick houses.
- **Distillation**: Continuous still, high‑temperature vaporization.

Tasting notes emphasize deep vanilla, oak, citrus, toffee, and a long, full‑bodied finish.
