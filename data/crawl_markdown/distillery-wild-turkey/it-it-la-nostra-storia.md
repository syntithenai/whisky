# Wild Turkey – History

Wild Turkey’s story spans from its early days as a wholesale liquor shop in 1855 to the present‑day bourbon icon. The brand’s roots lie with the Ripy brothers, who opened a distillery on Wild Turkey Hill in Lawrenceburg, Kentucky, in 1869. Over time the company evolved through key milestones: the first bourbon‑based liqueur in 1976, the launch of Rare Breed (1991) and Kentucky Spirit (1995), and the introduction of Longbranch (2018). Central to its legacy are Master Distiller Jimmy Russell – dubbed “the Buddha of Bourbon” – and his son Eddie Russell, who joined in 1981 and became Master Distiller in 2015. The brand’s creative direction was further shaped by Matthew McConaughey in 2016.
