# Wild Turkey Bourbon Production
Wild Turkey distills bourbon on a limestone plateau in Lawrenceburg, Kentucky. The process emphasizes natural water filtration, a proprietary yeast strain, non‑GMO grains, and high‑level (grade 4) charred American white oak barrels. Spirits are aged minimum five years, often longer, to develop deep amber color and rich vanilla‑caramel‑butter notes with a long, full finish.

## Key Production Steps
1. **Ingredient selection** – corn, rye, malted barley (non‑GMO). 2. **Mashing & malting** – limestone‑filtered water cooks grains; acid‑malt introduces live yeast. 3. **Fermentation** – proprietary yeast converts starch to sugar, creating the “moût”. 4. **Distillation** – high‑temperature vaporization and re‑condensation for purity. 5. **Barrel filling** – grade 4 charred barrels at lower alcohol content to preserve flavor. 6. **Aging** – minimum five years in oak; tasting annually until maturity. 7. **Bottling** – master distiller decides timing, filters, and bottles.

## Flavor Profile
- Deep amber color
- Aromas: vanilla, oak, citrus, caramel
- Palate: rich vanilla, caramel, lightly smoked butter
- Finish: long, full‑bodied
