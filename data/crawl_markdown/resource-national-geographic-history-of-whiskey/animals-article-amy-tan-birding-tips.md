# Amy Tan Shares Her Best Birding Tips

Amy Tan, the celebrated novelist, has turned her backyard in Sausalito into a thriving birdwatching hub. In this National Geographic feature she explains how she got started with birding at age 64, shares practical advice for beginners—such as getting outside, using binoculars, and keeping a field guide—and highlights the mental‑health benefits of watching birds.

Key takeaways:
- **Start simple**: Observe birds in your daily environment and ask questions about their appearance, sound, and behavior.
- **Invest in good binoculars** (≈$300) to spark interest.
- **Use a field guide or app** like *The Sibley Field Guide* or the Cornell Lab’s Merlin Bird ID.
- **Join local groups** or online communities for mentorship.
- **Set up feeders and plants** that attract diverse species.
- **Enjoy the health benefits**: even 15‑minute sessions lower cortisol and boost serotonin/dopamine.

The article also notes Tan’s own backyard now hosts 73 species, illustrating how dedication can turn a simple hobby into a rich, calming practice.
