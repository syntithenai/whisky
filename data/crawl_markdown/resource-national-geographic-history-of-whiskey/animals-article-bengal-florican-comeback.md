# Bengal Florican Conservation

The article tells how former poachers in Assam’s Manas National Park turned into protectors of the critically endangered Bengal florican, a two‑foot tall bustard. After decades of conflict and lack of patrols, community groups such as the Manas Maozigendri Ecotourism Society (MMES) and NGOs like Aaranyak worked with local villagers to stop hunting, surrender weapons, and volunteer for patrolling. The park was removed from UNESCO’s “in danger” list in 2011, and by 2023 over 800 volunteers aid conservation.

Today the florican population is slowly rising – a 2024 study found ten more birds than a 2011 survey – and tourism brings income to local families ($400‑$450/month during the breeding season). The story highlights community‑driven wildlife protection, economic benefits of ecotourism, and the ongoing challenge of conserving a species with fewer than 800 individuals worldwide.
