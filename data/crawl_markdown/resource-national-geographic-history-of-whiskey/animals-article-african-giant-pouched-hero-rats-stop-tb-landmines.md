# Carolina the Giant Rat

Carolina, an African giant pouched rat trained by the nonprofit **APOPO**, retired after a seven‑year career detecting tuberculosis (TB) in sputum samples across Tanzania and Ethiopia. In that time she identified more than 3,000 TB cases missed by routine microscopy, likely preventing over 30 000 infections. The article explains how APOPO trains rats to sniff volatile organic compounds produced by *Mycobacterium tuberculosis*, the bacteria that causes TB, and highlights the broader impact of the program—preventing nearly 400 000 new TB cases last year.

The piece also details Carolina’s life after retirement: she now lives with a fellow retired rat named Gilbert in a shaded enclosure, enjoys “retirement parties” with carrot‑cake treats, and is celebrated by her team as a hero.

Key points:
- **Detection speed**: 100 sputum samples in 20 min vs. four days for human microscopy.
- **Training**: Clicker‑based positive reinforcement; rats must pass a challenge of 500 samples with no missed positives.
- **Impact**: Each TB case detected saves an estimated 10–15 people from infection.
- **Future plans**: APOPO aims to expand the program to more countries, but faces funding and public perception challenges.
