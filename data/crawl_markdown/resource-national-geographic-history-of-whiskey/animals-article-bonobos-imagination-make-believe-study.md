# Can bonobos use their imagination?

National Geographic reports that a language‑trained bonobo, **Kanzi**, demonstrated the ability to track and identify *imaginary* objects in controlled experiments. The study, published in *Science*, used tea‑party style setups where Kanzi correctly identified cups containing “pretend juice” or jars holding “fake grapes.” His performance (≈68–69 % accuracy) suggests bonobos can understand secondary representation—an ability once thought unique to humans.

The article highlights the significance of this finding for primate cognition, conservation advocacy, and the evolutionary origins of imagination. It also notes that Kanzi’s extraordinary communication skills made him an ideal subject and calls for further research on other bonobos and chimpanzees.
