# Hangry Birds: Dominance at the Feeder
National Geographic’s March 2024 article explores how body weight, diet overlap and social hierarchy determine which North American birds win access to feeder food. Using data from Project FeederWatch (9,500 citizen scientists, 2016‑2022), it compares the downy woodpecker against five other species—blue jay, black‑capped chickadee, northern cardinal, red‑bellied woodpecker and house sparrow—and then expands to a broader list of 19 birds. The piece highlights that heavier birds often dominate, but behavioral traits (aggression, avoidance) also play key roles.

Key takeaways:
- **Dominance** is frequently signaled by body weight.
- Birds with similar diets interact more often.
- The downy woodpecker usually wins against lighter or similarly weighted species.
- In some cases, like the house sparrow, dominance status may still be shifting due to its invasive nature.

The article appears in National Geographic magazine’s March 2024 issue and is supported by Cornell Lab of Ornithology data.
