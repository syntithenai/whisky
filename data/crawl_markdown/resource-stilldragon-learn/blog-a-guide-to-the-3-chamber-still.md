# A Guide to the 3‑Chamber Still

StillDragon explains that a 3‑chamber still is a semi‑continuous batch still popular in the mid‑1800s for producing robust, aromatic spirits such as rye whiskey and rum. The design uses three interconnected kettles—primary, secondary, and tertiary—to extend dwell time, allowing more heat exposure and ester formation. Compared to double retort systems, the 3‑chamber still offers greater flavor complexity but is slower than column stills.

The article traces the historical decline of the design, its recent revival by Todd Leopold of Leopold Bros., and highlights StillDragon’s modular version that will be showcased at upcoming trade shows.
