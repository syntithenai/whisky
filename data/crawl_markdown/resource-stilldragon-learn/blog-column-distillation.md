# How a Distillation Column Works

This article explains the basic operation of a distillation column used in craft spirit production. It describes how vapor rises through plates with bubble caps, condenses into liquid beds, and repeatedly undergoes phase change until it reaches the condenser where it becomes the final product.

Key points:
- The process starts with fermented wash/beer/kettle charge.
- Heat and agitation produce vapor that travels up the column.
- Plates with bubble caps allow vapor to condense into liquid beds, which then re‑boil.
- The cycle repeats across all plates until the top dephlegmator and shotgun condenser finish the separation.
- Final liquid is collected in a parrot (condenser outlet) and can be measured for ABV.
