# Mixing Heads, Hearts, and Tails

Larry Taylor explains how the three distillation cuts—heads (beginning), hearts (middle), and tails (end)—vary in flavor profile. While a pure hearts cut yields a clean spirit, it often lacks complexity for flavored spirits like whiskey or rum. The article encourages distillers to experiment by adding small amounts of heads or tails back into the hearts to create a balanced, characterful final product, using food analogies to illustrate the concept.

## Key Takeaways
- **Heads**: early run, high in volatile compounds; often harsh.
- **Hearts**: middle cut, most desirable for flavor and spirit quality.
- **Tails**: late run, contains heavier, less pleasant flavors.
- A *pure hearts* product can be too neutral; blending with heads or tails adds depth.
- Experimentation is essential—find the right balance for each spirit style.
