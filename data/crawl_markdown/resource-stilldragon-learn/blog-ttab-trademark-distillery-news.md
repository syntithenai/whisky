# TTAB Trademark Guidance: Distillery News

The article explains a recent U.S. Patent and Trademark Office (TTAB) decision that clarifies what constitutes a *deceptive* product label under Section 2(a) of the Lanham Act for wines and spirits. The ruling emphasizes that:
- A wine or spirit cannot use any geographic reference on its label if it is not produced in that region.
- Even alluding to a person’s name can be problematic if that person is strongly associated with a specific geography.

Distillers are advised to consult legal counsel to ensure compliance, as the guidance may affect labels such as an American‑made “St. Patrick’s Day Whiskey.”

---

**Key Takeaway:**  Avoid geographic or iconic personal references on wine/spirits labels that could mislead consumers about origin.
