# White Whiskey vs. Aged Whiskey

- **White whiskey** is simply unaged whiskey (sometimes called “baby whiskey”). It must touch a barrel to be legal but does not need aging.
- It is often compared to vodka or moonshine, but the only real similarity is color. Vodka must exceed 95 % ABV during distillation; whiskey cannot.
- Moonshine is an illegal, unregulated spirit; white whiskey is legal and regulated.
- Advantages of white whiskey: showcases grain bill, yeast, fermentation, and distilling method; useful for startups to sell immediately.
- Disadvantages: small market, less complex flavor profile.
- Aged whiskey gains complexity from barrel aging (2‑6 years typical). It commands a higher price point.
