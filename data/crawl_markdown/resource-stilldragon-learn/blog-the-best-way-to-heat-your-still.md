# The Best Way to Heat Your Still
This blog post explains the pros and cons of various heating methods for distillation kettles, from open‑flame wood or gas burners to immersion elements, steam injection, and jacketed electric or oil‑heated kettles. It highlights safety considerations, heat transfer efficiency, potential dilution with steam, and practical tips such as using shrouds or insulation.

## Key Takeaways
- **Open flame** (wood, propane, natural gas) is simple but risky and can lose a lot of heat unless insulated.
- **Immersion elements** give direct contact heating but risk scorching solids in grain‑based charges.
- **Direct steam injection** offers good heat transfer but adds water, diluting ABV.
- **Steam‑fired jacketed kettles** provide efficient heat with minimal loss and can reach 245 °F at 15 psi.
- **Electric jacketed kettles** use a self‑contained steam generator; no direct contact with the charge.
- **Oil‑heated jackets** are slow to warm but retain heat well once reached.

The best method depends on budget, available resources, and safety regulations.
