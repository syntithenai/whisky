# How to Set Up a Reflux Still

StillDragon’s guide walks you through building a modular reflux column using their ProCap system and standard fittings. The process is broken into six phases: assembling copper plates with ProCaps, adding a starter collar, stacking tees to reach the desired height, installing the dephlegmator (partial condenser), connecting the Parrot kit for product collection, and finally attaching irrigation hoses for cooling.

Key take‑aways:
- **Column size matters** – wider columns run faster; taller columns yield higher proof.
- **ProCaps vs. Bubble Caps** – ProCaps are more efficient but require a 10 mm wrench to assemble.
- **Dephlegmator** – acts as a heat exchanger, partially condensing vapor back into the column.
- **Irrigation hoses** – blue for hot water (bottom port), red for cold water (top port).
