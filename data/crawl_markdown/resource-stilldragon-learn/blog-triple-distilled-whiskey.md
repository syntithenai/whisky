# Triple Distilled Whiskey Techniques

StillDragon explains that triple distillation involves three phase‑change cycles—evaporation and condensation repeated three times—to refine whiskey. The article discusses how each run can be tuned (speed, cuts, dilution) to influence flavor, congeners, and proof. It highlights the role of Maillard reactions during high‑heat stripping, the purpose of heads/tails cuts, and the trade‑off between speed and separation efficiency. Finally, it invites distillers to experiment with variables rather than assuming triple distillation is inherently superior.

---

**Key points:**
- Triple distillation = three evaporation/condensation cycles.
- Stripping run: fast vs slow, heads/tails cuts, Maillard reaction.
- Second run cleans low wines and boosts proof.
- Third run aims for barrel‑strength without dilution.
- No definitive claim that triple is better; experimentation encouraged.
