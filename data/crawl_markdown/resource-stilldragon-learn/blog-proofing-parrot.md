# How Does a Proofing Parrot Work?

A proofing parrot is a small cylindrical vessel that lets distillers measure the alcohol by volume (ABV) of their run in real‑time. The article explains how the traditional glass version works, its limitations (lag, inaccurate readings), and why an electric version from StillDragon offers faster, more reliable ABV estimates by measuring vapor temperature.

Key points:
- Traditional parrots use a hydrometer in a liquid reservoir; accuracy is low but consistency matters.
- Turn‑over lag can mislead distillers during rapid head‑to‑heart transitions.
- The electric parrot eliminates the reservoir, uses a microcontroller to convert vapor temperature to ABV, and offers quicker response with less risk of breakage.

The post concludes that no on‑the‑fly device is 100 % accurate but highlights the trade‑offs between glass and electric models.
