# Get a Dedicated Gin Still: Why Your Gin Design Isn't Working

StillDragon explains why using a dedicated gin still can solve common production issues, such as cleaning botanical oils and preventing cross‑contamination. The article weighs the cost‑benefit of adding a dedicated still or at least a dedicated condenser, especially for macerated gins. It also highlights how a single‑walled kettle can be a budget option while noting the risk of scorching solids. Finally, it stresses that quality control is vital to avoid alienating customers who expect pure gin flavor.

## Key Takeaways
- Dedicated gin stills keep botanical oils from contaminating other spirits.
- A dedicated condenser may suffice for vapor‑infused gins but not for macerated ones.
- Single‑walled kettles are cheaper but risk scorching solids; muslin bags or filtration can mitigate this.
- Quality control is essential to maintain brand reputation and customer satisfaction.
