# Get a Dedicated Gin Still: Why Your Gin Design Isn't Working

StillDragon explains why a dedicated gin still can solve common production challenges, such as cleaning botanical oils and preventing cross‑contamination. The article weighs the cost‑benefit of investing in a dedicated system versus using a single‑walled kettle or a dedicated condenser for vapor‑infused gins. It highlights advantages like recipe development, quality control, and protecting brand reputation when gin is not the primary product.

## Key Takeaways
- Dedicated gin stills keep botanical oils from contaminating other spirits.
- Vapor‑infused gin can use a dedicated condenser instead of a full system.
- Single‑walled kettles are cheaper but risk scorching solids; muslin bags or filtration mitigate this.
- A dedicated kettle doubles as a recipe‑development unit for small‑scale producers.
- Quality control is critical to avoid alienating customers who expect distinct gin flavors.
