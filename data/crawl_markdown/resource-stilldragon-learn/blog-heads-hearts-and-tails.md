# Heads, Hearts & Tails

StillDragon explains the classic distillation cut terminology and challenges new distillers to move beyond a rigid “smooth” focus. The article defines:
- **Heads** – early‑run alcohols high in low‑boiling compounds (e.g., aldehydes, ethyl acetate).
- **Hearts** – the desirable middle fraction.
- **Tails** – late‑run distillate rich in fusel oils and little alcohol.

It argues that obsessively cutting for a perfectly smooth spirit often removes complexity, leading to one‑dimensional finished products. Instead, experienced distillers learn to taste and adjust cuts gradually, adding back small amounts of heads or tails to enhance flavor while avoiding harshness. The piece stresses that cut points are subjective, vary by still type, raw material, and desired style, and should be guided by sensory feedback rather than fixed rules.

The post ends with encouragement to keep notes, experiment slowly, and seek expert advice when needed.
