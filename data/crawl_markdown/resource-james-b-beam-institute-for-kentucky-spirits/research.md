# James B. Beam Institute for Kentucky Spirits – Research

The **James B. Beam Institute** (JBBI) at the University of Kentucky focuses on scientific, engineering and business research that supports Kentucky’s bourbon industry. The institute offers a range of analytical services (electron microscopy, NMR, mass spectrometry, food‑systems innovation, heritage science labs) and runs several active projects:

- **Corrosion during maturation** – studying steel hoop failure in oak barrels.
- **Distillery microbiome** – characterising microbial communities that influence flavour.
- **Post‑maturation liquid recovery** – analysing angel’s share variability across warehouses.
- **Filtration methods** – linking filtration steps to clarity and sensory attributes.
- **White Oak genome** – decoding the genetics of Quercus alba for sustainable oak management.

Industry partners include Jim Beam, Wild Turkey, Four Roses, Buffalo Trace, Heaven Hill, and many others. The institute’s faculty fellows span environmental sustainability, biotechnology, packaging, business, public health, and more.

The page also lists extensive research publications across grain science, maturation chemistry, co‑product utilisation, and workforce education.
