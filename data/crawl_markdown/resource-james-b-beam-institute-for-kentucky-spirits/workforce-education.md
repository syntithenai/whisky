# Workforce Education at the James B. Beam Institute

The James B. Beam Institute for Kentucky Spirits offers a range of workforce‑development programs aimed at advancing skills in the American whiskey industry. Key offerings include:
- **Short Course Program** – a day‑long, classroom‑based experience covering grain handling, fermentation, process engineering, maturation and customer relations.
- **Bourbon Professionals’ Training Workshop** – a multi‑day workshop with lectures and hands‑on activities focused on the science and engineering behind whiskey production.
- **Whiskey Specialist Registered Apprenticeship Program** – a Department of Labor‑registered apprenticeship providing ~3 000 hours of on‑the‑job training plus ~200 hours of technical instruction, currently exclusive to James B. Beam Distilling Company.

The Institute also runs the **Workforce Training Standards Project**, aiming to unify industry standards for workforce excellence. Upcoming events include a Distillery Safety Series focused on EHS compliance.

For more details or to start a program, contact Education Coordinator Kevin Baldridge (Dr.) via email.
