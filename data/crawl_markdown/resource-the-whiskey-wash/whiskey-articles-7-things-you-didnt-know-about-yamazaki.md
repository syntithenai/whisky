# 7 Things You Didn’t Know About Yamazaki

- **Japan’s first whisky distillery** – founded in 1923 by Shinjiro Torii, modeled on Scottish designs but built with a Japanese twist.
- **Local folklore** – the “barley‑eating monster” myth that explained why grain never appeared at the gates.
- **Mizunara oak** – wartime scarcity forced use of this porous wood, giving Yamazaki its signature sandalwood and incense notes.
- **Diverse production** – 16 copper pot stills and five cask types (sherry, wine, bourbon, Mizunara) allow a wide flavor palette.
- **First Japanese single malt** – released in 1984, proving Japanese whisky could stand alone internationally.
- **World Whisky of the Year** – Yamazaki Sherry Cask 2013 won Jim Murray’s award in 2014, boosting global reputation.
- **Whisky library** – over 7 000 unblended bottles stored behind glass walls, a resource for blenders.

These facts highlight Yamazaki’s pioneering role, unique production choices, and cultural impact on Japanese whisky.
