# Eagle Rare 30 Year‑Old: Key Facts

- **Oldest age‑stated Buffalo Trace bourbon** – first bottled at 30 years.
- **Produced in Warehouse P**, a climate‑controlled experimental facility built to slow evaporation and preserve flavor.
- **Auctioned by Bonhams (24 Apr–8 May)** – Lot 1 (first bottle) estimated £7,500‑10,000 with a private tasting; Lot 2 includes the full Eagle Rare range plus a stay at Stagg Lodge.
- **Historical lineage** – Created in 1975 by Charles L. Beam at Four Roses, transferred to Sazerac/Heaven Hill, then settled at Buffalo Trace (formerly George T. Stagg Distillery) in 1999.
- **Line‑up context** – Completes a six‑bottle range from the 10‑year flagship (~$38–40) up to the 30‑year historic release.

## Why It Matters
The Eagle Rare 30 Year‑Old marks Buffalo Trace’s deepest aging experiment, offering collectors a rare bottle that showcases how climate control can extend bourbon maturity without over‑oaking. The Bonhams auction provides an accessible entry point for enthusiasts and a unique tasting experience tied to the distillery.
