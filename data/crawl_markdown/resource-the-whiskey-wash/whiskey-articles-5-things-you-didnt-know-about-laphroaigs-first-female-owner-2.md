# 5 Things You Didn’t Know About Laphroaig’s First Female Owner

- **Bessie Williamson** (born Elizabeth Leitch) began as a secretary at Laphroaig in 1934 and became the distillery’s first female owner in 1954 after Ian Hunter’s death.
- She managed the distillery through WWII, balancing wartime restrictions with supply demands for single‑malt and blending.
- In 1961 she sold 33 % of her shares to Long John Distillers (a U.S. subsidiary) and later divested the rest in 1972, retiring thereafter.
- Williamson was also a spokesperson for the Scotch Whisky Association (SWA) from 1961‑64, leveraging her unique position to promote Islay whisky abroad.
- A special edition of **Laphroaig 25 Year‑Old: The Bessie Williamson Story** was released in 2019/2024 to commemorate her legacy.

The article highlights her business acumen, charitable work (Order of St John), and lasting impact on Laphroaig’s global reputation.
