# Best Value Scotch Whiskies: 6 Affordable Bottles Worth Buying

Phil Dwyer lists six Scotch whiskies that offer great quality for their price, covering a range of regions and styles:

- **Torabhaig Taigh** – 46 % ABV, £45 / $57, smoky‑sweet with caramel notes.
- **Ledaig 10 Year Old** – 46.3 % ABV, £37 / $46, peated and affordable.
- **Glenallachie 12 Year Old** – 46 % ABV, £45 / $56, sherry‑cask influenced with nutty and chocolate flavours.
- **Glasgow 1770 Original Single Malt** – 46 % ABV, £42 / $52, unpeated Lowland style with coconut and fruit.
- **Maclean’s Nose Blended Scotch Whisky** – 46 % ABV, £30 / $29, high‑malt blend with peat, pepper and lemon zest.
- **Compass Box Nectarosity** – 46 % ABV, £52 / $50, modern blended whisky with fruit, vanilla and pineapple.

The article encourages buying several of these to enjoy variety without overspending.

### Distillery Facts
- **Torabhaig** – New distillery on Isle of Skye, uses ex‑bourbon and rumored ex‑Madeira casks.
- **Ledaig** – Distilled at Tobermory on the Isle of Mull.
- **Glenallachie** – Sherry‑cask influenced single malt from Lowlands.
- **Glasgow 1770** – Flagship unpeated whisky from Glasgow Distillery.
- **Maclean’s Nose** – Blend by Adelphi and Ardnamurchan Distilleries.
- **Compass Box Nectarosity** – Blend of malts from Linkwood, Clynelish, Balmenach; grain whisky from Girvan & Cameronbridge.

## Source Excerpts
- Glenallachie 12 Year Old, 46% ABV, £45 / $56 Some of you might be bored of hearing about Glenallachie, but it remains one of the best choices for value and flavour when it comes to sherry cask-influenced whisky.
- Affordable pricing, great flavour, varied regions, and a few surprises that might just encourage you to try something new.
- Matured in ex-bourbon and some ‘wine’ casks, rumoured to be ex-Madeira, this is a smoky, sweet, caramel-topped gem that should be hitting shelves very soon.
