# The Macallan: Six Lesser‑Known Facts

- **Small copper stills** – The Macallan’s stills are among the smallest on Speyside, a design choice that maximises copper contact and shapes the whisky’s fruity, malty profile.
- **Cirque du Soleil partnership** – For its 200th anniversary the distillery collaborated with the performance house to create an immersive experience celebrating nature.
- **Wildflower roof** – The new distillery features a 200‑m timber roof planted with Speyside long grass and wildflowers, touted as the most complex engineered timber roof in the world.
- **David Holmes advertising legacy** – Over four decades of iconic adverts (250+ pieces) helped define Macallan’s brand voice.
- **Extensive estate** – The 485‑acre property hosts >60 000 trees and supports more than 70 wildlife species, including several European protected ones.
- **Record‑setting 1926 bottles** – The 1926 single malt has sold for over $2.7 million at auction, holding the most expensive whisky record multiple times.

These points illustrate Macallan’s blend of heritage, innovation and environmental stewardship.

## Source Excerpts
- Internationally renowned as the pinnacle of luxury single malt, and producer of the most expensive whisky in the world—incidentally a title they have held more than any other distillery.
- The Macallan’s “Curiously Small Stills” Are Amongst The Smallest On Speyside The “Curiously Small Stills” are one of Macallan’s six pillars—the six points Macallan uses as the foundation for their whisky—and their size has an important part to play in creating Macallan whisky.
- “The size of these stills maximizes the contact of the spirit with the copper which is vital in creating this balance of flavors.
- In a previous role I held as Research Scientist at The Scotch Whisky Research Institute I spent most of my time working on projects looking at the importance of copper as the material of construction for malt stills.
- More specifically, the interaction between spirit vapors on the copper surface and how that impacts flavor.” Macallan completed work on their new distillery in 2018 to allow it to produce the highest quality whisky to an international audience.
- It is planted with Speyside long grass as well as wildflowers, and was designed to blend into and support the Speyside ecosystem that surrounds the distillery.
