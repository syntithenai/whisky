# 7 Standout Cask‑Finished Bourbons Worth Trying in 2026

Phil Dwyer reviews seven bourbons that have been finished in non‑American oak casks—Cognac, Port, maple syrup, etc.—to illustrate how finishing can add depth and nuance. Each entry lists the finish type, ABV, price, and a brief tasting note.

## Plain‑text summary
Phil Dwyer lists seven bourbons finished in various casks (Cognac, PX, Port, maple syrup, Armagnac) and gives each a short tasting note. Prices range from $38 to $160. He argues that finishing adds depth rather than being a gimmick.

## Distillery facts
- 1792 Bourbon – American distillery in Kentucky.
- Rabbit Hole – Kentucky distillery known for wheat‑forward bourbons.
- Boondocks – Kentucky distillery producing small‑batch bourbons.
- Michter’s – Kentucky distillery with a reputation for rich, indulgent finishes.
- Barrell Bourbon – Multi‑distillery blend of straight bourbons finished in Armagnac casks.
- Widow Jane – Kentucky distillery that experiments with maple syrup cask finishing.
- Bardstown Bourbon Co. – Kentucky distillery collaborating with Maison Ferrand Cognac.

## Source Excerpts
- By resting bourbon in casks that previously held wines or spirits such as Port, Sherry, Cognac, or even maple syrup, producers can introduce complementary notes that sit alongside the core profile.
- Boondocks 6 Year Old Port Cask Finish, 45%, £30 / $48 Port-finished bourbon is often associated with Angel’s Envy, but Boondocks takes that familiar profile and pushes it further, helped by a slightly higher ABV.
- A wheat-forward bourbon finished in PX casks, it adds an extra dimension to what wheat and sherry can achieve together.
- Barrell Bourbon Cask Finish Series: Armagnac, 56.5%, £95 / $86 This multi-distillery blend of straight bourbons is finished in Armagnac casks, and it’s a bold example of what Barrell does best.
- Classic American oak will always define bourbon, but finishing adds another layer of flavour, texture, and character.
- These 7 finished bourbons—from Cognac to maple syrup casks—prove exactly why the technique matters.
