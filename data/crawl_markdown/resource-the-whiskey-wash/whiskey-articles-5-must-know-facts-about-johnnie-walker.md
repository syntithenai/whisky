# 5 Must‑Know Facts About Johnnie Walker

- **Label tilt** – The iconic 24° slanted label was introduced in 1867 to make the bottle stand out and increase legibility; it was trademarked in 1877.
- **Colour hierarchy** – Red (1909), Black (12‑yr, 1909), Green (1997/2016), Gold (18‑yr, 1995), Blue (ultra‑premium, 1992). The range evolved over time rather than linearly.
- **Founder’s sobriety** – John Walker never drank whisky; his tea‑blending background gave him a keen sense of balance without tasting.
- **Origin in grocery/tea** – Started as a Kilmarnock grocer selling tea, wine and spirits; whisky blends were produced by 1850 for consistency.
- **Blue Label context** – Launched 1992 with no age statement; pricier variants (King George V, The John Walker) use older stock and command higher prices.

These points highlight design strategy, product evolution, and the founder’s unique background.
