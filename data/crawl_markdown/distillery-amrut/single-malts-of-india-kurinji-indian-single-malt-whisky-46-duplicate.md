# Kurinji Indian Single Malt Whisky

- **ABV**: 46%
- Named after the *Kurinji* flower of the Nilgiri hills.
- Described as a balanced, nuanced whisky with floral, fruity, and spicy notes.
- Part of Amrut’s “Single Malts of India” series following the successful Neidhal release.
- Tasting notes highlight pear, melon, muscovado sugar, vanilla, oak, toffee, honey, ginger candy, cracked pepper finish.

## Key Facts
- **Distillery**: Amrut Distilleries Private Ltd., Bengaluru, India.
- **Launch Context**: Second release in the Indian single‑malt line after Neidhal.
- **Cultural Reference**: Kurinji flower blooms every 12 years; linked to Tamil *tainais* classification of mountainous regions.
