# Regulatory Compliance & Internal Audits for Distilleries

- **Key Topics**: TTB record‑keeping, penalties, inventory management, reporting, labeling rules, OSHA safety requirements.
- **Primary Authors**: Joseph Infante and Douglas Crim of Miller‑Canfield’s Food, Beverage & Agri‑Business Practice Team.
- **Geographic Focus**: United States (Michigan, Florida, Illinois, New York) with references to Canada, China, Poland.

## Highlights
1. **TTB Record‑Keeping** – Distilleries must keep daily production, storage, and processing records; maintain them for three years; use computerized software if possible.
2. **Penalties** – $1,000 fine or up to one year in prison per offense; consistent late returns trigger audits.
3. **Inventory Issues** – Losses (angel’s share, theft) are not taxable; shortages require proof of loss and accurate record‑keeping.
4. **Reporting & Tax** – Monthly reports (F 5110 series), semi‑monthly/quarterly excise tax returns (F 5000.24). 
5. **Labeling Rules** – 27 CFR §5.22 defines 12 product classes; strict prohibitions on misleading or offensive imagery and statements.
6. **OSHA Compliance** – Emphasis on safety program, hazard identification, employee training, and the legal consequences of violations (up to $70 k for willful infractions).
