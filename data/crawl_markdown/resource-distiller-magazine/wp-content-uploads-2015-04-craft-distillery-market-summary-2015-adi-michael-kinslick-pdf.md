# Craft Distillery Market Summary 2015

Michael Kinstlick and Coppersea Distilling, LLC present a data‑driven snapshot of the U.S. craft distillery sector from 2006–2015. Key points include:
- **Growth**: The number of craft distilleries is projected to reach 1,000 within three years, with entrants doubling every three years.
- **Market share**: Craft distillers hold ~1 % of the U.S. distilled spirits market; craft brewers hold ~14 % of the beer market.
- **Production categories**: Distilleries produce vodka, gin, whisky, rum and other spirits (percentages not summing to 100 %).
- **Geography**: Data are broken down by state for 2010–2014 and earlier periods.
- **Licensing**: Licensed distilleries in the U.S. grew from ~1,800 in 1980 to over 9,000 by 2014.

The report defines a craft distillery as an independent firm producing spirits below a certain volume (≈40 k cases or 65 k proof‑gallons). It also discusses data sources (ADI directories) and methodology for compiling the figures.
