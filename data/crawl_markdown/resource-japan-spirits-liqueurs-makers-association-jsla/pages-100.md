## Key Points
- JSLA coordinates national whisky policy and collaborates with WHO on health‑related issues.
- Distilleries must obtain licenses, adhere to strict production protocols, and maintain traceability of ingredients.
- Minimum aging period is 3 years; alcohol content must be at least 20% ABV for standard whisky.
- Additives are limited; only specific enzymes or clarifying agents are allowed under regulation.
- Labeling must include producer name, origin, age statement, and proof.
- Exporters must comply with both Japanese and destination country regulations.
