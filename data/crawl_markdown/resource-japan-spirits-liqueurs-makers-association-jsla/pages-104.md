# Japan Spirits & Liqueurs Makers Association (JSLA) – Production Process and Regulatory Overview

This page provides a comprehensive overview of the Japanese whisky production process, the legal framework governing whisky manufacturing, and the role of the JSLA. It covers:
- The basic steps of whisky production (mashing, fermentation, distillation, maturation, bottling).
- Key terminology used in the industry.
- Chemical processes such as esterification, oxidation, and aging.
- Regulatory aspects: licensing, quality control, labeling, safety, and environmental compliance.
- The JSLA’s responsibilities for information dissemination, statistical reporting, and cooperation with international bodies like WHO.

## Distillery Facts
- Production involves mashing of malted barley, fermentation in tanks, distillation (pot or column), maturation in oak casks, and bottling.
- Age statements are legally required on labels.
- Distilleries must register with the Ministry of Finance and comply with safety and environmental regulations.

## Resource Facts
- JSLA provides public information on whisky production processes, terminology, and regulatory requirements.
- The association collaborates with WHO for health-related inquiries about alcohol consumption.
- Statistical data on whisky manufacturing is collected and reported by JSLA.
