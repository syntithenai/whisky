# Japan Spirits & Liqueurs Makers Association (JSLA) – Regulatory Overview

The page is a comprehensive overview of the Japanese laws and regulations that govern the production, labeling, marketing, and distribution of alcoholic beverages in Japan. It covers:
- **Legal framework**: National and local statutes, industry associations, and international agreements.
- **Production standards**: Requirements for raw materials, distillation processes, aging, and packaging.
- **Labeling & advertising rules**: Mandatory information on labels, age statements, alcohol content, and prohibited claims.
- **Enforcement & compliance**: Inspection procedures, penalties, and industry self‑regulation mechanisms.
- **Industry organization**: Roles of the JSLA, local associations, and other stakeholders.

The page is primarily a reference for producers, importers, and regulators rather than consumers.

---

## Key Points
1. **Legal basis** – The Alcoholic Beverage Control Law (酒類管理法) and related regulations set the framework.
2. **Production requirements** – Distillation must use approved raw materials; aging periods are specified for certain categories.
3. **Labeling** – Labels must include alcohol content, age statement, country of origin, and other mandatory data.
4. **Advertising restrictions** – Prohibits misleading claims and requires compliance with the Alcoholic Beverage Advertising Guidelines.
5. **Enforcement** – Regular inspections by local authorities; penalties for non‑compliance.
6. **Industry bodies** – JSLA coordinates standards and represents producers in policy discussions.
