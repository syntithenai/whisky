# The Magical History of the Great Brora Distillery
The page recounts how George Granville Leveson‑Gower, Marquess of Stafford, established a purpose‑built distillery near Clynelish farm in 1820 to give cleared Highland farmers a market for their grain. The venture integrated farming, piggery and distillation, using local coal and peat. Over time the site evolved under the Lawson family, who expanded the stills, built a new malt kiln, and shifted to private‑customer sales, making Clynelish whisky highly sought after.

Key points:
- Original stills: 200‑gal wash, 87‑gal spirit.
- Output ~10 000 gal/yr; duty paid £2 774 in 1821‑22.
- Lawson partnership (1834) added capital, improved facilities, and focused on private trade.
- Alfred Barnard’s 1886 visit described a two‑acre complex with peat drying, water cistern, and new pot stills heated by coal.
- Annual output rose to ~20 000 gal; whisky sent duty‑paid to private customers nationwide.
