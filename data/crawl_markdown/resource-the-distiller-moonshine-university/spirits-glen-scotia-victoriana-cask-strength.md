# Glen Scotia Victoriana Cask Strength
- **Type**: Single Malt Scotch Whisky (Campbeltown)
- **ABV**: 54.2%
- **Finish**: 30 % Pedro Ximénez sherry butts, 70 % heavily charred American oak (12 months)
- **Tasting notes**: Fruity, floral, oily, faint furniture‑polish aroma; spicy palate with charred oak, saline quality, vegetal peat.
- **Rating**: 3.93/5 from 96 community reviews.

## Key facts
- Campbeltown’s Victorian heritage inspired the name.
- Distilled in pot stills from 100 % malted barley.
- Aged in Pedro Ximénez sherry butts and heavily charred American oak casks.

## Reviews
> *"The Glen Scotia Victoriana is rather fruity and slightly floral, with a nice oiliness to boot and just a whiff of furniture polish. The palate is darker than expected and certainly much more spicier than the nose suggests. It's funky with notes of charred oak, a soft saline quality and a touch of vegetal peat. Clearly this is about heavy oak maturation—but done very well."* – Thijs Klaverstijn (Score 89)
