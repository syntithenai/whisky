# The TTB Story

The Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau (TTB) traces its roots back to the early days of the United States Treasury, beginning with the first excise tax on distilled spirits in 1791. Established formally in January 2003 after a reorganization of the Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco and Firearms under the Homeland Security Act of 2002, TTB’s mission is to collect excise taxes, protect consumers through compliance programs, and assist industry members with federal regulations.

Key points:
- **Historical lineage**: Treasury collected alcohol taxes since 1791; involved in Whiskey Rebellion, Civil War financing, Prohibition enforcement.
- **Modern role**: Over 500 employees across Washington, D.C. and Cincinnati, Ohio; staff includes analysts, chemists, investigators, auditors, legal and IT specialists.
- **Current initiatives**: Electronic label processing for beer, wine, spirits; e‑government services to improve efficiency and transparency.
- **Future focus**: Strengthening partnerships with industry stakeholders, advocacy groups, and the public while maintaining regulatory effectiveness.
