# Geographe Wine Region Overview

- **Location**: Wraps around Geographe Bay in Western Australia, extending from Harvey northwards through Capel to Busselton and eastwards to Donnybrook and Ferguson Valley.
- **History**: Named after the French explorer Baudin's ship *Geographe*, which first mapped the coastline.
- **Size & Diversity**: Home to 57 growers, 26 producers, and 36 different grape varieties, making it the most diverse wine region in Western Australia.

# Key Facts
- **Varietals**: Extensive range of varietals including wines from around the world.
- **Climate**: Golden sunlight-filled mornings and crisp afternoons contribute to ideal growing conditions.
- **Producers**: 26 producers contribute to the region's vibrant wine scene.

# Production Signals
- **Harvest Season**: Typically occurs between February and April, aligning with the warmer months in Western Australia.
- **Winemaking Techniques**: Emphasis on sustainable practices and traditional winemaking methods to preserve the unique terroir of Geographe Bay.

# Commercial Signals
- **Award Recognition**: Regular participation in wine competitions with notable mentions in the WA Good Food Guide Wine Awards.
- **Export Focus**: Increasing export presence, particularly to key markets in Asia and Europe.

# Risks / Unknowns
- **Climate Change Impact**: Potential challenges from shifting weather patterns affecting grape quality and harvest timing.
- **Market Competition**: Growing competition from other emerging Australian wine regions requiring continuous innovation.
