**The 1823 Excise Act**

- Passed on 18 July 1823, the Act set a £10 licensing fee for stills and duty levels for distilled spirits.
- It allowed warehousing of spirits before duty payment and curtailed tax evasion.
- Aimed to level competition, support Scottish grain farmers, and ensure consistent quality.
- The Act is 52 pages long and laid the regulatory foundation that helped Scotch Whisky become the UK’s largest food‑and‑drink export (worth £6.2 bn in 2022) and a major global brand.

The SWA commemorated its 200th anniversary by interviewing Arthur Motley of *The Liquid Antiquarian* about the Act’s lasting influence on quality, success and worldwide renown.
