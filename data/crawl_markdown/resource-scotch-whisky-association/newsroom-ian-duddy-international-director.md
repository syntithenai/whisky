# Scotch Whisky Association appoints former UK Ambassador Ian Duddy as International Director

The Scotch Whisky Association (SWA) has announced that **Ian Duddy**, a former UK ambassador with over 25 years of experience in senior government roles, will serve as its new International Director. Duddy’s background includes postings to Argentina, Afghanistan, the United Nations in Geneva, Uruguay (2016‑2020), and Chile (2020‑2021). He has also led the Scottish COVID‑19 Inquiry and worked on trade policy, EU/UN negotiations, national security, human rights, and intellectual property.

In his new role Duddy will lead SWA’s international engagement, aiming to improve market access for Scotch whisky across more than 160 markets. The appointment comes at a time when the industry faces tariff challenges in the United States but sees growth potential in China and India. Mark Kent, SWA Chief Executive, praised Duddy’s diplomatic expertise as vital for navigating a volatile global environment.

The SWA represents over 90 member companies and focuses on sustainable production, global trade, responsible consumption, and policy advocacy.
