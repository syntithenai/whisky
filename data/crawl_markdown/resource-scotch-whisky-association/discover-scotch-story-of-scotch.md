# Story of Scotch

The page traces the history of Scotch whisky from its earliest recorded distillation in 1494 to modern times, covering key milestones such as:
- Early illicit distilling and tax evasion.
- The 1823 Excise Act that legalized production.
- Invention of the Patent Still (1831) and rise of grain whisky.
- Global expansion by major brands in the 19th‑20th centuries.
- Formation of the Scotch Whisky Association (1912, renamed 1942).
- Impact of Prohibition, WWII, and the COVID‑19 pandemic.
- Current legal definition and export statistics.

It highlights cultural icons like Robert Burns and Churchill, and notes the industry’s role in supporting public health during crises.
