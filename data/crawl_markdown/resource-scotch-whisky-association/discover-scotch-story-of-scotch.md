# Story of Scotch

The page traces the history of Scotch whisky from its earliest recorded distillation in 1494 to modern times, highlighting key milestones such as:
- Early illicit distilling and tax evasion.
- The 1823 Excise Act that legalized production.
- Invention of the Patent Still (1831) leading to grain whisky.
- Global expansion by major brands in the 19th‑20th centuries.
- Formation of the Scotch Whisky Association (1912, renamed 1942).
- Legal protection and regulations (1933, 1988 Act, 2009 Regulations).
- The industry’s response to COVID‑19 with large‑scale ethanol production.

It concludes with current export figures (over £6 bn in 2022) and the SWA’s role in safeguarding Scotch whisky worldwide.
