# Story of Scotch

## Key Facts
- Scotch Whisky is a product of quality, crafted in Scotland with a unique heritage stretching back over 500 years.
- The earliest documented record of distilling in Scotland occurred in 1494.
- Illicit stills and smuggling were common practices due to taxes on Scotch whisky.
- The Excise Act of 1823 sanctioned the legal production of whisky, leading to the rise of modern distilleries.

## Production Signals
- Distillation began with malt whisky using pot stills, evolving to include grain whisky after Aeneas Coffey invented the Patent Still in 1831.
- Grain whisky is a lighter spirit blended with more potent malts for wider market appeal.

## Commercial Signals
- Scotch Whisky's global expansion was driven by entrepreneurs like James Buchanan and Tommy Dewar who took it to the British Empire and beyond.
- The phylloxera beetle in France led Scots to export Scotch instead of brandy, capitalizing on the demand for a substitute spirit.

## Risks/Unknowns
- While legal protection has grown, counterfeiting and illicit distilling remain concerns.
- Changes in consumer preferences and competition from other spirits could impact future growth.
