# Scotch Whisky Association – Community Alcohol Partnerships

The page explains the partnership between the Scotch Whisky Association (SWA) and Community Alcohol Partnerships (CAP). Since 2013, SWA has funded projects to reduce alcohol‑related harm in Scotland through its Scotch Whisky Action Fund (SWAF). In 2024 a new £300 000 three‑year grant was announced to support CAP initiatives aimed at curbing underage drinking. The page also provides background on the SWAF, its funding history, and links to specific regional projects.

---

## Key facts
- **SWA**: Scottish trade body for Scotch whisky producers.
- **CAP**: Community interest company focused on reducing alcohol harm among children and young people.
- **Funding announced (2024)**: £300 000 over three years via SWAF to establish CAPs in high‑harm areas.
- **SWAF history**: Launched 2013, committed £100 k annually 2014‑23, awarded 89 projects totaling £1.17 m, supporting >66 k people.
- **Regional projects**: Highlights include Highlands & North East, Glasgow & Strathclyde, Argyll & Bute, Borders & Ayrshire, Edinburgh & the Lothians.
