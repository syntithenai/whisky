**Scotch Whisky Association responds to rise in excise duty**

The Scotch Whisky Association (SWA) has condemned the Chancellor’s decision to increase alcohol duty, arguing that it will further strain an industry already facing job losses and stalled investment. The SWA had previously urged a freeze on duty to support growth. The latest 3.65 % rise in spirits duty is said to have cut spirits revenue by 7 %, costing the Treasury £150 m. Mark Kent, SWA Chief Executive, warned that continued tax hikes will hurt distillers, hospitality venues, farmers and suppliers, ultimately stifling economic growth.

---

**Key points:**
- Duty increase announced on 26 Nov 2025 by Chancellor Rachel Reeves.
- SWA calls for duty freeze to protect jobs and investment.
- Spirits revenue fell 7 % after last year’s 3.6 % duty rise, a £150 m loss to the Treasury.
- OBR downgrades alcohol revenue forecast by £1.7 bn per year due to high tax burden.
- SWA stresses need for “breathing room” for Scottish industry.
