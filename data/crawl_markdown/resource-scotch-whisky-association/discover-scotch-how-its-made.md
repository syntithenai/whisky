# How Scotch Whisky is Made

Scotch whisky is made from just three simple ingredients: water, yeast and cereals. The process transforms these basic elements into a spirit full of character, influenced by the Scottish environment and crafted with skills honed over 500 years.

## Key Facts
- **Ingredients**: Water, malted barley (and other cereals), yeast
- **Production Steps**: Mashing, Fermentation, Distillation, Maturation
- **Regulation**: Strict rules govern what can be called 'Scotch whisky'
- **History**: Over 500 years of tradition and craftsmanship

## Production Signals
1. **Mashing**: Crushed barley is mixed with hot water to release sugars
2. **Fermentation**: Yeast converts sugars into alcohol, creating wash
3. **Distillation**: Wash is distilled twice in pot stills, producing new make spirit
4. **Maturation**: Spirit ages in oak casks for a minimum of three years

## Commercial Signals
- Distillery-specific flavors influenced by location and wood type
- Aging period affects flavor profile (e.g., longer maturation = smoother)
- Blending of different distillates to achieve desired taste

## Risks / Unknowns
- Environmental impact of water usage in distillation
- Climate change affecting barley growth and peat availability
- Future regulations on whisky production methods
