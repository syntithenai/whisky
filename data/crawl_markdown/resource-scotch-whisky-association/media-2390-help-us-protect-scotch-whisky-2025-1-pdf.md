# Help us protect Scotch Whisky

The Scottish Whisky Association (SWA) urges the public to report any whisky that may be misrepresented as Scotch Whisky. The document explains what constitutes genuine Scotch Whisky, how it can be illegally marketed, and lists specific warning signs such as false age claims, misleading brand names or labels, and the use of descriptors like “Scotch type” on non‑Scotch spirits.

The SWA maintains a register of verified brands via HM Revenue & Customs and invites consumers to notify them of suspicious products. The page also highlights common tactics used by local producers to pass off their whisky as Scotch, including Scottish‑themed branding and tartan imagery.
