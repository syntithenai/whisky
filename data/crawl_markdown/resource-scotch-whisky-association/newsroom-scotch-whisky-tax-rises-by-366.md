# Industry stakeholders urge fairer tax treatment of Scotch Whisky

The Scottish Chambers of Commerce, the Scotch Whisky All‑Party Parliamentary Group, InchDairnie Distillery and the Scotch Whisky Association (SWA) have called for a more balanced excise duty regime following a **3.66 % rise in spirits duty** effective 1 Feb 2026. The increase adds to an 18 % cumulative rise over three years, pushing the total tax on a standard 70 cl bottle from £10.58 (2023) to £12.45 (2026). Stakeholders argue that higher duties stifle growth, hurt supply‑chain jobs and tourism, and reduce public revenue.

Key points:
- Duty rise is the third in less than three years.
- 70 % of UK spirits are produced in Scotland; the impact is disproportionately felt there.
- The SWA stresses that a fair duty system would support investment, export opportunities and overall economic prosperity.
- The government’s upcoming excise review is seen as an opportunity to address these concerns.
