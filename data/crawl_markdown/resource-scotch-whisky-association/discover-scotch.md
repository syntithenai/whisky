# Discover Scotch Whisky

## Key Facts
- Scotland's national drink, crafted with quality and a unique heritage stretching back over 500 years
- Over 150 malt and grain distilleries across the country
- Three simple natural ingredients: water, barley, and yeast

## Production Signals
- Distillation process involves heating fermented wash to separate alcohol from other components
- Maturation in oak casks for a minimum of three years, allowing flavors to develop
- Quality control measures ensure consistency and adherence to Scotch Whisky regulations

## Commercial Signals
- Over 140 operating distilleries producing thousands of expressions
- Growing demand worldwide, with Scotland being the second-largest whisky exporter
- Distinct regional styles influenced by geography, climate, and production methods

## Risks/Unknowns
- Climate change may impact peat bogs used for drying malted barley
- Regulatory changes in importing countries could affect Scotch Whisky trade
- Competition from other global spirits markets
