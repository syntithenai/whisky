# Protecting Scotch Whisky

The page explains why the Scotch Whisky Association (SWA) protects the brand, outlines the legal framework that defines what constitutes Scotch whisky, and lists guidance documents for producers, bottlers and investors. It highlights recent legal victories in South Korea, the Philippines and Hong Kong, and provides links to downloadable PDFs on cask regulations, verification schemes and investment guidance.

## Key points
- **Legal protection**: The SWA’s Legal Affairs team defends against passing‑off, counterfeit products and unfair competition worldwide.
- **Definition of Scotch whisky**: Must be made from cereals, water and yeast; matured in oak casks (≤700 L) for at least 3 years; bottled ≥40% ABV; distilled below 94.8% ABV; no flavouring or sweetening.
- **Five categories** of Scotch whisky are defined by UK law.
- **Guidance documents**: *Scotch Whisky Regulations 2009* (producers & bottlers), Q&A on allowable casks, personal investment in a cask, verification scheme, and help‑us‑protect guide.
- **Recent wins**: GI protection in the Philippines; trademark protection in Hong Kong and U.S.; successful enforcement against fake Scotch whisky in South Korea.
