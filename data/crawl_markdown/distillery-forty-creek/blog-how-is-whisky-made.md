# How Is Canadian Whisky Made? – Forty Creek Blog

Forty Creek explains the full whisky‑making journey from grain selection to blending, highlighting its use of both pot and column stills, careful mashing, fermentation, distillation, aging in wooden barrels, and a meticulous blending process led by Master Blender Bill Ashburn. The article also touches on Canadian whisky regulations that allow flexibility in grain mix, distillation methods, and the inclusion of up to 9 % wine or spirits.

## Key Points
- **Grains**: corn, wheat, rye, malted/unmalted barley.
- **Mashing & Fermentation**: enzymes convert starches to sugars; yeast ferments for several days.
- **Distillation**: pot and column stills produce different flavor profiles.
- **Aging**: wooden barrels (new or used) in Canada’s climate add depth.
- **Blending**: Master Blender Bill Ashburn balances aged whiskies for complexity.
- **Regulations**: Canadian law requires at least 3 years aging, allows up to 9.09 % wine/spirits and caramel color.

The post showcases how science, art, and regulation combine to create Forty Creek’s distinctive Canadian whisky.
