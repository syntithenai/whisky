# What Grain Is Used to Make Whisky

Forty Creek’s blog post explains how the choice of grain shapes whisky flavour across styles—from Japanese and Irish to Scotch, Bourbon, and Canadian whiskies. It details the main grains (barley, wheat, rye, corn) and their characteristic flavours, then focuses on Forty Creek’s own grain blend: high‑quality corn, wheat, rye, and barley milled into coarse flour for mashing, fermentation, distillation, and aging in wooden barrels.

The article concludes that careful grain selection is key to producing a smooth, complex Canadian whisky.
