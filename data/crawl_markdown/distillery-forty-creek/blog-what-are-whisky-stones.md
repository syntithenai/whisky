# What Are Whisky Stones? The Forty Creek Guide

Forty Creek explains whisky stones—cooling stones that chill a spirit without dilution. The article covers their history from ancient Egypt to modern craft whisky, the materials used (soapstone, stainless steel, granite), and how they affect temperature and flavour. It also promotes the brand’s own stone sets and highlights the importance of temperature control for complex Canadian whiskies such as Cherrywood Reserve.

---

## Key Take‑aways
- **Purpose**: Chill whisky while avoiding dilution.
- **Materials**: Soapstone (best cold retention), stainless steel (modern, often with gel), granite (durable but can damage glass if dropped).
- **History**: From stone jars in Egypt to modern craft whisky accessories.
- **Impact on flavour**: Cooler temperatures can unlock new notes in complex whiskies.
- **Practical use**: Freeze stones, add 2‑3 per glass, pour whisky, adjust by removing stones if too cold.
- **Cleaning**: Warm water and cloth; avoid dishwasher for natural stones.
