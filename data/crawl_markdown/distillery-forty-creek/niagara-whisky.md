# Niagara Whisky – Forty Creek

Forty Creek’s **Niagara Whisky** celebrates the region that inspired Canada’s first craft distillery. The page explains the whisky’s heritage, its place in Canadian whisky history, and how the brand honours Niagara’s terroir.

- Origin: Niagara Peninsula, Canada
- Focus: Craft whisky with a “relentless spirit” narrative
- Highlights: Historical context of Canadian whisky, mash‑bill flexibility, barrel variety, and a botanical variant called *The Forager*.
- Call to action: Shop online (Ontario only) or visit the distillery for tastings.

---

## Key facts
- Niagara is known for winemaking, brewing, and distilling.
- Forty Creek pays homage to its roots by producing a whisky that reflects local terroir.
- Canadian whisky historically uses diverse grains (corn, wheat, rye) and barrels (oak, maple, wine‑cask).
- The Forager is the world’s first botanical Canadian whisky, steeped with wild botanicals for 48 h before aging in virgin oak casks.

### Glossary terms
- **Terroir** – the environmental factors that affect a crop’s phenotype.
- **Mash bill** – the grain mixture used for fermentation.
- **Cask strength** – whisky bottled at the strength it was aged in.
- **Botanical whisky** – whisky infused with botanicals, similar to gin.

## Source Excerpts
- Foraged our own botanicals Forager botanical whisky The world's first botanical Canadian whisky, The Forager is crafted by steeping a 4-year-old Candian whisky for 48 hours with wild botanicals foraged from the Candian wilderness and aged in virgin oak casks.
- This playful sensibility continues to flavour the rich history of Canadian whisky today.
- German and Dutch farmers brought the earthy, malty flavour of rye, which they added to their whisky mash to impart the spicy, fruity notes that are distinctive of Canadian rye whisky.
- The result of this unique alchemy is a botanical whisky and gin substitute noted for its medley of flavours including toffee, herbal tea, and a long peppery finish.
