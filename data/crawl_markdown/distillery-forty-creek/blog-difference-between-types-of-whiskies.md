# Differences Among Bourbon, Scotch, Irish, and Canadian Whiskies
Forty Creek explains the key legal and stylistic distinctions between four major whisky categories:
- **Bourbon** – U.S.‑made, ≥51% corn, new charred oak barrels, at least 2 years for “straight” bourbon.
- **Scotch** – Scottish origin, aged ≥3 years, primarily malted barley; includes single‑malt, blended, etc.
- **Irish whiskey** – Irish production, triple‑distilled, aged ≥3 years, can be peated or unpeated.
- **Canadian whisky** – Canadian‑made, aged ≥3 years in small wooden vessels, ≥40% ABV, may include caramel/flavouring.
The post also notes spelling conventions (whiskey vs. whisky) and promotes Forty Creek’s tasting flight sets.
