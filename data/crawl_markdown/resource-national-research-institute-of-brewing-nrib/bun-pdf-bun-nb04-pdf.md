# Summary of NRIB Document 4 – “合成清澈” (Synthetic Clarity)

The PDF outlines experimental procedures for measuring the solubility of nitrogen in water under controlled temperature and pH conditions. It references earlier sections (3‑1 to 3‑18) for general methodology, then details specific protocols for two types of experiments:

* **4‑2** – *Nitrogen Solubility by Standard Procedure*
  * Trial 4‑2‑1 follows section 3‑6‑1.
  * Trial 4‑2‑2 involves heating a 10 mL water sample from 5 °C to 35 °C, measuring pH with an automated sensor, and then adjusting the solution to pH 8.2 using N/10 water‑nitrogen solution. A further 5 mL of medium‑temperature water is added before re‑adjusting to pH 8.2.

* **4‑3** – *Nitrogen Solubility by Alternative Procedure*
  * Trial 4‑3‑1 follows section 3‑5‑1.
  * Trial 4‑3‑2 uses a 100 mL water sample, splits it into two portions, heats them from 5 °C to 35 °C, and then adds the 10 mL test sample. After a 1‑minute heating period, the temperature is measured; after 30 seconds of cooling, the sample is placed in a 5‑minute interval before further cooling.
  * Trial 4‑3‑3 measures pH with an automated sensor and adjusts to pH 7.2 using N/10 water‑nitrogen solution.

All measurements are expressed as millimoles per liter (a mL or b mL) following the same formulae as sections 3‑6‑2 and 3‑5‑2, respectively. The document notes that increasing the sample volume does not affect the results significantly.

---

**Key Takeaways**: Temperature control, pH adjustment, and precise volume handling are critical for accurate nitrogen solubility measurements in aqueous solutions.
