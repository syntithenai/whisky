# 101 Water for Whisky Production
This document (JIS K 0094:1994) outlines laboratory methods for preparing and analysing water used in whisky production. It covers sampling, physical‑chemical testing (pH, turbidity, dissolved solids), preparation of test solutions with various salts and acids, and detailed analytical procedures such as UV‑vis spectroscopy, ion chromatography, and gravimetric determination of metal ions. The text is organized into numbered sections (101‑1 to 101‑17) each describing a specific test or procedure, including reagent preparations, sample volumes, temperature controls, and calculation formulas for concentration.

- Sampling follows JIS K 0094:1994.
- Physical tests: pH, turbidity, dissolved solids (mg L⁻¹).
- Preparation of standard solutions (e.g., NaCl 30 % w/w, CaCl₂·2H₂O, EDTA, etc.).
- Analytical methods: UV‑vis absorbance at specific wavelengths, ion chromatography for cations/anions, gravimetric determination of metals.
- Calculation formulas are provided for converting absorbance to concentration and for determining metal content from titration data.
