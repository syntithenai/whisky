# Analysis of Japanese Fermentation Agents

The page is a research note from the **National Research Institute of Brewing (NRIB)** that discusses the *scientific names* and *classification* of two traditional Japanese fermentation starters:

1. **Koji** – historically used in Japan for sake, miso, etc.
2. **Kombu koji** – a fermented product from the island of Okinawa.

Key points include:
- Koji was originally classified as *Aspergillus oryzae* (sometimes *A. sojae*) by Japanese brewing associations.
- Kombu koji has been identified as *Aspergillus luchuensis*, distinct from *A. niger*.
