# List of Standard English Expressions for Sake Terminology (Sake Terms trial English edition)

This PDF from the National Research Institute of Brewing provides a comprehensive glossary of Japanese sake‑making terminology translated into standard English. It includes definitions, Romanized spellings, and brief descriptions for terms related to ingredients, processes, equipment, and product categories.

Key highlights:
- **Alcohol & Dilution** – Definitions of ABV, full‑strength (genshu), and dilution practices.
- **Rice & Koji** – Terms for different rice cultivars, polishing ratios, and koji production.
- **Fermentation & Starter Cultures** – Kimoto, shubo, and other starter culture names with process notes.
- **Product Types** – Genshu, ginjo‑shu, honjozo‑shu, kijoshu, and others.
- **Equipment & Techniques** – Pressing (shibori), filtering (oribiki), and cask opening (kagami‑biraki).

The glossary is organized alphabetically by English term, with Japanese characters and Hepburn Romanization provided for each entry.
