# Japan National Tax Agency – Whisky Labelling Standards (PDF 0022009‑067)

This document outlines the **Japanese tax regulations governing the use of whisky in public sector activities**. It specifies:

1. **Tax‑exempt usage rates** for whisky in government events and the criteria for maintaining those rates.
2. **Procedures for approving and monitoring whisky consumption** by civil servants, including annual reporting requirements and audit mechanisms.
3. **Special provisions for different categories of public employees** (e.g., senior officials, teachers, military personnel) and how their status affects eligibility.
4. **Administrative responsibilities** for local governments, ministries, and other agencies in enforcing the rules.
5. **Penalties and enforcement measures** for non‑compliance.

The PDF is a legal reference used by Japanese public institutions to ensure compliance with tax law when whisky is served at official functions.

---

## Key Points
- **Tax‑exempt rate**: 2.70 % (as of 2016‑08‑11) for approved events.
- **Approval process**: Annual reporting, audit by the Ministry of Finance, and documentation of event details.
- **Eligibility**: Applies to civil servants, teachers, military personnel, etc., with specific criteria per category.
- **Monitoring**: Regular audits, record‑keeping, and penalties for violations.

---

## Practical Implications
Public institutions must:
1. Submit annual reports on whisky usage.
2. Maintain detailed logs of events where whisky is served.
3. Ensure that only approved personnel consume whisky at official functions.
4. Undergo audits by the Ministry of Finance to verify compliance.
5. Face penalties if they exceed the allowed consumption or fail to report accurately.
