# Japan National Tax Agency – Whisky Labelling Standards

This PDF outlines the Japanese tax regulations governing the import and sale of whisky, focusing on how customs duties are assessed and when they can be paid in advance. Key points include:
- **Advance payment** is allowed only under specific circumstances (e.g., to support business continuity) and must be requested through a formal application.
- The duty is calculated based on the *original* value of the whisky, limited to a 5‑year period, with possible extensions up to 2 years in special cases.
- **No duty** may be required if the importation is for personal use or certain exempt categories (e.g., gifts, small quantities).
- The document references Article 151 of the Customs Duties Act and Article 46 of the Customs Law as legal bases.

The text provides procedural guidance on how to apply for duty exemptions, the conditions under which they are granted, and the documentation required.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. **Advance Duty Payment** – Allowed only when justified by business continuity or other specific reasons; requires a formal application.
2. **Duty Calculation Period** – Original value within 5 years; extensions up to 2 years are possible under special circumstances.
3. **Exemptions** – Personal use, gifts, and small quantities may be exempt from duty.
4. **Legal Basis** – Articles 151 (Customs Duties Act) and 46 (Customs Law).

---

## Practical Implications for Importers
- Prepare detailed application forms and supporting documents if you need to pay duty in advance.
- Keep records of the original value and any changes that might extend the duty period.
- Verify whether your shipment falls under an exemption category before proceeding with payment.
