# Summary of NRIB Document 103 – 原料分析

This document outlines the analytical procedures used in the National Research Institute of Brewing (NRIB) for various raw materials and process parameters. It references earlier sections (e.g., 102‑1, 102‑2) for detailed methods and provides specific calculation formulas for determining concentrations such as **酵母活性** (yeast activity), **水分** (water content), and **酵素パラメータ** (enzyme parameters). The procedures include:

- Sampling protocols (e.g., 103‑1, 103‑2)
- Weight‐based determinations of total solids, ash, volatile matter, non‑volatile matter, and other components (103‑4)
- Water content measurement (103‑5)
- Denaturation tests for enzymes (103‑6)
- Calculation of **酵素パラメータ** using the formula 
  
```
酵素パラメータ(%) = (a – b) × F0.28 × 6.25 / 試料採取重量(g)
```

The document also describes a detailed procedure for measuring **発酵率** and **発酵残量** in fermentation experiments, including temperature control, sampling intervals, and calculation of yield percentages.

## Key Points
- The methods are built upon the protocols described in sections 102‑1 to 102‑14.
- Weight‐based calculations are used for most determinations.
- Enzyme activity is assessed via a denaturation test (103‑6).
- Fermentation performance metrics are calculated from mass balances over time.

## Practical Use
Researchers and brewers can use these procedures to standardize raw material analysis, monitor fermentation progress, and evaluate the impact of process variables on product quality.
