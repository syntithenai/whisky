# Overview
The PDF (NB‑03) from the National Research Institute of Brewing is a technical manual that outlines standard procedures for sampling, measuring, and analysing whisky (Japanese whisky). It covers:
- **Sampling**: 600 mL sample volume, use of clean containers.
- **Physical tests**: density, colour, aroma assessment.
- **Density measurement**: two methods – the *water‑bath* method (3‑3‑A) and the *refractive‑index* method (3‑3‑B), both performed at 15 °C. The refractive‑index calculation uses the formula 
  
  τ = (ρ(15/4 °C) – 0.99997)/ρ(15/4 °C)
- **Alcohol content determination**: by distillation and by refractive index.
- **Distillation apparatus**: description of condenser, reflux ratio, temperature control, and sampling points.
- **Analytical methods for flavour compounds**: gas‑chromatography (GC), high‑performance liquid chromatography (HPLC), spectrophotometry, and specific tests for sulphur compounds, acids, esters, etc.
- **Standardisation**: references to JIS B 7548:2009, JIS K 8809:2007, and other Japanese standards.
- **Safety & quality control**: handling of volatile solvents, calibration procedures, and documentation requirements.

The document is highly technical and intended for laboratory personnel involved in whisky research or quality assurance.
