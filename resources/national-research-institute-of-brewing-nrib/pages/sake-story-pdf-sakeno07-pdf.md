## Summary Text (plain English)
The NRIB PDF No. 7 explains how Japanese law classifies three types of sake‑derived alcohols—mirin, kyojō‑seki, and koshiki—and describes their production methods, typical alcohol contents, and uses in food and industry. Mirin is a sweet, low‑proof spirit made by fermenting rice with yeast and a small amount of added alcohol (≈13–14 % ABV). Kyojō‑seki is a neutral spirit produced from fermented rice with added alcohol or distilled sake, used mainly as a cooking ingredient. Koshiki is a high‑proof distilled spirit (>60 % ABV) made by distilling fermented sake and is employed in industrial processes, flavoring, and as a solvent. The report also covers the historical development of these spirits, their regulatory status, and their roles in food preservation, flavor enhancement, and chemical production.
