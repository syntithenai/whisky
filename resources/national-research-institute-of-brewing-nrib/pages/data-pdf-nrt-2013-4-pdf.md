# The scientific name of shōchū became *Aspergillus luchuensis*

The PDF reports that the traditional Japanese distilled spirit **shōchū** was historically classified as *Aspergillus awamori*. In 2013, researchers at the National Research Institute of Brewing (NRIB) clarified that the correct species is actually *Aspergillus luchuensis*, distinct from *A. niger* and *A. awamori*.

The study was published in **PLoS ONE** (volume 8, e63769). It also notes that *A. niger* has been used in Kyoto for producing kenuji (a type of fermented rice), but the shōchū strain is a separate species.
