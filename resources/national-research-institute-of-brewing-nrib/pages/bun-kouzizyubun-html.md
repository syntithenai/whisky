# Koji (Yellow and Black) Fermentation Analysis

The National Research Institute of Brewing (NRIB) provides a brief overview of the two main types of *koji* used in Japanese fermentation: **yellow koji** (*Aspergillus oryzae* / *A. sojae*) and **black koji** (*Aspergillus luchuensis*, formerly *A. niger*). The page references academic papers that confirm these taxonomic identifications and notes the importance of koji in Japanese food culture.

- **Yellow koji**: historically used for sake, miso, soy sauce; classified as *A. oryzae* (or *A. sojae*) by the Japan Society of Brewing Science.
- **Black koji**: derived from *A. luchuensis*, distinct from *A. niger*; identified in a 2013 study.

The page also mentions that NRIB is involved in protecting the intellectual property of brewing microorganisms and provides contact details for further inquiries.
