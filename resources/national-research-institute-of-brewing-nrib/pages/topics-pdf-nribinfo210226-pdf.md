# NRIB Sulphite Analysis Methods

The National Research Institute of Brewing (NRIB) released a technical note on **sulphite analysis methods** for wine and beer, dated 26 Feb 2021. The document reviews the standard analytical procedures used by the Japanese Brewers Association, discusses common pitfalls, and compares different measurement techniques such as the *U.S. method* and the *Ripper method*. It also examines how sulphite concentration is affected by adding 25 % sulphite to a 15 mL sample and highlights discrepancies observed when using white wine versus other matrices.

Key points:
- Standardised methods for measuring SO₂ in alcoholic beverages.
- Practical issues encountered during routine analysis.
- Comparative results of different analytical approaches.
- Observations on the influence of added sulphite on measurement accuracy.

The note serves as a reference for brewers and analysts seeking to ensure compliance with Japanese regulations regarding sulphite content.
