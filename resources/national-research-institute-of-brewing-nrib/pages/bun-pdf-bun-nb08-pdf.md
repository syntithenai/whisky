# Overview of the NRIB Technical Guide (Section 8)

- **Sampling**: 1 L of whisky is taken, sealed in a proper container and stored.
- **Physical tests**: The sample is cooled to 10–15 °C, then checked for clarity, colour, viscosity and aroma.
- **Chemical analysis**: Uses reagents such as potassium ferricyanide, sodium thiosulfate, copper sulfate, etc. to determine alcohol content, acidity, sulphur compounds and other key parameters.
- **Instrumentation**: Describes a 3‑cube apparatus for sampling, a spectrophotometer for absorbance measurements, and an automated analyser for pH and alcohol calculations.
- **Methodology**: Provides step‑by‑step procedures for preparing reagents, performing the tests, calculating results (e.g., % alcohol from absorbance), and interpreting the data.

The document is a technical manual aimed at researchers or quality control staff working with whisky.
