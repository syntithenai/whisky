# Whisky Science – Overview

Whisky Science is a blog that compiles detailed, science‑based information on Scotch single‑malt whisky, covering its history, production techniques, chemistry, flavour compounds, and regulatory aspects. The site hosts long‑form articles such as *Sugars in whisky*, *History of the column still*, and many others that delve into topics like caramel colouring (E150a), sherry cask usage, pot‑still variations, and steam heating innovations.

The content is written for enthusiasts and professionals alike, with a focus on empirical data, laboratory analyses, and historical context. Each post includes references to scientific literature, tables of measured values, and explanatory diagrams where relevant.

Key themes:
- **Sugar chemistry** – how sugars from oak, sherry casks, and caramel colouring contribute to whisky flavour.
- **Cask science** – extraction rates, effects of toasting/charring, and the influence of previous cask use.
- **Distillation technology** – pot‑still design, steam heating, reflux mechanisms, and historical evolution.
- **Regulatory & terminology** – legal limits on additives, glossary of whisky terms, and chemical nomenclature.

The blog is a valuable resource for researchers, distillers, and connoisseurs seeking evidence‑based insights into whisky production.
