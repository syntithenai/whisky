## Long Description
The American Single Malt Whiskey Commission (ASMWC) is a government‑backed portal that consolidates regulatory information, competition calendars, and educational content for distillers, marketers, and enthusiasts of the newly defined American single malt whiskey category. The site hosts the Code of Federal Regulations entry for the standard of identity, outlining requirements such as 100 % malted barley, single‑distillery production, oak cask maturation not exceeding 700 L, and proof limits. In addition to regulatory details, ASMWC provides a searchable list of spirits competitions with deadlines, entry costs, and member discounts, helping distilleries plan marketing strategies. The resource also offers blog posts, case studies, workshops, and downloadable guides that cover compliance, production best practices, and market positioning for this emerging whiskey segment.

The primary audience includes craft distillers, industry professionals, regulatory bodies, and students of spirits science who need up‑to‑date legal definitions and practical competition information. The site serves as a one‑stop reference for anyone looking to produce or promote American single malt whiskey in compliance with federal standards.
