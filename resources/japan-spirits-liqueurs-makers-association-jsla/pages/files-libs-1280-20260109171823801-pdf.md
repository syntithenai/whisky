# Japanese Alcoholic Beverage Labeling Regulation

This PDF is the official text of Japan’s *Alcoholic Beverage Labeling Regulation* (酒類表示規則). It sets out the legal requirements for how alcoholic drinks—including spirits, wine, beer and other beverages—must be labeled in terms of content, packaging, and information provided to consumers. The regulation specifies:

- **Scope**: Applies to all alcoholic beverages sold in Japan.
- **Labeling elements**: Alcohol content, volume, ingredient list, producer name, batch number, expiration date, and safety warnings.
- **Packaging requirements**: Use of clear or opaque containers, labeling on the front and back, and specific font sizes for key information.
- **Enforcement dates**: The regulation came into force in 2012, with subsequent amendments in 2014 and 2018.
- **Compliance**: Producers must ensure labels meet all stated criteria to avoid penalties.

The document is a legal reference used by manufacturers, importers, retailers, and regulatory bodies.

---

## Key Points
1. **Legal basis** – The regulation is enacted under the *Alcoholic Beverage Control Act* (酒類管理法).
2. **Labeling content** – Mandatory fields include alcohol percentage, volume, ingredients, producer details, batch number, and expiration date.
3. **Packaging** – Labels must be affixed to the container in a legible manner; specific font sizes are prescribed for safety warnings.
4. **Amendments** – The regulation was amended in 2014 (to clarify labeling of non‑alcoholic beverages) and again in 2018 (to update volume units and warning text).
5. **Enforcement** – Non‑compliance can result in fines, product recalls, or market withdrawal.

---

## Practical Implications for Producers
- Verify that all labels contain the required information before shipment.
- Use approved fonts and sizes for safety warnings.
- Keep records of batch numbers and expiration dates for traceability.
- Monitor regulatory updates to ensure ongoing compliance.
