# 2025 North American Barley Crop Report

RahrBSG’s agronomists report that the 2025 barley crop in Canada and the U.S. is **larger and higher‑quality than expected**. Key points:
- Adequate soil moisture early in spring, followed by a dry growing season.
- Late‑season rains were intense but largely saved the crop from drought damage.
- Protein levels are lower and yields up to 140 bushels/acre in Alberta and Western Saskatchewan.
- Minimal chitting and lodging; overall quality is good for malting.

The report suggests maltsters will likely secure a 16‑month supply hedge for 2026.
