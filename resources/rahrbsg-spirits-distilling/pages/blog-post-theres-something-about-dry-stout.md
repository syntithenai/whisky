# There’s Something About Dry Stout

A blog post from RahrBSG that explains the character of dry stout, its history, flavor profile, popular examples (especially Guinness Draught), and a recipe from RahrBSG’s training manager. The article highlights the contrast between dry stouts and sweeter styles, notes regional variations in Ireland, and offers practical brewing details such as malt mix, hop schedule, OG/FG, and carbonation.

## Key Points
- Dry stout is dark but light‑bodied with roasted barley bitterness, coffee‑like aromas, and a creamy head when nitrogenated.
- Popular brands: Guinness Draught, Pfriem, Murphy’s, Beamish, O’Hara’s, Left Hand, Russian River, Three Floyds, Brooklyn.
- Brewing recipe uses 77 % Irish Stout Malt, 9 % roasted barley, 4 % pale brown malt, 10 % Chit malt; 60‑minute hop addition of Hallertauer Magnum (35 IBU).
- Suggested carbonation: 2.4–2.5 vol or mixed gas.
