# Atlantic Puffins: Winter Migration and Conservation

- **Key discovery**: Atlantic puffins (Fratercula arctica) spend the winter in two main areas – first the Gulf of St. Lawrence, then about 200 mi off Cape Cod.
- **Tracking method**: Small geolocators attached to 19 birds released in Maine revealed the migration pattern.
- **Ecological importance**: The wintering grounds are rich in fish (herring, white hake) and support a diverse marine ecosystem including corals, halibut, cod, whales, and seabirds.
- **Conservation concern**: Climate‑driven changes in sea temperature threaten prey species; the area is proposed for protection as a marine national monument to guard against dredging, mining, and drilling.
- **Research leaders**: Steve Kress (National Audubon Society) and Tony Diamond (University of New Brunswick) conducted the studies.

## Take‑away
Understanding puffin wintering behavior informs conservation strategies for both the birds and their marine habitat.
