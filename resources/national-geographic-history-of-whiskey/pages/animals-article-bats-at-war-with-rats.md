# Rats Wage War on Bats

National Geographic reports that invasive brown rats are preying on bats in German caves, a phenomenon also observed in Israeli bat colonies. The study shows rats actively hunting flying bats and storing carcasses, with over 50 dead bats found in one cave. Researchers warn that such predation could threaten already vulnerable bat populations and potentially facilitate disease transmission to humans.

Key points:
- Brown rats (Rattus norvegicus) attack bats in darkness.
- Over 50 dead bats cached by rats in a German cave.
- Similar rat‑bat interactions observed in Israel’s Egyptian fruit bat colony.
- Predation could kill thousands of bats annually, exacerbating threats from habitat loss and disease.
- Recommendations include stronger rodent control at hibernation sites and public awareness campaigns.

The article highlights the complex dynamics between urban rodents and bats, stressing the need for conservation measures to protect these essential pollinators and insect controllers.
