# 100,000 Elephants Killed by Poachers in Just Three Years

National Geographic reports that a recent study estimates **100 000 African elephants** were killed for ivory between 2011 and 2013, with roughly one‑twelve of the continent’s elephants dying each year. The research, published in *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences*, used data from CITES’ MIKE program and population surveys to produce the first continent‑wide quantitative estimate of poaching losses.

Key points:
- Central Africa lost 64 % of its elephant population over a decade.
- Poachers use firearms, poisoned arrows, and mass killings (e.g., 2012 Bouba Ndjidah National Park).
- The study urges policymakers to act on hard numbers rather than anecdotal evidence.

The article also discusses the ecological role of elephants as keystone species and highlights ongoing efforts such as the Great Elephant Census.
