# These 7 Famous Conservationists Also Hunted

National Geographic’s July 30, 2015 article explores how several celebrated conservation figures—most notably **Theodore Roosevelt**, **John James Audubon**, and **Charles Darwin**—also engaged in hunting or killing wildlife for sport, scientific study, or population control. The piece contextualizes their actions within the era’s attitudes toward nature, contrasts them with modern trophy‑hunting controversies (e.g., Cecil the Lion), and discusses how some contemporary conservationists still argue that regulated hunting can fund protection efforts.

The article lists seven individuals, briefly describing each person’s contributions to conservation and the specific ways they hunted or killed animals. It also touches on the broader debate about whether hunters who are also conservationists should be allowed to pursue trophy hunting today.
