# Why Chimp Friends Turned Into Foes in an Unprecedented 'Civil War'

National Geographic reports on the second documented civil war among wild chimpanzees, occurring at Ngogo in Uganda’s Kibale Forest. The once‑unified community split into two factions—Western and Central—leading to violent patrols, multiple deaths, and ongoing conflict. Researchers highlight key factors such as the loss of central individuals, changes in alpha leadership, and respiratory disease outbreaks that destabilized social bonds.

The article also reflects on Jane Goodall’s earlier observations of a similar war at Gombe Stream, underscoring how chimp societies can shift from cooperation to lethal aggression when social structures break down.
