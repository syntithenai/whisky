# Verification of Scotch Whisky

The Scotch Whisky Association (SWA) explains that *Scotch Whisky* is protected as a Geographical Indication (GI) under UK, EU and WTO law. The GI status requires a **Product Specification**—the technical file detailing production and labelling rules—and a **Verification Scheme** run by Her Majesty’s Revenue & Customs (HMRC). HMRC inspects sites every two years and maintains an online lookup service to confirm which facilities are verified for distillation, maturation, blending, bottling or import of bulk Scotch Whisky. Only brands registered with HMRC may be sold; unregistered brands are presumed non‑genuine.

---

## Key Points
- GI status protects against mislabelled whisky and ensures age claims are accurate.
- The Product Specification is published by DEFRA and forms the legal basis for production standards.
- HMRC’s verification scheme covers all stages of production within Scotland and any outside‑Scotland bottling or import operations.
- An online lookup service lists verified sites, brands and importers.

---

## Practical Implications
- Distilleries, maturation houses, blenders, bottlers and overseas bulk importers must apply to HMRC for verification.
- Verification is mandatory before a brand can be placed on the market.
- Unverified or unregistered brands are considered non‑genuine Scotch Whisky.
