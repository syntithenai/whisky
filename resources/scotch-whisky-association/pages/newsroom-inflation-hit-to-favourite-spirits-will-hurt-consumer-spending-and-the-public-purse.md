# Inflation and Alcohol Duty

The Scotch Whisky Association (SWA) reports that alcohol duty has contributed **£3 of every £100** to inflation in the year to August 2025. A 1 % rise in duty would raise government borrowing costs by **£90 m** next year, potentially costing the public purse over **£300 m** if duty is not frozen. The industry argues for a multi‑year freeze on spirits duty to protect consumer spending and support growth ahead of the festive season.

Key points:
- Alcohol accounts for 3 % of CPI inflation.
- Duty increases have reduced Treasury revenue by £600 m versus forecasts.
- Spirits receipts fell 17.1 % in September 2025 vs last year.
- The Scotch whisky sector generates £7.1 bn GVA and supports 66,000 jobs (41k in Scotland).

The SWA urges the Chancellor to halt further duty hikes to ease inflationary pressure on consumers and public finances.
