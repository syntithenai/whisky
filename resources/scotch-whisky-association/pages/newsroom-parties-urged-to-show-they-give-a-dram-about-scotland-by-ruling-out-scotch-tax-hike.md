# Parties urged to show they give a dram about Scotland by ruling out Scotch tax hike

The Scotch Whisky Association (SWA) calls on all UK political parties to **commit to backing the Scotch whisky industry** ahead of the general election. The SWA urges parties to *rule out further increases in excise duty* on Scotch whisky, citing new polling that shows 86 % of Scots believe Scotch is vital to the economy, exports and tourism.

Key points:
- UK excise duty on Scotch remains the highest among G7 nations and more than double the EU average.
- Nearly 73 % of a bottle’s price in the UK is tax.
- The 2023 duty hike cost the Treasury over £115 m, with an additional £100 m lost since the last increase.
- Scotch whisky generates £7.1 bn annually for the UK economy, £5.6 bn in exports and attracts >2 million distillery visitors each year.
- The SWA seeks a reduction of the duty burden to EU levels by the end of the next Parliament.

> *“Spirits like Scotch Whisky are taxed up to four times more than beer and cider. That is unfair, and something the next government must put right.”* – Mark Kent, Chief Executive, SWA
