# Guidance on Allowable Casks for Maturation
This PDF outlines the Scotch Whisky Association’s updated technical file (TF) guidance on which oak casks may be used to mature Scotch whisky. The key points are:
- Only new oak or previously used for wine, fortified wine, beer/ale, spirits (excluding certain prohibited uses) are allowed.
- Casks must be empty of prior contents before filling with whisky.
- Capacity cannot exceed 700 L and the resulting spirit must retain traditional colour, taste and aroma.
- The guidance confirms that casks traditionally used for Scotch whisky remain acceptable, while allowing some flexibility for other spirits if they meet the established character criteria.

The document supersedes earlier circulars and clarifies conditions for permissible previous uses of oak casks.
