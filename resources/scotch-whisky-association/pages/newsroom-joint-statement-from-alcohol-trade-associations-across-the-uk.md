# Joint Statement from Alcohol Trade Associations Across the UK

On **12 November 2025**, a coalition of alcohol trade bodies—including the British Beer and Pub Association, National Association of Cider Makers, Scotch Whisky Association, Society of Independent Brewers and Associates, UK Spirits Alliance, WineGB, and Wine and Spirits Trade Association—issued a joint statement urging the UK government not to raise excise duty. The statement highlights:

- **Economic pressure** on breweries, distilleries, vintners, cider makers, pubs, bars, restaurants, visitor experiences and retailers.
- A call for **investment and job creation** rather than increased taxation.
- Support for the Chancellor’s role in fostering confidence and growth within the sector.

The statement was delivered to the Exchequer Secretary and is part of ongoing advocacy on behalf of the alcohol industry.

---

## Key Points
- The coalition stresses that **higher duties would stifle investment** and lead to job losses.
- They argue that **revenue can be generated through growth**, not higher taxes.
- The statement aligns with broader industry efforts to secure fairer tax treatment for Scotch whisky and other alcoholic beverages.
