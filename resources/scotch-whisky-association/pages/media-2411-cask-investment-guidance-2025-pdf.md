# Cask Investment Guidance

The Scotch Whisky Association (SWA) publishes a PDF that cautions potential investors about buying individual casks of Scotch whisky. It explains the lack of a regulated market, the costs involved (bottling duty, storage, insurance), and legal requirements for ownership transfer and warehouse approval. The guide stresses due diligence, realistic pricing checks, and awareness of risks such as evaporation loss and strength reduction below 40 % ABV.

## Key Points
- No official price list or open market for casks.
- Investment is highly speculative; only a tiny fraction of whisky is traded this way.
- Costs: bottling duty (£32.79/litre + VAT), storage, insurance, possible movement fees.
- Legal safeguards: receipt, contract details, warehouse registration (HMRC WOWGR).
- Risks: evaporation (~2 %/yr), strength drop below 40 % ABV, potential loss of ownership if not properly recorded.

The document is a practical warning rather than an investment endorsement.
