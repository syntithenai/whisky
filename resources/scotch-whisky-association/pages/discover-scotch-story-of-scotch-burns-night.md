# Burns Night
Burns Night is an annual celebration on January 25th that honours Scotland’s poet Robert Burns and his love of whisky. Traditions include the Selkirk Grace, the Address to the Haggis, a toast to the lassies, and often a dram of Scotch whisky.

## Key points
- Burns (1759‑1796) was also an exciseman who inspected distilleries.
- He wrote *Scotch Drink*, celebrating whisky’s role in community.
- The first Burns Night supper was held five years after his death by friends.
- Modern celebrations feature toasts, ceilidhs and Scotch whisky.
