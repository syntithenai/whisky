# Joint statement with Distilled Spirit Council: Removal of tariffs

The Scotch Whisky Association (SWA) and the U.S. Distilled Spirits Council (DISCUS) announced the removal of retaliatory tariffs on American whiskey and Scotch whisky, a move that restores zero‑for‑zero trade between the two countries. The decision was made possible through diplomatic efforts by UK and US trade officials and is expected to revive bilateral trade flows that had fallen 35 % during the tariff period.

Key points:
- Tariffs on single‑malt Scotch whisky and American whiskeys were lifted in June 2022.
- Bilateral trade between 1997–2017 grew by 212 % when no tariffs existed; it fell from $1.51 billion to $982 million during the tariff period.
- The SWA welcomes further U.S.–UK trade agreements and a potential bilateral free‑trade pact.

The announcement underscores the importance of free trade for both industries and signals a renewed commitment to strengthening trans‑Atlantic commercial ties.
