## Chancellor Rachel Reeves announces an RPI inflation increase to alcohol duty

The Scotch Whisky Association (SWA) has criticised the UK government’s decision to raise alcohol duty again, arguing it will worsen job losses, stalled investment and business closures in the sector. The SWA had previously urged a freeze on duty to support growth. Revenue from spirits fell 7 % after last year’s 3.6 % increase, costing the Treasury £150 m. The Office for Budget Responsibility (OBR) has downgraded alcohol revenue forecasts by an average of £1.7 bn per year due to weak in‑year data.

Mark Kent, SWA Chief Executive, warned that a third duty hike in two years will limit economic growth and productivity while costing jobs. He called for the government to heed industry concerns and maintain duty levels to support distillers, bars, restaurants, farmers and suppliers.
