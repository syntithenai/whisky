# New Zealand Whisky: What You Need To Know

The article explains that New Zealand (Aotearoa) is on the brink of a craft whisky boom, with fewer than 20 active distilleries and many more in development. It traces the history from Scottish settlers in the 1800s to a decline caused by UK anti‑competitive practices, noting Willowbank as the last operating distillery until 1997. In 2021 New Zealand established a governing body that sets clear guidelines for whisky production.

The piece compares the current NZ scene with Australia’s earlier craft movement, highlighting how Australian distilleries have grown from a handful to over 400 and how early releases are now highly prized on the secondary market. It stresses that NZ whiskies are poised to join international awards soon.

A key example is **Pōkeno Whisky** – described as a clean spirit with tropical fruit, sweet malt, citrus, vanilla and butterscotch notes, matured in American oak casks at 46 % ABV without chill‑filtration or colour. The founder Matt Johns aims to put NZ on the global whisky map.
