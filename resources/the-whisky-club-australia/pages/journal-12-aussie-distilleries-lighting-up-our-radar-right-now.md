# 12 Aussie Distilleries Lighting Up Our Radar Right Now
The Whisky Club Australia’s 2019 journal article highlights twelve Australian distilleries—two from each state—that are gaining attention for their craft whisky production. The piece traces the history of whisky in Tasmania, noting the long ban that ended in 1991 with Lark Distillery, and then profiles each distillery’s founding story, production style, cask usage, and notable releases.

## Key Highlights
- **Tasmania**: Overeem (Casey Overeem) – single‑cask releases aged in French/American oak; Launceston (Chris Condon) – small‑batch whisky in French/US oak.
- **New South Wales**: Black Gate (Brian & Genise Hollingworth) – 3,000 L of whisky per year, quality over quantity; Craft Works (Craig “Crafty” — uses diverse cask types).
- **Victoria**: Bakery Hill – oldest mainland single‑malt, award‑winning.
- **South Australia**: Chief’s Son (McIntosh family) – peated & specialty malts at 45–60% ABV; Rochfort – premium single‑cask releases, $2,200 bottle.
- **Queensland**: Wild River Mountain – high‑altitude distillery, “Elevation” single malt.
- **Western Australia**: Limeburners (Cameron Syme) – award‑winning continuous‑still whisky.
- **South Australia (Fleurieu)**: Fleurieu – sea‑air influence, occasional peat.
- **Geographe**: Steve Ryan’s Bellwether – peated whisky at 46.8% and cask strength 70.4%.

The article underscores how climate, local ingredients, and dedicated distillers are enabling Australian whisky to compete globally.
