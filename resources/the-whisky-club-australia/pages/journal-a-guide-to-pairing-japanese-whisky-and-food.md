# A Guide to Pairing Japanese Whisky and Food

The article explains how to match Japanese whisky with food, emphasizing balance or contrast between flavours. It lists general pairing rules (light whiskies with seafood/cheese; heavier whiskies with protein and starchy foods; bold whiskies with smoky dishes). The author describes the typical flavour profile of Japanese whisky—light, fruity, floral, complex oak notes—and attributes this to seasonal maturation, use of Mizunara casks, bamboo filtration, high‑altitude distillation, and mineral‑rich mountain spring water. Finally, it offers specific pairing suggestions from Jota Tanaka of Fuji Gotemba Distillery (e.g., sashimi, root vegetables, tiramisu, apple tart) and invites readers to share their own ideas.

---

## Key Takeaways
- **Pairing principle**: whisky and food should either enhance or contrast each other’s flavours.
- **Japanese whisky profile**: light, fruity, floral, complex oak; influenced by climate, cask type (Mizunara), filtration method, altitude, and water source.
- **Food suggestions**: Asian dishes, ramen, grilled meats, sushi, creamy dishes, curries, seafood, delicate cheeses, chocolate.
- **Expert tip**: Jota Tanaka recommends Fuji Japanese Whisky with sashimi or root vegetables; also suggests sweet desserts for a sweeter whisky.
