# Australia’s Whisky History Could Be Yours

The article celebrates the **Hellyers Road 21 Year‑Old Single Cask #2157.05**, the oldest single‑cask Australian whisky officially bottled by a distillery. Hellyers Road, Tasmania’s oldest continuously operating distillery (licensed in 1997), produced this cask from 2002 and aged it in American oak for 21 years. The stills are the largest in Australia, giving the spirit a distinctive flavour profile with less reflux and longer cask contact.

Key points:
- **Hellyers Road**: oldest continuous Australian distillery; pioneers alongside Lark, Overeem, Sullivans Cove.
- **Cask #2157.05**: filled 6 June 2002, matured in American oak, released as a historic single‑cask bottling.
- **Price history**: originally $1,800 per bottle; now sells for $4,000–$5,000+ on secondary markets.

- Licensed in 1997; has operated single‑malt production since then.
- Uses the largest stills in Australia, resulting in lower reflux and a distinctive flavour profile.
