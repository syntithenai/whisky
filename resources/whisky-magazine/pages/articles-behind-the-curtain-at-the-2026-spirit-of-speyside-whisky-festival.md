# Behind the Curtain at the 2026 Spirit of Speyside Festival

The article describes the 27th annual Spirit of Speyside Whisky Festival, which runs from 29 April for six days and offers visitors rare access to 12 traditionally closed distilleries. Highlights include themed events such as a silent disco at The Glenlivet, a canoe trip on the River Spey, and a historic opening dinner at Craigellachie. The festival also showcases supporting crafts (cask building, malting) and runs a four‑day whisky school.

Key points:
- 12 distilleries open for the first time: Aultmore, Benrinnes, Craigellachie, Dailuaine, Dalmunach, Glendullan, Kininvie, Longmorn, Mortlach, Tamdhu, Tamnavulin, Tormore.
- Over 600 events in total; 60 compete for Best New Event.
- Notable experiences: hill‑tour pairing at Tormore Estate, picnic by Murray McDavid’s huts, silent disco in The Glenlivet loft, snap‑and‑sip tour at Dallas Dhu.
- Industry dinner and ceilidh at Craigellachie mark its 135th anniversary.
- Crafts such as cask building (Caledonian Cooper) and malting tours (Boortmalt) are featured.
- The festival promotes Speyside’s wider attractions, including the Keith & Dufftown Railway.

The piece ends with remarks on economic impact (£2.2 million boost) and local‑visitor balance.
