# The Magical History of the Great Brora Distillery

Ainslie & Co faced financial trouble and dissolved, leading to a series of ownership changes involving John Risk, The Distillers Company Limited (DCL), and later the Clynelish Distillery Company. Key dates include:
- **October 15, 1913** – Retirement of James and Thomas Ainslie.
- **December 18, 1913** – Registration of the Clynelish Distillery Company with a £20,000 capital (2,000 shares at £10 each).
- **July 1934** – First board listed: John Risk, William H. Ross, and Secretary John A. Seatter.
- **1919‑1921** – Expansion of capital to £30,000; acquisition of Coleburn distillery; liquidation of Ainslie, Baillie & Co Ltd; formation of Ainslie & Heilbron (Distillers) Ltd.

These events illustrate the consolidation and restructuring common in early 20th‑century Scottish whisky industry.
