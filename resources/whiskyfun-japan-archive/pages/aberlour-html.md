# Aberlour Single Malt Scotch Whisky
Aberlour is a Speyside distillery founded in 1826 (officially 1879). The name means “mouth of the babbling brook” and the local spring, Saint Drostan’s Well, appears on all bottles. It operates four stills (two wash, two spirit) and is known for its rich sherry‑cask finishes.

## Product Highlights
- **Aberlour 10 yo** – 40–43% ABV, often finished in European oak or oloroso sherry butts.
- **Aberlour A’bunadh** series – unpeated, high‑proof (≈60%) cask‑strength releases from various sherry and bourbon casks.
- **Aberlour 12 yo Double Cask Matured** – double‑cask finish with sherry or bourbon influence.
- **Aberlour 18 yo** – 43% ABV, traditionally finished in sherry butts.
- **Special editions**: “Triple Cask”, “Casg Annamh”, “A’bunadh Alba” and limited‑run high‑proof releases.

## Tasting Notes (selected)
- Caramel, vanilla, sweet wine, tea, sherry, malt, oak, apples, toffee, raisins, honey.
- Many bottles feature a pronounced sherry character with notes of dried fruit and spice.
