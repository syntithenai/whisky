# The Magical History of the Great Brora Distillery (to come)

- **Ownership change**: Sold to James Ainslie & Company, a Leith-based Scotch whisky blender.
- **Rebuilding**: Completed in 1897‑1898; expanded capacity for private customers and wholesalers.
- **Infrastructure updates**: Added new warehouses, incorporated an old stone bearing the coats of arms of the Marquess of Stafford and Countess of Sutherland with the date 1820.
- **Power sources**: Pelton water wheel supplemented by a horizontal steam engine (Shanks of Arbroath). Introduced screw and band conveyors for raw‑material handling and built a powerhouse for electric lighting.

> *Harper’s Weekly* praised Brora as “a singularly valuable property” with the highest price among single Scotch whiskies, noting its strong export demand.
