# The TTB Story

The Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau (TTB) traces its roots back to the early days of the United States Treasury, beginning with the first excise tax on distilled spirits in 1791. Created in January 2003 after a reorganization of the Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco and Firearms under the Homeland Security Act of 2002, TTB’s mission is to collect excise taxes on alcohol, tobacco, firearms, and ammunition; protect consumers through compliance programs; and assist industry members with federal tax, product, and marketing requirements. With over 500 employees nationwide—many analysts, chemists, investigators, and auditors—TTB modernizes services via e‑government initiatives such as an all‑electronic label processing system.

Key historical milestones:
- 1791: First excise tax on distilled spirits pays off Revolutionary War debt.
- 1794–1800s: Treasury’s role in the Whiskey Rebellion and Civil War financing.
- Early 20th century: Enforcement of Prohibition (Eighteenth Amendment) and actions against illegal liquor networks.

Today, TTB continues to evolve its mission while honoring a legacy that spans more than two centuries.
