# Reminder: FDA Revokes Authorizations for Use of Brominated Vegetable Oil and FD&C Red No. 3

The U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) has issued final rules that revoke the authorization to use **brominated vegetable oil (BVO)** in food—including alcoholic beverages—and **FD&C Red No. 3** in food and ingested drugs. The TTB newsletter explains the regulatory changes, compliance dates, and the need for new formula approvals if a product contains these ingredients.

- **BVO**: Effective August 2 2024; compliance deadline August 2 2025.
- **FD&C Red No. 3**: Effective January 15 2027 (food) and January 18 2028 (drugs).

Companies must reformulate, relabel, or deplete inventory of BVO‑containing products before the compliance dates and obtain new TTB formula approvals if needed.

---

For more details, see the FDA’s official pages on [Brominated Vegetable Oil](https://www.fda.gov/food/banned-substances-and-ingredients/brominated-vegetable-oil-bvo) and [FD&C Red No. 3](https://www.fda.gov/food/color-additives/fdc-red-no-3).
