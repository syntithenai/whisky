# 2023 Pre‑Budget Submission – Australian Spirits Industry

The Australian Distillers Association and Spirits & Cocktails Australia submitted a comprehensive report to the 2023‑24 Federal Budget. The document argues that the current spirits excise regime—high rates, twice‑annual CPI indexation and a $350 k remission cap—is stifling growth, investment and export potential. It recommends aligning the spirits excise rate with the brandy rate and freezing CPI indexation for three years to unlock sustainable manufacturing, job creation, regional development and international trade.

Key points:
- 400+ distilleries in Australia; $11.6 bn added value; 52,900 direct jobs (5,000 in manufacturing).
- Current excise is the third highest globally; indexation doubles tax pressure twice a year.
- Modest reform could increase government revenue while supporting industry growth.
- Case studies of Brown‑Forman and Granddad Jack’s illustrate how tax relief drives investment and export.

The report calls for targeted policy changes to create a “boom” similar to the wine sector of the 1980s.
