# Whisky’s Curious Medicinal History

The article traces whisky (aqua vitae) from medieval Scottish monasteries to modern perceptions of its health benefits. It explains how early distillation, rooted in Arabic alchemy and Galenic medicine, was used for medicinal cocktails and folk remedies. Key historical moments include:
- 1494 Lindores Abbey grant to Brother John Cor.
- The Beatons’ role as court physicians.
- King James IV’s patronage of alchemist John Damian.
- Post‑1707 whisky’s illicit trade and its use by farmers for everyday ailments.
The piece ends with a note on the diaspora spreading whisky traditions worldwide.

---

## Key Takeaways
- **Medieval medicine** relied on distillation as a panacea.
- **Monasteries** were early centers of whisky production.
- **Royal patronage** helped legitimize and expand distillation.
- **Illicit trade** kept whisky accessible to the poor after 1707.
- The belief in whisky’s medicinal value persists today (e.g., hot toddys).
