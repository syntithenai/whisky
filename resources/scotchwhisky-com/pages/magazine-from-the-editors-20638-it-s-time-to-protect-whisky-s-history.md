# It’s time to protect whisky’s history

Dave Broom argues that key historic sites—Kennetpans and Lindores Abbey—are disappearing, urging preservation of early distillation evidence.

- **Kennetpans**: once Scotland’s largest distillery, now sinking into the Firth of Forth; its ruins illustrate the boom‑and‑bust cycle of the industry.
- **Lindores Abbey**: archaeologists uncovered a potential medieval still pit that could push back whisky’s origins.
- Broom stresses the fragility of heritage sites and calls for urgent conservation to safeguard whisky’s legacy.

The article reflects on how easily industrial history can be lost, urging responsibility across generations.
