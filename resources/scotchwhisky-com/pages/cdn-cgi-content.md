# Tree Physiology: The Hidden World of Xylem and Phloem

This article explores the intricate vascular systems that sustain trees—**xylem**, which transports water and minerals, and **phloem**, which moves sugars and organic compounds. It explains how transpiration creates a negative pressure pulling water upward, how active loading powers sugar transport in phloem, and how these two systems are interdependent for tree growth and survival.

The piece highlights the symbiotic relationship between xylem and phloem, the role of ATP‑driven sucrose transporters, and the broader implications for plant biology and ecological understanding.
