# Whisky’s place in rock ’n’ roll history

Matt Evans explores how iconic musicians and bands have intertwined whisky with their music, from early blues‑rock songs to modern artist‑branded bottlings. The article highlights:
- Slane Castle’s transition from a rock venue to a distillery run by Alex Conyngham.
- Historical ties between whisky and artists such as Frank Sinatra, Mick Jagger, Lemmy Kilmister, and Michael Anthony.
- Modern collaborations: the Rolling Stones’ limited‑edition Suntory blend, Metallica’s Blackened whiskey, and other artist‑owned brands.
- The cultural impact of whisky on music scenes, especially in Ireland during the Troubles.

The piece underscores that while marketing drives many partnerships, genuine affinity between musicians and whisky often stems from shared values and authenticity.
