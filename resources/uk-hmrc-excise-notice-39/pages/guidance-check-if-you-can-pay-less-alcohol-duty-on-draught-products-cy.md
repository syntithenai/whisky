# Lower Alcohol Duty on Draught Products (Welsh)

This GOV.UK guidance explains when and how businesses can pay a reduced alcohol duty rate on draught alcoholic beverages in the UK. Key points:
- **Eligibility**: Products with less than 8.5 % ABV, such as certain beers, ciders, ready‑to‑drink wines, and fruit‑based spirits.
- **Conditions for lower rate**:
  - Must be produced or imported by a licensed manufacturer or importer.
  - Must be stored in compliant containers (e.g., tap systems with sealed bottles).
  - The container must hold at least 20 L of product.
- **Re‑packaging rules**: Re‑packaging for takeaway sales is allowed, but transferring to non‑draught containers or selling on the premises requires full duty unless specific exemptions apply.
- **Duty rates (2026)**:
  - 3.5–8.5 % ABV beer/cider: £8.95 per litre of pure alcohol.
  - 3.5–5.5 % ABV wine: £8.95.
  - >5.5 % ABV wine: £19.45.
  - 3.5–8.5 % ABV spirits/ready‑to‑drink: £19.45.
- **Reporting**: Businesses must declare the lower rate on customs returns and provide evidence of compliance.
