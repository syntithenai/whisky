# Alcoholic Products Technical Guide – Section 1 Introduction

This section of the UK HMRC guidance explains the legal framework for producing, storing and accounting for duty on alcoholic products. It replaces several older Excise Notices (e.g., Notice 39 for spirits) and outlines key responsibilities for producers, importers and exporters.

- **Scope**: Covers all alcoholic products, including those with protected geographical indications such as Scotch whisky, Irish whiskey, and Somerset cider brandy.
- **Legal basis**: References the Finance (No.2) Act 2023, Alcoholic Products (Excise Duty) Regulations 2023, Customs and Excise Management Act 1979, and related regulations on records, drawback, and movement of goods.
- **Online services**: From 1 Feb 2025 producers can apply for an Alcoholic Products Producer Approval (APPA) online; from 1 Mar 2025 duty returns and payments will also be handled online.
- **Compliance**: Producers must secure approvals, maintain physical security, keep accurate records, submit timely returns, and register under the Alcohol Wholesaler Registration Scheme if selling to other businesses.
- **Enforcement**: Non‑compliance can lead to assessments, civil penalties (see Notice 209), seizure of goods or equipment, and potential appeals.
