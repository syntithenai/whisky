# Become an Appointee for Someone Claiming Benefits

This GOV.UK guide explains how a person can act on behalf of someone who cannot manage their own benefit claims due to mental incapacity or severe disability. It covers:
- Who may become an appointee (individuals, organisations, representatives)
- Responsibilities and duties of an appointee
- How to apply for the role (contacting the relevant benefit helpline, completing Form BF56/ BF57)
- Conditions under which an appointment can be terminated
- Special considerations for Scottish or Northern Irish benefits.

The page also links to related support services such as needs assessments, financial help and fraud prevention.
