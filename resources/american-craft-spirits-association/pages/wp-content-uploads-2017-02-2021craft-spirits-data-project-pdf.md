# Final2021Craft Spirits Data Project

The American Craft Spirits Association (ACSA) released a PDF briefing summarizing the state of U.S. craft spirits in 2020‑2021. Key points include:
- **Industry growth**: 2,290 active craft distillers as of Aug 2021 (+1.1% YoY). Retail volume exceeded 12 million 9‑liter cases (≈$6.7 billion) with a 7.3 % volume CAGR and 9.8 % value CAGR.
- **Market share**: 4.7 % of U.S. spirits by volume, 7.1 % by value in 2020 – up from 2.2 %/3 % in 2015.
- **Concentration**: <2 % of producers account for >56 % of cases; 90.1 % are classified as small and produce only 10.3 % of cases.
- **Geography**: 54 % of sales occur outside the distiller’s home state, with large producers driving most out‑of‑state business.
- **Employment & investment**: Full‑time staff fell from ~30k in 2019 to <17k in 2020; capital invested rose to $759 m (≈$61 m increase).
- **Definitions & methodology**: ACSA’s craft definition limits producers to ≤750,000 proof gallons removed from bond and requires self‑proclamation as craft.

The briefing also outlines ACSA’s governance, partnership with Park Street, and the broader research methodology used to compile these figures.
