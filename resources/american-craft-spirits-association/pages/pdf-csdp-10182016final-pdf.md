# American Craft Spirits Association – 2016 Craft Spirits Economic Briefing

The PDF is a PowerPoint presentation summarizing the inaugural **Craft Spirits Data Project** launched by the American Craft Spirits Association (ACSA) in partnership with IWSR and Park Street. It outlines:
- The definition of *U.S. craft spirits* used for research: licensed producers removing ≤750,000 proof gallons from bond, self‑proclaimed as craft, not openly controlled by a large supplier, and compliant with the ACSA Code of Ethics.
- Market statistics (2015): 1,315 active craft distillers; $2.4 bn retail sales; 4.9 m cases sold; CAGR 27–28% (volume/value) from 2010‑15; 2.2 % volume share and 3.0 % value share.
- Concentration: top 2 % of producers account for >60 % of sales; 91.7 % are small (<10,000 proof gallons).
- Sales channels: direct‑to‑consumer (DSP) vital for small distillers (≈25 % of sales); out‑of‑state business dominates large producers (>61 %).
- Employment and investment: >12,000 FTEs in 2016; $300 m invested in 2015.
- Stakeholder concerns and research methodology are detailed, including survey participation (~1,000 distillers/wholesalers/retailers, 5,000 consumers).

The presentation also reviews various craft‑spirit classification attempts (ACSA, ADI, state DSP licenses) and discusses size, ownership, production steps, and ethics as key differentiators.
