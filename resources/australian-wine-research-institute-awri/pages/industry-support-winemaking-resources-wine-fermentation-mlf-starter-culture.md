# Preparation of a Malolactic Fermentation (MLF) Starter Culture Using Freeze‑Dried Bacteria

The Australian Wine Research Institute (AWRI) provides a step‑by‑step protocol for activating and acclimatising freeze‑dried *Oenococcus oeni* cultures to overcome problematic MLF in wine. The method is recommended when direct inoculation fails or when key wine parameters (temperature, pH, alcohol, SO₂, yeast compatibility) are unfavourable.

**Key steps**
1. **Prepare activation medium** – 5 L grape juice + 5 L water + nutrient mix; adjust to pH 4.0.
2. **Rehydrate bacteria** – dissolve in lukewarm water, let sit 30 min, then mix with activation medium at 18–22 °C. Monitor malic acid until ~50 % consumed (48–72 h).
3. **Acclimatise to wine** – add 10 L of problem wine to the culture; keep temperature 18–22 °C and monitor malic acid, stopping when it drops to ~1 g/L.
4. **Inoculate bulk wine** – gently stir the acclimatised culture into 1,000 L of wine.

The protocol emphasises maintaining L‑malic acid above 1 g/L throughout and adjusting wine conditions before inoculation to maximise success.
