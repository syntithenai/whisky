# Ask‑the‑AWRI: Top Tips for Yeast

This PDF from the Australian Wine Research Institute (AWRI) provides practical guidance on yeast selection, inoculation rates, cell viability, hydration, and bulk culture management for winemakers. Key points include:
- Yeasts differ in nitrogen demand, sulfur compounds, glycerol production, alcohol tolerance, pH tolerance, and fermentation speed.
- Inoculum rates typically range from 20–40 g hL⁻¹ (≈250 mg L⁻¹). Higher rates (300–500 mg L⁻¹) are advised for high‑sugar or clarified musts.
- A minimum of 5×10⁶ viable cells mL⁻¹ is required; healthy fermentations aim for ~1×10⁸ cells mL⁻¹ midway.
- Hydration should use chlorine‑free water, avoid clumping, and allow 10–15 min for yeast to rehydrate.
- Bulk cultures should be used within five days to preserve viability and purity.

For more details, visit AWRI’s website or contact their winemaking services team.
