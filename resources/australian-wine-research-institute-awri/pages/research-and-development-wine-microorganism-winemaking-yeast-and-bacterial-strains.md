# Available microbial strains – AWRI

The Australian Wine Research Institute (AWRI) hosts a comprehensive culture collection of yeast and bacterial strains for winemaking, cider, beer, kombucha, mead and vinegar production. The page lists:

* **Commercialised strains** – e.g., *Oenococcus oeni* (YV Select), several *Saccharomyces cerevisiae* products from AB Biotek, and hybrid strains such as *S. paradoxus × cerevisiae*.
* **Experimental & heritage strains** – hybrids with low‑H₂S or high‑ester output, non‑*Saccharomyces* species (e.g., *Lachancea thermotolerans*, *Torulaspora delbrueckii*), and flor/aperà yeasts like AWRI 81.
* **Specialised strains** – for sherry/flor winemaking, vinegar production (acetic acid bacteria), and teaching/research use.

The collection is accessed through the Wine Microorganism Culture Collection (AWMCC) with terms & conditions, propagation protocols, and contact details provided.

---

## Key facts
- AWRI offers both commercialised and experimental yeast/bacteria strains for a wide range of fermented beverages.
- Commercial products are supplied by manufacturers such as Lallemand, AB Biotek, Anchor Oenology, Novello, and others.
- Experimental strains include hybrids with desirable fermentation traits (low H₂S, high fruity esters, low‑temperature tolerance).
- Flor/aperà yeasts (e.g., AWRI 81) are historically significant for fortified wine production in Australia.
- The collection also supports vinegar production via acetic acid bacteria and provides reference strains for quality assurance.
