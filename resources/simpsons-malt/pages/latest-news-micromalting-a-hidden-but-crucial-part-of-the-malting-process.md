# Micromalting: A Hidden but Crucial Part of the Malting Process

Simpsons Malt explains micromalting as small‑scale, controlled malting trials that inform barley variety selection and commercial malt production. The process involves steeping, germination and kilning on kilogram‑sized samples to predict how a variety will behave at scale. It is used by the company’s Seed Director Laura Beaty and Quality Manager Cole Mather to guide decisions for growers, seed breeders and distillery customers.

Key points:
- Micromalting provides data before commercial malting, helping choose varieties and adjust steeping schedules.
- The practice supports the MAGB Malting Barley Committee’s variety approval process.
- Results are shared with farmers, seed breeders and end‑users to build confidence in new barley varieties.
- At the malting sites, micromalting informs batch‑specific adjustments, reducing risk compared to large‑scale decisions.

The article highlights how small‑scale trials reveal unexpected behaviours (e.g., nitrogen levels affecting steeping time) and emphasises that no two batches are identical, requiring adaptive management.
