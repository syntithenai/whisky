# American Single Malt Gains Official Status

The U.S. Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau (TTB) has finalized a standard of identity for **American single malt whiskey** effective **January 19, 2025**. The definition requires:
- Production entirely in the United States.
- 100 % malted barley mash.
- Distillation at ≤160 proof (≤80 % ABV).
- Aging in new or used oak barrels no larger than 700 L.
- No neutral spirits, flavorings, or other blending materials (caramel color allowed with disclosure).

Whiskeys aged two years or more may carry the “straight” label. The rule follows a decade of advocacy by the American Single Malt Whiskey Commission (ASMWC) and aligns closely with its provisional proposal from 2022.

Some distillers objected to specific clauses—such as requiring all steps at one distillery, mandating 100 % malted barley mash, or allowing only U.S. oak barrels—but these were ultimately omitted to accommodate diverse production practices.

> *“Having this formal definition is going to protect the integrity of American Single Malt whiskey and drive experimentation, creativity and innovation in this popular category.”* – Chris Swonger, DISCUS CEO
