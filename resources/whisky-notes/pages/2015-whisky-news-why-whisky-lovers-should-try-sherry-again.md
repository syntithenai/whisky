# Why Whisky Lovers Should Try Sherry (Again)

Ruben, a long‑time whisky enthusiast, argues that sherry’s diverse styles and rich flavour profile make it an ideal companion for whisky drinkers. He explains how sherry casks have historically mellowed whiskies and added complexity, and notes the current scarcity and high demand for sherry barrels.

**Key points**
- Sherry comes in at least eight distinct styles (Manzanilla, Fino, Amontillado, Oloroso, Pedro Ximénez, etc.).
- Dry sherries such as Oloroso and Amontillado share aromas of dried fruit, chocolate, nuts, spices, and oak that are familiar to whisky drinkers.
- Sweet styles (sweet Oloroso, Pedro Ximénez) offer intense caramel, fig, date notes but may be too rich for some.
- Sherry‑finished whiskies often exhibit a smoother mouthfeel and deeper flavour complexity.
- The price gap between high‑end sherries and premium single malts is significant; a 30‑year‑old whisky can cost €300–€400, while an equivalent aged sherry may be under one‑fifth of that price.
- Ruben invites readers to explore sherry through a free Twitter tasting during International Sherry Week (Nov 2‑8).

---

**Takeaway** – By sampling various dry sherries, whisky lovers can better anticipate the flavours imparted by sherry cask maturation and enjoy more affordable, flavourful alternatives.
