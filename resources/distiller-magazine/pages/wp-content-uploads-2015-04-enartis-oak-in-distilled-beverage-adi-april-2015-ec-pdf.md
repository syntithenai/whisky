# Oak Aroma Design for Distilled Beverages

This PDF is a PowerPoint presentation from Enartis Vinquiry (technical winemaker Eglantine Chauffour) presented at ADI, Louisville, April 2015. It outlines how to design and control oak aroma in spirits and wines using barrel alternatives such as chips, staves, and tannins, and discusses the chemistry of oak components, oxygen diffusion, and micro‑oxygenation.

## Key Points
- **Oak composition**: hemicellulose 20‑25 %, lignin 25‑30 %, cellulose 40‑45 % with extractable compounds (tannins, aromas, polyphenols).
- **Primary aroma compounds**: whisky lactone, vanillin, eugenol, syringaldehyde, furfural, etc., each linked to sensory descriptors.
- **Barrel alternatives**: chips, mini‑staves, tannin extracts; advantages include consistency, faster maturation, and cost control.
- **Oxygen dynamics**: 20‑50 mg L⁻¹ yr⁻¹ enters barrels mainly through the bung (21 %) and between staves (63 %).
- **Micro‑oxygenation**: a controlled method to simulate barrel conditions in any container, adaptable to fermentation or ageing.
