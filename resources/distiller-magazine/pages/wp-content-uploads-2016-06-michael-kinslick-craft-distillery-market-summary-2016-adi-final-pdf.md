# Michael‑Kinstlick Craft Distillery Market Summary (2016)

This PDF provides a statistical snapshot of the U.S. craft distillery sector for 2015 and projections for 2016. Key points include:
- **Market size**: 1,016 unique U.S. listings in ADI directories (2006‑2016) and 817 new listings in 2016.
- **Active producers**: 962 operating distilleries at year‑end 2015; 772 identified as active.
- **Growth trend**: The number of entrants doubles every three years, with an expected 1,000 producing craft distilleries by 2016.
- **Exit rates**: Declining from 21.3 % (2005‑07) to 0.4 % (2014‑15).
- **Product mix**: Distilleries produce a range of spirits; the report lists product types and exit percentages for each.
- **Market share**: Craft distillers hold about 1.5 % of the U.S. distilled spirits market, while craft brewers hold ~15 % of the beer market.
- **Definition**: A craft distillery is an independent firm producing <40‑50k L per year, bottling and marketing its own spirits.

The author, Michael Kinstlick, co‑founded Coppersea Distilling and provides background on his industry experience.
