# Taxes – What, Where & How Much?

A concise overview of the tax landscape for distilled spirits in the United States and Kentucky, including federal excise rates, state/local taxes, indirect taxes, and a breakdown of the 2012 tax burden. The document also details Kentucky’s specific excise, wholesale sales, retail sales, and property taxes on alcohol, as well as contact information for Erica L. Horn.

---

## Key Points
- Federal excise: $13.50 per proof‑gallon (≈$2.14 per 750 ml bottle at 80 proof).
- Kentucky excise: $1.92/gal (min $0.12/half‑pint), plus a $0.05 case tax.
- Wholesale sales tax: 11% on distilled spirits; wine & beer rates taper to 10% by 2018.
- Retail sales tax: 6% on retail receipts, with a former exemption until 2009.
- 2012 tax burden: 20 % state/local, 15 % federal, 20 % indirect, 46 % private share; total $7.83 per bottle.
