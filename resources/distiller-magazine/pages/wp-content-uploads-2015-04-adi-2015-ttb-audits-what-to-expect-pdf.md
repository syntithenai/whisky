# Applying for a TTB Distillery Permit
This PDF from the American Distilling Institute (ADI) outlines the Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau’s (TTB) audit process for distilleries. It explains the mission of the Tax Audit Division (TAD), the three‑phase audit cycle—Planning, Fieldwork, Reporting—and details risk‑based scheduling, required documentation, common findings, record‑keeping tips, and preparatory steps for industry members.

## Key Points
- TAD uses a risk‑based approach to schedule audits.
- Audits consist of Planning (engagement letter, information request), Fieldwork (interviews, control assessment, transaction testing), and Reporting (management letter, tax adjustments).
- Common findings include missing documentation for removals, incomplete inventories, and late operational reports.
- Distilleries must maintain separate records per DSP account and keep supporting documents for at least three years.
- TTB provides resources such as the CFR, online tools, and newsletters to aid compliance.
