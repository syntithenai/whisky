# Geographical Indications and Quality Schemes Explained

The European Union protects product names that are linked to a specific place or traditional production method through several quality schemes:
- **PDO** (Protected Designation of Origin) – strongest link, all stages must occur in the region.
- **PGI** (Protected Geographical Indication) – at least one stage tied to the region.
- **GI** – for spirit drinks; at least one production stage in the region.
- **TSG** (Traditional Speciality Guaranteed) – protects traditional methods, not location.
- **Mountain product**, **Outer‑most regions** and other voluntary schemes also exist.

A new regulation (Regulation (EU) 2024/1143) entered force on 13 May 2024, simplifying registration, strengthening online protection, recognising sustainable practices, and creating a voluntary system for producer groups. The EU’s GI database is eAmbrosia; the GIview portal lists both EU and non‑EU GIs under agreements.

---

**Key takeaways:**
- GIs are intellectual property rights that help consumers trust quality products linked to geography.
- The new regulation merges rules across food, wine, and spirits into one simplified procedure.
- Sustainable practices can now be part of GI specifications.
- Producer groups may manage their own GIs under the new framework.
