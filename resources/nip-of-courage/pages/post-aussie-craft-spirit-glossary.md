# Aussie Craft Spirit Glossary
A concise guide to the terminology used in Australian craft distilling, covering everything from ABV and proof to botanicals, barrel ageing, and production techniques.

- **ABV vs Proof** – How alcohol is measured in Australia/UK vs the US.
- **Angel’s Share** – Evaporation loss during cask ageing.
- **Barrel Ageing & Cask Strength** – Minimum two‑year on‑wood rule for whisky and rum.
- **Base Alcohol & Neutral Spirit** – Starting alcohol used to make gin, vodka, whisky, etc.
- **Botanical Basket & Vapour Infusion** – Methods of extracting flavour from botanicals.
- **Cuts (Heads, Hearts, Tails)** – Distillation fractions and why they’re separated.
- **Fermentation, Mash, Pot/Column Still** – Core steps in spirit production.
- **Navy Strength & Bottling Strength** – Minimum ABV for certain spirits.
- **Paddock‑to‑Bottle & Single Malt** – Production models unique to Australian craft distillers.
