# Australian Brandy Alexander

A classic dessert cocktail made with Aussie brandy, dark and white crème de cacao, and single cream. The article highlights the resurgence of this drink using high‑quality craft brandies from Australia.

## How to Make It
- **30 ml 23rd Street Prime 5 Brandy**
- **20 ml dark crème de cacao**
- **10 ml white crème de cacao** (or all dark/white)
- **40 ml single cream**

Shake with ice, strain into a chilled coupe glass and garnish with nutmeg, chocolate or orange peel.

## Featured Brandies
| Distillery | Brandy | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 23rd Street | Prime 5 (700 ml, 40%) | Soft dried fruit, vanilla, spices from oak |
| 23rd Street | Not Your Nanna’s (700 ml, 40%) | Younger, ex‑Chardonnay cask, buttery vanilla |
| Ironhouse | Strange Omen (700 ml, 48%) | Tasmanian wine base, American oak, buttery vanilla, ginger, caramel |
| Bass & Flinders | Ochre Fine (700 ml, 41%) | Pink lady apples, orange peel, cherry blossom, caramel, vanilla, dried fruit, cinnamon, clove |

## Cocktail Alternatives
- Burnt Fuselage: Aussie brandy + Grand Marnier + dry vermouth.
- Brandy Boulevardier: Aussie brandy + rosso vermouth + Campari.
- Sidecar, Between the Sheets, Zombies, sangria – all work well with brandy.

## Origin Speculation
The name may derive from Russian Tsar Alexander II, a 1922 royal wedding, or New York’s Rector’s bar where Troy Alexander worked. The drink is essentially a gin‑based Alexander adapted for brandy.
