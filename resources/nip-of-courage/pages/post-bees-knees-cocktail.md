# Bee’s Knees Cocktail

A classic Prohibition‑era gin cocktail that balances the herbal notes of gin, the tartness of lemon juice and the sweet umami of honey syrup. The recipe calls for **60 ml Stone Pine Original Dry Gin**, **20 ml honey syrup** (equal parts honey and water), and **15 ml fresh lemon juice** shaken with ice and strained into a cocktail glass, garnished with a lemon twist.

The drink originated in 1920s France, credited to Austrian barman Frank Meier at the Hotel Ritz Paris. It spread to America during Prohibition, where it became popular in speakeasies. The name “Bee’s Knees” comes from 1920s slang coined by flappers and popularised by cartoonist Tad Dorgan.

A variant called the Gold Rush replaces gin with whisky.

> *“The Bee’s Knees also came the Gold Rush cocktail, which replaces gin with whisky.”*

---

**Key ingredients**: gin, honey syrup, lemon juice. **Recommended gin**: Stone Pine Original Dry Gin (700 ml 40%).
