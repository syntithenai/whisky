# Diageo to Close Clynelish Visitors Centre
Diageo announced the closure of the visitors centre at Clynelish Distillery after a recent review, despite having invested £185 million in renovations just three years earlier. The decision does not affect production; whisky will continue to be made at Clynelish and other Diageo sites.

## Key Points
- Closure follows a cost‑cutting strategy under CEO Sir Dave Lewis and CFO Nic Jhangiani.
- Other visitors centres may also see changes, but details are pending.
- The move is part of broader restructuring: Teaninich has been idled, Roseisle Maltings paused, and several U.S. distilleries halted production until mid‑2026.
- Diageo will close its Crown Royal bottling facility in Amherstburg next month, sparking political backlash.

## Context
Clynelish is one of the “Four Corners” of Johnnie Walker. The £185 million upgrade linked it with Cardhu, Glenkinchie, Caol Ila and the Johnnie Walker Experience in Edinburgh.
