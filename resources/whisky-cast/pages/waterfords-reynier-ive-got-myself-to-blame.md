# Waterford’s Reynier: "I’ve Got My Own Blame"

Mark Reynier, founder of the Irish distillery Waterford Distillery, reflects on the company’s recent receivership and his plans to regain control. He attributes the collapse to a combination of rising interest rates, weak whisky sales, and broader economic pressures. Reynier is now seeking investors to buy back the distillery and its maturing stock before bids close in January.

---

## Key Points
- Waterford secured €45 million credit from HSBC (Jan 2023) but could not repay when rates rose to 5.25%.
- The bank appointed receivers; the distillery is up for sale or asset liquidation.
- Reynier aims to raise funds to purchase the distillery and its whisky inventory.
- He cites market conditions, post‑COVID destocking, cost‑of‑living crisis, and interest rates as contributing factors.

---

## Takeaway
Reynier remains committed to Waterford’s terroir‑focused vision and is actively pursuing a buy‑back strategy amid challenging financial circumstances.
