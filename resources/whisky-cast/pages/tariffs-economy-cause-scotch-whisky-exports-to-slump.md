# Tariffs, Economy Cause Scotch Whisky Exports to Slump

The article reports a decline in Scotch whisky exports for 2025, driven by U.S. import tariffs and a weaker American economy. Global volume fell 4.3 % and sales value dropped 0.6 %. The U.S., the largest export market, saw a 9.2 % drop in volume and a 15 % fall from May‑December after tariffs were imposed. Mark Kent, chief executive of the Scotch Whisky Association (SWA), highlighted the impact on barrel trade and pledged to lobby for tariff removal.

Other markets also suffered: Asia‑Pacific exports fell 8.3 % in value; the EU lost 1.8 % in value but remained the top regional market.

> *“Of course, the U.S. is our biggest market…when our volumes drop off, so do our purchases of barrels from the U.S., and that’s actually happened.”* – Mark Kent
