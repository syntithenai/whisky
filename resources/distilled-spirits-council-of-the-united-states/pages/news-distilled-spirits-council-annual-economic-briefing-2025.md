# Distilled Spirits Council Annual Economic Briefing 2025

The 2025 briefing highlighted that U.S. spirits maintained a 42.4 % market share despite a 2.2 % decline in total sales, driven by the rapid growth of ready‑to‑drink (RTD) cocktails which grew 16.4 % to nearly \$4 billion. Vodka, tequila/mezcal and American whiskey remained top categories but all saw modest declines. Trade tensions—particularly tariff threats and Canadian shelf removals—continued to strain distillers, while the council secured several policy wins such as zero‑tariff protection for spirits and expanded RTD access in multiple states.

Key points:
- Spirits sales: \$36.4 billion (‑2.2 %)
- Volumes: 318.1 million 9‑liter cases (+1.9 %)
- RTDs: \$3.8 billion, +16.4 % YoY
- Market share: 42.4 %
- Trade issues: tariff threats, Canadian shelf removal, export decline (‑9 %)
- Policy wins: zero‑tariff protection, expanded RTD licensing, tax threat defeats.

The briefing included supporting PowerPoint slides and data tables.
