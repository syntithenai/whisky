# PA‑Increased‑Spirits‑Outlets Testimony (10‑29‑19)

David Wojnar, Vice President of State Government Relations for the Distilled Spirits Council of the U.S., testifies before the Senate Law and Justice Committee in support of bills SB 548, SB 880 and SB 916 that would expand the number of distilled‑spirits outlets in Pennsylvania.  The testimony argues that:

- Pennsylvania is **under‑retail** for spirits compared to neighboring states and other control states.
- Expanding retail outlets (home distributors, expanded restaurant licenses or franchise stores) would increase consumer convenience, boost sales, and generate significant new tax revenue for the Commonwealth.
- No state liquor stores would close under any proposal.
- The PLCB must maintain control of the wholesale system and provide pricing transparency.

The testimony also notes that Act 39’s expansion of wine outlets has not cannibalized PLCB store sales, suggesting a similar outcome for spirits.

---

**Key Points:**
1. **Consumer Convenience** – 3,000 potential stores vs. current ~600.
2. **Fiscal Impact** – 24 % tax on new sales plus PLCB markup.
3. **Wholesale & Pricing Transparency** – PLCB to publish pricing system.
4. **State Store Viability** – No loss of sales with expanded outlets.
