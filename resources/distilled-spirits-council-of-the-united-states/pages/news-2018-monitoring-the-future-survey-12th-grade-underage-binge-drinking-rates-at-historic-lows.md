# 2018 Monitoring the Future Survey

- **Key finding**: Binge‑drinking among 12th graders fell to its lowest level since the survey began in 1975.
- **Statistics**: 13.8 % reported binge drinking (five or more drinks) in the past two weeks, down from 16.6 % in 2017 and 31.5 % in 1998.
- **Industry role**: The Distilled Spirits Council credits its Responsibility.org programs for helping parents discuss alcohol with teens.
- **Data source**: Joint study by NIDA and the University of Michigan’s Institute for Social Research, covering ~44,482 students from 392 schools.

## Source Excerpts
- According to the 2018 Monitoring the Future Survey, jointly released by the National Institute on Drug Abuse (NIDA) and the University of Michigan, the proportion of 12 th grade students reporting binge drinking at least once in the two weeks prior to the survey has declined 56 percent since its peak year in 1998.
- “This is excellent news and our country is making important progress in reducing underage drinking, but our work is not done,” said Chris Swonger President and CEO of the Distilled Spirits Council and Responsibility.org.
