# American Spirits Exports Report (2025)

- U.S. spirits exports fell **3.8 %** to **$2.37 billion** in 2025, mainly due to trade friction and economic headwinds.
- The sharp decline was driven by:
  - A **70 %+ drop** in Canadian exports after the removal of U.S. spirits from provincial shelves (March‑Dec 2025).
  - A **19 % fall** in American whiskey exports, largely from a **35 % drop** to the EU following front‑loading shipments amid tariff fears.
- Outside Canada and the EU, exports grew **2.5 %**; other markets such as Brazil, UK, Australia, and emerging economies saw gains (up 13.2 %).
- Top export categories: American whiskey ($1.08 bn), liqueurs & cordials ($511 m), vodka ($282 m), rum ($90 m), gin ($48 m), brandy ($35 m).
- Leading markets: EU ($1.2 bn), UK ($153 m), Australia ($138 m), Mexico ($127 m), Canada ($89 m) – together 72 % of total exports.
- Exporting states (2025): Kentucky, Tennessee, Florida, Texas, Indiana.
- DISCUS priorities for 2026+: restore tariff‑free trade with EU/UK, negotiate new agreements with Argentina, Malaysia, Indonesia, Ecuador, and open markets in India, Brazil, Vietnam, South Africa, etc.

> *“The decline of U.S. spirits exports in 2025 underscores the industry’s vulnerability to uncertainty in the global trade environment and the vital importance of restoring permanent zero‑tariff access.”* – Chris Swonger, DISCUS President & CEO.

- Spirits Exports Decline 3.8% in 2025 Amid Ongoing Trade Friction Removal from Canadian Shelves, Drop in American Whiskey Exports to EU Contributing Factors WASHINGTON — U.S.
- spirits exports declined 3.8% in 2025 to $2.37 billion, reflecting the impact of ongoing trade friction, retaliatory actions and broader economic headwinds, according to the 2025 American Spirits Exports Report released today by the Distilled Spirits Council of the United States (DISCUS).
- Exports to Canada dropped sharply following the removal of American-made spirits from provincial store shelves beginning in March 2025, while exports of American whiskeys to the European Union declined after some producers front-loaded shipments in late 2024 amid the threat of retaliatory tariffs.
- Despite these setbacks, over the past 25 years, exports have grown fivefold, from $478 million in 2000 to $2.37 billion in 2025, driven by decades of tariff-free access to key markets and rising global demand for premium American spirits.
