# AI and Social Media Age‑Restrictions on ABAC’s Agenda in Annual Report

The **Alcohol Beverages Advertising Code (ABAC)** released its 2025 Annual Report, highlighting continued progress in self‑regulation of alcohol marketing in Australia. Key points include:
- A **21 % YoY increase** in signatory companies, now **665**, covering **88 %** of the industry’s media spend.
- The Adjudication Panel issued **103 determinations** with an average turnaround of **19.5 business days**; **52 %** led to ads or packaging being removed from market.
- Pre‑vetting remained highly effective, reviewing **2,766** communications and rejecting/modifying **282**, preventing any breaches.
- Focus on **age‑restriction controls on social media** and addressing alcohol advertising in children’s podcasts through collaboration with the Interactive Advertising Bureau Audio Council.
- New guidance released for **water‑activity marketing** and responsible use of **generative AI** in advertising.
- Partnerships to strengthen placement policies, banning ads near schools, and recruiting a panelist with youth market expertise.

The report underscores ABAC’s proactive, risk‑based approach and its role in supporting public health outcomes.
