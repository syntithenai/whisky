# Global Beverage Alcohol Contracts Decline 2% in 2025

- **Overall trend**: Total beverage alcohol volumes across 22 key global markets fell by 2 % in 2025, driven by high inflation, political polarisation and international conflict.
- **Major market impacts**:
  - United States: –5 % volume, –4 % value.
  - China: –2 % volume, –12 % value (national spirits excluded: –1 % volume, flat value).
  - Emerging markets with growth: South Africa and India (+4 % volume, +5 % value in India).
- **Category performance**:
  - Ready‑to‑drink (RTD): +2 % volume, +4 % value.
  - Spirits: –4 % volume, –9 % value (driven by national spirits).
  - Irish whiskey: +2 % volume/value.
  - Wine: –4 % volume, –2 % value; Prosecco grew +2 % volume, +4 % value.
  - Alcoholic beer: –2 % volume, flat value.
  - Non‑alcoholic beer: +8 % volume, +12 % value.
- **Drivers**: Rising cost of living, health and wellness focus, premiumisation trend offset by price increases.
- **Data context**: Preliminary IWSR data covering ~70 % of the global market; full 2025 data to be released Q2 2026.
