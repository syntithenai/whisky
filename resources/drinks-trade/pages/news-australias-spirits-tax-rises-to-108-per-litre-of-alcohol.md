**Australia’s Spirits Tax Rises to $108 per litre of alcohol**

- On 4 Feb 2026, the excise on spirits increased to **$107.99 per litre of alcohol**, a rise of $2.01.
- This is the **78th inflation‑linked increase** since automatic indexation began in 1983.
- The tax hike occurs while the government’s two‑year freeze on draught beer remains unchanged, creating a pricing bias between spirits and other liquor categories.
- Industry voices (Spirits & Cocktails Australia, Night Time Industries Association) argue that the higher tax burdens consumers and small venues, especially for cocktails and mixed drinks.
- Calls are being made to extend the beer freeze to tap spirits served in pubs and clubs.
