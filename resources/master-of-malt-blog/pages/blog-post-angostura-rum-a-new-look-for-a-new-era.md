# Angostura Rum: A New Look for a New Era

Master of Malt’s feature on the House of Angostura highlights a brand‑wide rebrand that goes beyond packaging to showcase Trinidad‑based rum production, award‑winning blends and a renewed focus on sustainability. The article details:
- **Brand history** – from Johann Gottlieb Benjamin Siegert’s bitters in 1824 to the current Trinidad distillery.
- **Rebranding elements** – butterfly motif, lighter bottles, responsibly sourced paper, reduced secondary packaging.
- **Production process** – blackstrap molasses fermentation (up to 72 h), dual stills (single‑column for heavy styles, five‑column for light), cask ageing in bourbon American oak with tropical climate acceleration and a flexible blending system.
- **Product range** – from the affordable 3‑year‑old white rum to the premium 15‑year‑old Special Reserve, each with tasting notes and price points.
- **Tasting impressions** – consistent house style of cooked apple, dried fruit, coffee fudge, baking spice; emphasis on Trinidad identity and practical pricing.

The piece ends with a call to action for readers to explore the new rum lineup and reflects on the brand’s role in elevating Trinidadian rum globally.
