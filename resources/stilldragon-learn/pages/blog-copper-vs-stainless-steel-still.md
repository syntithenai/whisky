# Copper vs. Stainless Steel Still

StillDragon explains why copper has historically been the preferred material for distillation vessels: it is easy to work with, antimicrobial, and can sequester sulfides that cause off‑odors in fermented liquids. Stainless steel, introduced in the 1800s, offers corrosion resistance and ease of cleaning but lacks the sulfur‑sequestration benefit. The company recommends a hybrid approach—using copper where vapor contacts the surface while employing stainless steel for structural components—to balance performance and cost.

## Key Takeaways
- **Copper**: removes sulfides, improves flavor, historically dominant.
- **Stainless Steel**: resistant to corrosion, easy to sanitize, widely used in modern distilleries.
- **Hybrid Systems**: combine copper vapor contact with stainless steel structure for optimal results.
