# Distillation Theory: Where to Pack Your Vodka Still Column

The article explains how the placement of packing material in a vodka still column affects heat distribution and alcohol enrichment. It argues that placing packed sections above the plate assemblies creates a stronger temperature gradient, reduces unneeded heat at the top, and improves ABV. The author discusses theoretical vs actual plates, liquid bed depth, and the role of pressure in packing performance.

## Key Points
- **Packing placement**: Above plates is preferable for optimal heat management.
- **Heat source**: Kettle generates most heat; it should be shifted into the column.
- **Liquid beds**: Deeper beds on plates absorb more heat than droplets in packing.
- **Temperature gradient**: Stronger gradients lead to higher ABV and faster operation.
- **Practical advice**: Minimize return of previously distilled alcohol to the boiler.

The post is aimed at small distillery operators looking for cost‑effective design choices.
