# Can You Over‑Distill Vodka?

StillDragon’s blog post discusses whether vodka can be over‑distilled and how the U.S. TTB definition of vodka has changed. The article explains that traditional vodka is defined as a neutral spirit with no distinctive character, but since May 2020 the definition allows for some flavor. It argues that while excessive distillation removes all character, a moderate amount of distillation can produce a clean yet slightly expressive vodka that appeals to consumers.

The post also touches on market considerations: if customers prefer pure mixers, more distillation is unnecessary; if they want subtle base‑ingredient notes, less distillation may be better. Finally, StillDragon offers consulting and equipment for distillers.

## Key Takeaways
- **TTB definition (pre‑May 2020):** vodka must be neutral with no distinctive character.
- **Revised definition (post‑May 2020):** vodka may have some flavor.
- **Over‑distillation:** removes all character; not desirable for most consumers.
- **Moderate distillation:** can yield a clean, silky mouthfeel while revealing subtle base notes.
- **Market focus:** choose distillation level based on consumer preference for purity vs. nuance.
