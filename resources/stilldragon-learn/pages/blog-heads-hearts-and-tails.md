# Heads, Hearts and Tails

- **Heads**: Early distillate rich in low‑boiling alcohols, aldehydes and ethyl acetate.
- **Hearts**: The desirable middle portion of the run.
- **Tails**: Late distillate high in fusel oils with little alcohol.

The article discusses how new distillers often focus on precise cuts to achieve a *smooth* spirit, but this can strip complexity. It encourages experimenting by adding small amounts from the heads or tails into the hearts to enhance flavor while maintaining balance. The author stresses that cut points are subjective and should evolve with experience and sensory feedback.

> “Cutting clean” is good for beginners, but seasoned distillers may benefit from a slightly looser approach to preserve nuance.

The post ends with a reminder to keep notes, seek expert advice, and enjoy the learning process.
