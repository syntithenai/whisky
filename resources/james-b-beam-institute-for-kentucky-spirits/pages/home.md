# James B. Beam Institute for Kentucky Spirits
The James B. Beam Institute, part of the University of Kentucky’s Martin‑Gatton College of Agriculture, Food and Environment, is a research and development hub for the American whiskey industry. Founded in 2019 with a $5 million gift from Suntory Global Spirits, its mission is to advance workforce education, scientific discovery, environmental sustainability, and community responsibility within the bourbon sector.

Key offerings include:
- **Workforce Training Standards Project** – developing industry‑aligned training for Kentucky distillers.
- **Research & Outreach** – multidisciplinary studies covering production science, supply‑chain efficiency, and sustainability practices.
- **Annual Conference & Events** – e.g., the Distillery Safety Series on EHS compliance.

The institute also publishes economic impact data (e.g., 23 k jobs, $9 B in aging barrels) and hosts newsletters and conference registrations.
