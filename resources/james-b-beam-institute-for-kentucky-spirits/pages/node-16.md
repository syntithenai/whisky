# Faculty Fellows – James B. Beam Institute

The page lists faculty members from the University of Kentucky who are Fellows of the James B. Beam Institute for Kentucky Spirits. Each fellow’s name, title, department and a brief description of their research interests or projects that intersect with bourbon, distilling, or related fields is provided.

- **Seth DeBolt** – Director of the Institute.
- **Anastasia Curwood** – History professor exploring Black Kentuckians’ role in distilling.
- **Janice Fernheimer Zantker** – Jewish Studies professor working on a graphic‑novel project about bourbon history.
- **Alan Fryar** – Hydrogeologist studying limestone water’s influence on bourbon.
- **Michael Matthew McGlue** – Earth sciences professor developing a course on geology of spirits.
- **Arnold Stromberg (in memoriam)** – Statistician who helped distillers with data analysis.
- **Edward W. Woolery** – Chair encouraging courses on water resources for distillation.
- **Junfeng Zhu** – Geologist focusing on water quality for distilleries.
- **Jessica Barfield** – Information science professor researching AI and robotics in distilling.
- **Anthony Limperos** – Communication professor interested in bourbon media.
- **Brian Real** – Library‑science professor linking archival work with distillery history.
- **SK O’Brien** – Product design professor exploring packaging materials from distillery waste.
- **Carolyn Oldham** – Education professor collaborating on safety training for distilleries.
- **David “Travis” Thomas** – Nutritionist and home brewer interested in bourbon science.
- **Luke H. Bradley Chellgren** – Neuroscience professor studying smell and taste perception.
- **Dan Han** – Neurologist with interest in neurogastronomy.
- **Michele Staton** – Behavioral scientist focusing on responsible drinking.
- **Joseph Benitez** – Health‑economics professor examining workforce insurance.
- **Stephen Lusch** – Accounting professor studying excise taxes and bourbon economics.
- **Brad Patrick** – Management professor supporting business operations in spirits.
- **Simon Sheather** – Data‑analytics dean with industry experience.
- **Ken Troske** – Economist analyzing price dynamics of bourbon and scotch.
- **Rajeev Darolia** – Public policy professor studying spirits industry impacts.
- **Akinbode A. Adedeji** – Biosystems engineer converting distillery spent grain to value‑added products.
- **Tyler Barzee** – Bioprocess engineer working on renewable energy from distillery by‑products.
- **Donald Colliver** – Energy‑systems researcher in distilled spirits.
- **Mark Coyne** – Soil microbiology professor studying stillage residues.
- **Czarena Crofcheck** – Bioprocessing researcher focusing on ethanol projects.
- **John H. Grove** – Agronomy professor researching grain crops for bourbon.
- **Vanessa P. Jackson** – Retail & tourism professor building industry partnerships.
- **Chad D. Lee** – Grain research director improving bourbon‑grade corn and wheat.

The page serves as a directory of interdisciplinary scholars whose work supports the Institute’s mission to advance Kentucky spirits through research, education, and collaboration.
