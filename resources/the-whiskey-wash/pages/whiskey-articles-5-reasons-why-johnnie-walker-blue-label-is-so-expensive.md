## Source Excerpts
- Long aging means more complexity, but also more evaporation, or what’s known as the “angel’s share.” By the time these casks are used, much of their volume has vanished, making what’s left both scarce and expensive.
- Priced around $160 or more, it costs far more than other Johnnie Walker expressions like Red or Black Label , and even more than many aged single malts.
- It’s Made from Rare and Aged Whiskies Only one in 10,000 casks is considered good enough for Blue Label.
- Many of the component whiskies are aged for decades, some for 28 years or more, with rumors of 40–60 year-old casks in the mix.
- A number come from “ghost” distilleries like Port Dundas or Brora, long since closed and running low on stock.
- Johnnie Walker ’s blending team selects each whisky by hand from over 10 million maturing casks.
