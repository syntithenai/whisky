# Dewar’s Aberfeldy Distillery: 25‑Year Visitor Milestone

Dewar’s Aberfeldy Distillery celebrated 25 years of welcoming guests in July 2025, tracing its evolution from a simple tour venue opened in April 2000 to an immersive whisky destination. The article chronicles key milestones—early interactive spaces, partnership with the Edinburgh Military Tattoo, expansion of blending and maturation areas, café and bar redesigns, introduction of an app‑guided experience, and special commemorative releases (175th anniversary blend, 125‑year Aberfeldy Single Malt, 25‑year‑old single malt). The narrative emphasizes community, heritage, and continuous improvement.

---

## Key Points
- **Opening**: April 17 2000 – first visitor centre, stills polished, limited edition 25‑yr single malt released.
- **Early attractions**: nosing & tasting room, cinema screens, interactive museum.
- **Visitor growth**: 100 000th guest by 2004; partnership with Edinburgh Military Tattoo (2002‑2007).
- **Expansion**: 2012 filling store/warehouse upgrade, blending/maturation showcase, “fill your own bottle” station.
- **Café & bar redesign**: 2014 Whisky Lounge, Tunnel of Staves, local food partnerships.
- **Digital shift**: 2016 interactive app replacing audio guides; Secret Bar added.
- **Commemorations**: 2021 175th‑anniversary blend; 2023 Aberfeldy Single Malt 125th‑year release and 25‑yr single malt; 2024 immersive warehouse experience.
- **Future outlook**: continued evolution driven by visitor stories and heritage storytelling.

## Source Excerpts
- Since the day we opened our doors back in April 2000, our home in the heart of the Scottish Highlands has been more than just a place where whisky is crafted; it’s been a place where whisky lovers from around the world meet our welcoming team, connect with our heritage, our craft, and, most importantly, each other.
- Where It All Began April 17th, 2000 — the day the stills were polished, the film reels were ready, and a limited edition 25-year-old single malt was uncorked for an audience of over 100 whisky industry VIPs, and Dewar’s World of Whisky, as it was called then, was officially open.
- It was Scotland at its best: music, pageantry, whisky and visitors from across the globe soaking up the magic under the Highland sky.
- Visitors could now explore the crucial final steps of our craft: blending and maturation.
- Out came the builders and in came Whisky Lounge and bar, along with the Tunnel of Staves, a beautiful passageway of oak that still guides guests from our museum to the café.
- When Aberfeldy Single Malt turned 125 in 2023, we marked the occasion the only way we know how: by sharing it.
