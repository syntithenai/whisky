# 5 Things You Didn’t Know About Officer’s Choice
Officer’s Choice is a dominant Indian whisky brand that outsold global giants like Johnnie Walker in volume, yet remains largely unknown outside India. The article traces its rise from a family feud and legal battles to clever marketing that positioned the drink as the choice of honest, everyday officers. Key points include:
- 2014 sales hit **28.4 million cases**, making it the world’s best‑selling whisky by volume.
- Launched in 1988 by Kishore Chhabria after splitting from his brother’s company; a protracted legal dispute with Vijay Mallya ended in 2012 when Kishore paid ₹20 crore for ownership rights.
- The brand’s name and imagery evoke the respected image of police/army officers, resonating with government employees and working‑class consumers.
- A trademark clash with John Distilleries’ *Original Choice* reached India’s Supreme Court; the court upheld the competitor’s name in 2011.
- Officer’s Choice pioneered socially conscious advertising, notably a domestic‑violence campaign that leveraged surrogate ads to comply with strict Indian alcohol‑marketing laws.

These facts illustrate how a brand built on legal resilience and relatable positioning became a cultural institution in India while remaining obscure internationally.
