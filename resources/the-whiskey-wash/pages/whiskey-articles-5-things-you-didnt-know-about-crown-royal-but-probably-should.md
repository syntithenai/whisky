# 5 Things You Didn’t Know About Crown Royal

- **Royal origins** – Created for King George VI’s Canadian tour in 1939, the whisky was first sold only in Canada.
- **Hidden home** – Since 1968 it has been distilled in Gimli, Manitoba, a small lakeside town with a massive 360‑acre plant.
- **Blend of over 50 whiskies** – Crown Royal is not a single spirit; each grain (corn, rye, barley) is distilled separately and blended, often using Coffey‑still rye and various cask types.
- **Iconic purple bag** – Introduced in 1939, the velvet pouch has become a cultural symbol and is still used for the flagship expression.
- **Award‑winning rye** – The 2015 Northern Harvest Rye became the first Canadian whisky to win *World Whisky of the Year* in Jim Murray’s book.

These facts reveal why Crown Royal is more than a mass‑market label: it has royal heritage, prairie roots, a complex blending philosophy and critical acclaim.

## Source Excerpts
- Crown Royal uses both base whiskies and bolder flavoring whiskies, aged in a mix of barrel types including charred oak, ex- bourbon , and even Cognac casks.
- Bronfman reportedly tested over 600 blends before landing on the final recipe, presented in a crown-shaped bottle and wrapped in a regal purple bag.
- It spans 360 acres, runs 24/7, and fills up to 1,000 barrels a day.
- The extreme temperature swings help the whisky mature faster by forcing the spirit deeper into the oak.
- This gives the master blender precise control over flavor.
- Distilled in a rare column still imported from Scotland, it adds a creamy texture to the final product.
