**5 Whiskies Like Johnnie Walker Black Label – Why You’ll Like Them**

Phil Dwyer explains how to dissect the appeal of Johnnie Walker Black Label and recommends five whiskies that capture different aspects of its flavor profile:

1. **SMWS Fife Peaty Potion** – smoky‑sweet blend finished in Pedro Ximénez & Oloroso sherry casks.
2. **Bunnahabhain 12 Year Old** – unpeated Islay with sherried richness, sea‑salt and chocolate notes.
3. **Caol Ila Distillers Edition** – peat‑rich finish in Moscatel wine casks adding soft sweetness and salinity.
4. **Kilkerran 12 Year Old (Glengyle)** – Campbeltown style with light peat, maritime salt and a slightly oily character.
5. **Benromach 10 Year Old** – Speyside peat with vanilla‑caramel from first‑fill sherry & bourbon casks.

The article encourages readers to identify which element of Black Label they enjoy most—smoke, sweetness, or breadth—and then choose a whisky that excels in that area.

## Source Excerpts
- Distilled in Fife from a mix of peated and unpeated malt, matured in bourbon casks and then finished for 18 months in first-fill Pedro Ximénez and Oloroso sherry casks.
- First-fill sherry and bourbon casks give it vanilla, raisin, caramel, and biscuit.
- That extra time in sherry does something interesting — the smoke and sweetness genuinely seem to belong together rather than fighting for space.
- This version, finished in Moscatel wine casks, gives you a cleaner look at what it brings on its own.
- It has that same “covers all the bases” quality as Black Label, but the individual flavours feel a bit more distinct.
