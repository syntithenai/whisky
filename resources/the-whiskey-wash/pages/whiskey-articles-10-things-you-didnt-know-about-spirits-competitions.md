# 10 Things You Didn’t Know About Spirits Competitions

- **Author**: Christopher J. Davies, photojournalist and co‑founder of Wine Country Network.
- **Focus**: Inside the logistics, judging protocols, and common pitfalls of international spirits competitions such as the Denver International Spirits Competition.
- **Key points**:
  1. Competitions handle >120 categories; planning is weeks long.
  2. Vodka judging is tough because it’s almost tasteless.
  3. Mixologists aren’t always ideal judges due to different palates.
  4. Judges taste each spirit multiple times, using double‑blind procedures and second bottles for consistency checks.
  5. Mis‑categorisation can cost points.
  6. Accurate ABV is critical; mislabeling affects scoring.
  7. High‑proof entries (up to 62 % ABV) fatigue judges; sessions are monitored.
  8. Judges spit but still absorb alcohol; transport logistics matter.
  9. Glassware matters: tulip‑shaped Neat™ glasses improve nuance detection.
 10. Scores vary yearly due to batch differences, weather, and packaging.
