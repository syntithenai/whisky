# 7 Things You Didn’t Know About Small Batch Whiskey

Will Woodington, National Whiskey Brand Ambassador for Grain & Barrel Spirits (owner of Chicken Cock Whiskey), explains why the *small batch* category is unique:

1. **No regulatory rules** – unlike bourbon or rye, small‑batch whiskey isn’t governed by grain recipes, aging limits, or barrel counts.
2. **Emerged in the 1990s** – first marketed by Booker Noe of Jim Beam with *Booker’s Bourbon*.
3. **The “small” has grown** – early batches used a handful of barrels; now many brands blend hundreds to meet demand.
4. **Barrels are fingerprint‑unique** – each barrel develops distinct flavor, color and quality due to micro‑climate differences in the rickhouse.
5. **Batches mirror barrel uniqueness** – Chicken Cock’s Small Batch Bourbon blends fewer than 20 barrels (often <10) from different rickhouse floors, producing a 50% ABV spirit with subtle variations.
6. **High consumer demand** – artisanal, limited‑run products appeal to modern consumers seeking hand‑crafted quality.
7. **Blending is an art** – master blenders sample dozens of barrels, test combinations, and refine until the final blend meets the distillery’s standards.

The article highlights Chicken Cock Whiskey’s 160+ year history and its commitment to returning to true small‑batch roots.

---

## Key Takeaways
- Small batch whiskey is defined by *producer discretion*, not regulation.
- The category has evolved from a niche boutique label to mainstream mass‑produced blends.
- Barrel selection and blending are critical for flavor depth and uniqueness.
- Consumer preference leans toward limited, artisanal releases.

---

## Practical Implications
- Distilleries can market small batch as a premium, artisanal product without regulatory constraints.
- Blending expertise is a differentiator; training and documentation of barrel profiles are essential.
- Limited‑run batches create scarcity that drives demand and allows for price premiums.

## Source Excerpts
- The original intention with this new category was to create a blend of bourbon utilizing a small, hand-selected variety of barrels in the inventory that showcased the most distinct quality and flavor.
- While a variety of barrels from a producer can contain the same exact whiskey, made from the exact same grains, barreled at the exact same proof, and placed in the exact same place within the aging warehouse, they will each develop their own unique flavor, color, and quality characteristics.
- This is driven throughout the maturation process, where each barrel is subjected to varying temperatures and surface area interaction with the whiskey inside, making for distinct characteristics in every single barrel of whiskey.
- While these differences in flavor and quality can range from negligible to discerning, no two barrels are alike.
- These variances are what continue to propel the popularity of the Chicken Cock Single Barrel program and are the reason why true small batch whiskey blends can create an abundance of depth, character, flavor, and quality over more mass-blended products.
- Some batches may be higher on the citrus and oak side, while others may exhibit dessert-forward and sweeter flavors.
