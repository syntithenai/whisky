# A Master Taster’s Guide to Tasting Kentucky Bourbon

Peggy Noe Stevens explains how the flavor of bourbon comes from grains, yeast and barrel aging, and offers a practical tasting framework that links aroma, palate intensity and food pairings. The guide includes:
- **Flavor sources**: corn (sweetness), rye (spice), malted barley (cereal notes), wheat (soft bread‑like character); yeast contributes nutty‑fruity nuances; barrels add saltiness, wood, cherry and vanilla.
- **Tasting steps**: sniff first to identify dominant sweet, spicy, earthy, salty or fruity notes; taste next, noting any differences between nose and palate; use the intensity table to gauge how strong each note is.
- **Pairing strategy**: balance, counter‑balance or “explode” flavors with food. Examples cover sauces, cheeses, meats, seafood, fruit and sweets, with guidance on matching light vs heavy bourbons.
- **Practical tips**: use a simple intensity chart to become fluent in flavor recognition; consider the overall weight of both bourbon and dish when pairing.

The article also profiles Peggy Noe Stevens—founder of the Women’s Bourbon Association, first master bourbon taster, and author of *What Fork Do I Use With My Bourbon?*.
