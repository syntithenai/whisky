# Bourbon & Boos: Inside Kentucky’s Most Haunted Distilleries
A light‑hearted feature from the Kentucky Distillers’ Association that lists three of the state’s most famous bourbon producers—James B. Beam, Wild Turkey, and Old Forester—and recounts alleged ghost sightings, paranormal activity, and local folklore tied to each site. The article frames these stories as part of a Halloween‑themed tour of the Bourbon Trail, inviting readers to “sit back, take a sip and read on…if you dare.”

- **James B. Beam (Clermont)** – reported sightings of a former guard, a woman in windows, and activity in warehouses D and K.
- **Wild Turkey (Lawrenceburg)** – associated with the Ripy family ghosts, including voices, moving objects, and a still‑captured figure on SyFy’s *Ghost Hunters*.
- **Old Forester (Louisville)** – located on historic Whiskey Row; linked to Civil War deaths, ghostly apparitions, and nearby haunted venues such as Doc Crow’s Smokehouse and the Seelbach Hotel.

The piece concludes with a call for readers to share their own haunted bourbon stories and offers a ghost‑tour booking line for the Seelbach Hotel.
