# FAQ – Kentucky Distillers' Association

- **Bourbon origin**: Must be produced in the U.S.; “Kentucky Bourbon” requires grain cooking, fermentation, distillation *and* aging in Kentucky for at least one year.
- **Key differences from other whiskies**: New charred oak barrels, ≥51 % corn mashbill, ≤160 proof distillation, entry proof ≤125, bottled ≥80 proof, no added color or flavoring.
- **Secondary maturation**: Finishing in a second barrel (new or used for wine, sherry, etc.).
- **Legal status**: Protected by the 1964 Congressional Resolution; gluten‑free by definition of distillation.
- **Consumer guidance**: Mix cocktails, dilute with water/ice, learn tasting skills, consider proof, age, rarity when buying.
- **Industry facts**: 70 companies, 86 distilleries, ~10 million barrels aging (9.3 m Bourbon). Reciprocal states for shipping listed.
- **Membership & support**: KDA is a 501(c)(6) trade association; membership application available; Lifting Spirits Foundation and Safe Ride KY are related charities.

## Source Excerpts
- Also known as a finishing, double matured or wood-finished, the process of secondary maturation involves adding an additional aging process into the orginal barrel – or – dumping a barrel of whiskey into a second barrel or cask.
- The second barrel may be new or may have been previously used to age another product such us wine, sherry, port, rum, etc.
- All flavoring and coloring comes from the grains, yeast, and barrel.
- B – Bourbon must be aged in a new, charred oak container.
- This container is almost always a barrel (unlike other whiskeys which often employ used barrels).
- This preserves flavors and character from the cooking and fermentation process.
