# Bourbon's Five Sources of Flavor

The Kentucky Distillers' Association article by Fred Minnick explains the five core elements that shape bourbon flavor:
1. **Grains** – at least 51 % corn, usually 70‑80 %, blended with wheat, rye or barley.
2. **Water** – typically limestone‑filtered water that removes iron and sulfur.
3. **Fermentation** – mash cooled, yeast added; distilleries use proprietary strains to influence flavor.
4. **Distillation** – mash heated in a still, vapors condensed; bourbon must leave the still at ≤160 proof.
5. **Maturation** – new charred oak barrels (often #4 char) impart vanilla, caramel, spice, smoke, etc.; aging loss is called the “angel’s share.”

The piece also highlights notable wheat‑based bourbons and Four Roses’ use of multiple yeast strains to create distinct recipes.

---

## Key Takeaways
- Corn provides sweetness; rye adds spice; wheat can add extra sweetness.
- Limestone filtration is common in Kentucky but not exclusive.
- Proprietary yeast strains are a major flavor lever.
- New charred oak barrels (char #4) deliver the classic bourbon profile.
- Aging loss varies by barrel porosity, influencing final taste.
