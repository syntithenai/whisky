# Option 3 Plan: Legal NSW Small Distillery Setup (Compliance-First)

Important: This plan is for a legal, licensed commercial setup in NSW. It is not a home-distilling guide.

## Scope and Goal
- Build a compliant, small whisky distillery operation in NSW.
- Prioritize natural production pathways (grain, fermentation, oak maturation).
- Treat flavor additives as secondary and clearly optional.

## Plan Overview (High-Level)
1. Form business entity and site strategy.
2. Secure site and development approvals.
3. Obtain federal excise approvals and NSW liquor approvals.
4. Install production, safety, storage, and QA systems.
5. Commission operations, then run pilot and first production cycles.
6. Enter market through wholesale and/or approved on-premise channels.

## Legal Checklist (NSW + Federal)
- Confirm business structure and responsible persons (fit-and-proper readiness).
- Choose compliant premises and validate zoning/use with local council.
- Lodge development application (or modification) for distillery use and fit-out as required.
- Apply for ATO excise manufacturer licence (alcohol).
- Confirm excise controls: records, stock movement, strength/volume measurement, returns.
- Prepare for possible ATO financial security requirement.
- Apply for NSW Producer/Wholesaler liquor licence.
- If serving at venue, apply for relevant drink-on-premises or micro/small distillery authorisation.
- Complete mandatory RSA and licensee/manager training where required.
- Implement site safety controls: fire systems, ventilation, spill response, SOPs, signage.
- Set up annual fee calendar and compliance calendar (NSW annual fee due dates and penalties).
- Put insurance and product recall process in place before sales.

## Natural-First Production Path
Primary pathway:
- Grain to fermented wash to distilled spirit to oak maturation.
- Natural flavor development from grain selection, fermentation character, barrel oak type/toast/char, maturation time, and blending.

Secondary shortcut pathway (optional, not primary):
- Spirit flavoring products/essences can be used for R&D bench trials only.
- Keep these separate from flagship natural products and label transparently under applicable law.
